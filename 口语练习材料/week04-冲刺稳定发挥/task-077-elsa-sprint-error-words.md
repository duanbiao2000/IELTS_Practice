# Task 77: ELSA Sprint – Clear Your Error List

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why an "error list sprint"?**

Over the past three weeks, you've accumulated a list of problematic words. These are your **personal pronunciation weak points**. This sprint session:
- Systematically clears your accumulated error backlog
- Ensures no pronunciation issue remains unaddressed
- Provides measurable progress (error count before → after)
- Builds confidence through deliberate improvement

### The Compound Effect of Pronunciation Errors

| Issue | Impact on Score | Cumulative Effect |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 1 word mispronounced | Minor | Examiner notices |
| 10 words mispronounced | Noticeable | Pattern forms |
| 20+ words mispronounced | Significant | Affects overall impression |

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Common Error Patterns (for grouping)

| Sound Type | Example Words | Common Error |
|------------|---------------|--------------|
| /θ/ (think) | thought, through, anything | → /s/ (sink, sore, anysing) |
| /ð/ (this) | weather, together, although | → /z/ (zeather, togezzer) |
| -ed endings | watched, studied, decided | Drop the /t/, /d/, /ɪd/ |
| /r/ vs /l/ | really, colleague, collect | Sound confusion |
| Schwa /ə/ | about, problem, camera | Over-pronouncing as /ɑ/ |

### 🔊 Mouth Position Quick Reference

**For the Most Common Problem Sounds:**

| Sound | Mouth Position | How to Produce | Self-Check |
|-------|---------------|---------------|-----------|
| **/θ/** (think) | Tongue BETWEEN teeth | - Bite tongue gently<br>- Blow air through gaps<br>- Voice OFF | - Use mirror<br>- Feel air on lips |
| **/ð/** (this) | Tongue BETWEEN teeth | - Same position as /θ/<br>- Voice ON (vibrate) | - Touch throat<br>- Feel vibration |
| **/æ/** (man) | Mouth WIDE open | - Open like at dentist<br>- Tongue LOW, flat | - 2-finger width<br>- Lower jaw |
| **/v/** (very) | Top teeth on bottom lip | - Light bite lip<br>- Vibrate voice | - Feel lip vibration<br>- Check teeth contact |
| **/r/** (red) | Tongue curled BACK | - Pull tongue back<br>- DON'T touch roof | - Tongue pulled back<br>- No roof contact |
| **/l/** (love) | Tongue BEHIND teeth | - Tap tongue tip<br>- Just behind front teeth | - Tongue tip up<br>- Light tap only |
| **/ŋ/** (sing) | Back of tongue touches soft palate | - Back of tongue UP<br>- Touch soft back roof | - Mouth feels "full"<br>- Air flows through NOSE |
| **/ʃ/** (ship) | Round lips, push air out | - Puck lips slightly<br>- Push air continuously | - "Sh" sound<br>- Continuous air stream |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why 80%?**
> 80% in ELSA typically corresponds to "clear enough for IELTS Band 6+" for that sound. Perfection (95%+) is not necessary and is often diminishing returns.

**2. The Value of Your Error List**
Your accumulated error list is **personal data**. Generic pronunciation advice doesn't target YOUR specific issues. This list is worth more than a textbook because it's customized to you.

**3. Sentence Context Matters**
Pronunciation can change in sentences due to:
- **Connected speech**: "five eggs" → /faɪv ɛgz/ (not /faɪv ɛgs/)
- **Stress patterns**: "REcord" (noun) vs "reCORD" (verb)
- **Linking**: "pick it up" → /pɪk ɪt ʌp/

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Rushing through the list** | Quality over quantity. 5 words mastered > 20 words "sort of" practiced |
| **Practicing in isolation only** | ALWAYS include at least one sentence practice per word |
| **Not recording baseline** | You need "before" scores to see progress |
| **Giving up on stubborn words** | Some words take 10+ attempts. That's normal. |
| **Moving on too soon** | 80% is the MINIMUM. Aim for 85%+ on critical words |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Group similar sounds** – it's more efficient
2. **Practice in the morning** – your mouth muscles are fresher
3. **Review tomorrow** – retention check
4. **Save your final scores** – you'll compare with Task 94

### 🔧 Self-Correction Techniques

**How to Monitor and Fix Your Pronunciation in Real-Time:**

**1. The Pre-Speak Check (2 seconds)**
Before saying a word with your problem sound:
- **/θ/ or /ð/**: Briefly touch tongue to teeth
- **/v/**: Lightly bite bottom lip
- **/r/**: Pull tongue tip back
- **/æ/**: Drop jaw open wide
- **/l/**: Tap tongue behind teeth

**2. The "Feel It" Method**
- Use your mirror to see mouth position
- Feel physical sensations (air flow, vibration)
- Connect the feeling to the sound

**3. Minimal Pair Practice**
For each problem sound, practice contrasting pairs:
- **/θ/**: sink/think, sank/thank, sing/sink
- **/æ/**: men/man, pen/pan, bet/bat
- **/v/**: very/wary, vein/wane, vent/went
- **/r/ vs /l/**: read/lead, rice/lice, long/wrong

**4. Sentence-Level Self-Correction**
- Identify words with your problem sounds BEFORE speaking
- Plan your mouth position 1 word early
- Practice: "I **think** (touch teeth) that... works better than I **sink** that..."

**5. Recording Analysis Loop**
- Record yourself speaking naturally
- Listen specifically for your target sounds
- Count: How many times did you miss /θ/ this time?
- Re-record focusing on physical correction

**6. The "Anchor Word" Technique**
Choose one easy word for each problem sound as your "anchor":
- /θ/: "think" → your reference
- /æ/: "cat" → your reference
- /v/: "very" → your reference
- Before speaking a hard word, say your anchor word to "reset" your mouth
