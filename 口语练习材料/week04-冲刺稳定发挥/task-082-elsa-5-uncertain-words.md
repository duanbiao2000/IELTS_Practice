# Task 82: ELSA – Conquer Your 5 Most Uncertain Words

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why target your "most uncertain" words?**

These are your pronunciation blind spots – the words you're never quite sure about:
- **You hesitate** before saying them
- **You avoid them** or substitute easier words
- **You've guessed wrong** in the past

By researching the IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet) and practicing systematically:
- You replace uncertainty with confidence
- You expand your active vocabulary
- You eliminate "pronunciation anxiety"

### The "Uncertain Word" Profile

| Certain | Uncertain |
|---------|-----------|
| You know how to say it | You're not sure |
| You use it freely | You avoid it |
| You've practiced it | You've been guessing |

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Uncertain Word Analysis

**WORD:** "Colleague"

**IPA:** /ˈkɒliːg/

**Breakdown:**
- /k/ = like "cat"
- /ɒ/ = like "hot" (British) or "lot" (American)
- /l/ = like "love"
- /iː/ = like "see"
- /g/ = like "go"

**Stress:** FIRST syllable: **COL**league

**Common Mistake:** Pronouncing like "col-league" (three syllables) instead of "COL-leeg" (two syllables)

**Memory Trick:** "COL" (like "college") + "leeg" (rhymes with "league")

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why IPA Matters**
> English spelling is unreliable. "Read" and "read" look identical but sound different. IPA never lies – it tells you exactly how to pronounce a word.

**2. The Confidence Factor**
When you're uncertain about a word, you either:
- Avoid it (limits your vocabulary)
- Say it wrong (affects your score)
- Hesitate (affects fluency)

Mastering your 5 uncertain words eliminates all three problems.

**3. From Passive to Active Vocabulary**
These 5 words are probably in your passive vocabulary (you understand them). This task moves them to active vocabulary (you use them confidently).

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Guessing the IPA** | Don't guess – use a reliable dictionary (Oxford, Cambridge, Merriam-Webster) |
| **Practicing only in isolation** | Always include sentence practice |
| **Moving on too soon** | 80% is minimum. Aim for 85%+ on these high-uncertainty words |
| **Forgetting by tomorrow** | Review these 5 words every day for a week |
| **Choosing words you never use** | Select words you actually NEED for IELTS or daily life |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Use Forvo.com** – hear real people pronounce words
2. **Record yourself** – compare with dictionary audio
3. **Create a "5 Words" list in your phone** – review daily
4. **Use the words today** – force yourself to use them in conversation
