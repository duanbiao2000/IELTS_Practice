## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why "No Re-Dos" Matters**
> In the real exam, you cannot restart. Training yourself to recover from mistakes is MORE important than avoiding them. The ability to bounce back from a stumble shows true fluency.

**2. Posture and Performance**
Your physical position affects your voice:
- **Sitting straight** = fuller breath support = clearer speech
- **Slouching** = compressed lungs = weaker voice
- **Facing forward** = psychological readiness

**3. The Stamina Factor**
Most IELTS takers are fine in Part 1. The real test is maintaining quality in Part 3 when you're mentally tired. This mock reveals your stamina level.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Accepting "just one more chance"** | NO. The rule is NO re-dos. If you break it, you're not simulating the real exam |
| **Stopping timer when thinking** | Timer runs CONTINUOUS. Real exam doesn't pause when you think |
| **Being too casual with friend** | Agree beforehand: formal mode only |
| **Giving up after a bad answer** | The exam continues regardless. Train yourself to reset mentally |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Video record yourself** – you'll catch posture issues you didn't notice
2. **Check your watch** – are you running out of things to say in Part 2?
3. **Note your energy dip point** – when do you start getting tired?
4. **Compare with Mock Exams ① & ②** – you SHOULD see progress
