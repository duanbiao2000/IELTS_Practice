# Task 85: Review Recording – Analyze Your Tone & Pitch

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why analyze your tone in detail?**

Most IELTS candidates have no idea they sound monotone until they hear themselves. This task:
- Makes you aware of your pitch patterns
- Identifies specific monotone sections
- Shows you WHERE variety is needed
- Gives you targeted areas to improve

### The Monotone Problem

| Monotone Speech | Varied Speech |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Flat, robotic delivery | Dynamic, expressive |
| Harder to follow | Engaging, clear |
| Lower pronunciation scores | Higher pronunciation scores |
| Sounds bored or nervous | Sounds interested and confident |

**Good news:** Pitch variation is a SKILL, not a talent. You can learn it.

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Tone Analysis

**Original (Monotone):**
"So I was going to the store. I bought some groceries. Then I came home. It was normal."

**Analysis:**
- "So I was going →" (flat statement)
- "to the store →" (continuing flat)
- "I bought some groceries →" (no emphasis)
- "Then I came home →" (no variation)
- "It was normal →" (flat conclusion)

**Improved (Varied):**
"So ↑ I was going to the store ↓, just a regular day ↑. I bought some groceries ↓,
and then ↑ something weird happened ↑! I came home ↓, and... it was NOT normal ↓."

**What changed:**
- Up inflection on "So" (story beginning)
- Down inflection after "store" (completing thought)
- Higher pitch on "weird" (excitement)
- Stress on "NOT" (contrast)
- Final down inflection (conclusion)

### When to Vary Your Pitch

| Function | Pitch Direction | Example |
|----------|-----------------|---------|
| **Beginning a story** | UP slightly | "So, there I was..." |
| **Listing items** | UP on each, DOWN on final | "First ↑, second ↑, and third ↓" |
| **Showing surprise** | WAY UP | "I couldn't believe it!" |
| **Being certain** | DOWN | "I know that's true." |
| **Asking (real) question** | UP at end | "Really?" |
| **Showing doubt** | UP in middle | "I... don't know..." |
| **Emphasizing** | UP on key word | "It was AMAZING." |
| **Concluding** | DOWN | "And that's what happened." |

### Common Monotone Patterns

| Pattern | Why It Happens | Fix |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| **List monotone** | Treating all items equally | Vary: "First ↑, second ↑, third ↓" |
| **End-of-sentence drop** | Always going down at end | Vary: Up for questions/emotion, down for certainty |
| **Mid-sentence flatness** | Rushing, no breath | Add natural pauses |
| **Emotional neutrality** | Not feeling the story | Actually experience the emotion |
| **Reading aloud** | Eyes scanning, voice flat | Look up, speak from memory |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Awareness is 80% of the Solution**
> Most monotone speakers have NO IDEA they sound flat. This analysis makes you conscious of your pitch patterns. Once aware, you naturally start to vary.

**2. Pitch Variety is Physical**
It's not just mental – it's physical:
- **Open your mouth wider** → more resonance
- **Use more air** → more energy in voice
- **Move your body** → voice follows body
- **Make faces** → facial expressions affect tone

**3. The "Rule of Three"**
Groups of three are your friend for variety:
- "First ↑, second ↑, and finally ↓"
- "I thought ↑, I felt ↑, and I knew ↓"

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Not hearing monotone** | Record yourself and listen. If you still don't hear it, ask someone else |
| **Over-correcting** | Don't make it dramatic! Subtle variation is natural, swinging is weird |
| **Only varying at sentence ends** | Vary WITHIN sentences too, not just at periods |
| **Forced variation** | Real emotion creates real variation. Don't fake it – feel it. |
| **Inconsistent variation** | Practice the SAME story 5 times, adding variety in the same places each time |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Use your hands** – hand gestures naturally vary your voice
2. **Stand up** – better posture = better voice
3. **Read to a child** – their feedback is immediate (bored vs. interested)
4. **Record yourself daily** – track your improvement over time
5. **Mimic emotional speakers** – notice how they use their voice
