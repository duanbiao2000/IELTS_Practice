# Task 11: Shadowing Day 2 - Focus on Intonation

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**What is Intonation?**

Intonation is the **"music" of speech**—the rise and fall of your voice as you speak. It's NOT what you say, it's **how you say it**.

**Why Intonation Matters:**

| Without Good Intonation | With Good Intonation |
|-------------------------|----------------------|
| Sounds robotic, flat | Sounds engaged, human |
| Hard to follow | Easy to understand |
| Boring to listen to | Interesting, expressive |
| Lower IELTS score | Higher IELTS score |

### Key Concepts

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Rise** | Voice goes up | Questions ("Really?") |
| **Fall** | Voice goes down | Statements ("I agree.") |
| **Rise-Fall** | Up then down | Surprise, emphasis |
| **Fall-Rise** | Down then up | Uncertainty, hesitation |

### Intonation Patterns to Notice Today

**1. Questions vs. Statements:**
- Questions (yes/no): RISE at end "Are you coming?"
- Statements: FALL at end "I'm coming tomorrow."
- Wh-questions: Slight FALL "Where are you going?"

**2. Lists and Series:**
- Rise, rise, RISE-FALL "I need eggs, milk, and bread."

**3. Emphasis/Contrast:**
- Extra stress + higher pitch "I said HERE, not there."

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Common Intonation Patterns

**1. YES/NO Questions (Rise at end)**
```
Are you coming? ↗
Do you like it? ↗
Can you help me? ↗
```

**2. WH-Questions (Fall at end)**
```
Where are you going? ↘
What time is it? ↘
How does it work? ↘
```

**3. Statements (Fall at end)**
```
I live in Beijing. ↘
She's a doctor. ↘
The weather is nice today. ↘
```

**4. Lists (Rise, rise, rise-fall)**
```
I like apples, ↗ bananas, ↗ and oranges. ↘
First, ↗ second, ↗ and finally. ↘
```

**5. Contrast/Emphasis (Higher pitch on stressed word)**
```
I said NOW, ↘ not later.
SHE went, ↘ not him.
I WANT to, ↘ not I have to. ↘
```

**6. Uncertainty/Hesitation (Fall-Rise)**
```
Well... ↘↗ I'm not sure.
It depends... ↘↗ maybe.
Actually... ↘↗ I think so. ↘↗
```

### Sample Sentence with Intonation Marked

> "You ↗ know, I've been ↘ thinking about HOW ↘ much our ↘ phones have become like an ↘ extension of our↘selves these ↘ DAYS. I ↘ MEAN, I ↗ HONESTLY can't re↘MEMber the ↘ LAST time I ↘ WENT more than an ↘ HOUR with↘OUT checking ↘ MINE."

### Why These Patterns Work

| Pattern | Function | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Final rise** | Signals question, uncertainty | "Really?" ↗ |
| **Final fall** | Signals certainty, completion | "I agree." ↘ |
| **Mid-sentence rise** | Incomplete thought, continuation | "First..." ↗ |
| **Extra stress** | Emphasizes key information | "NOW, not later." ⬆ |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Hand Gestures Work**
> Your voice and your hands are connected.
> When you move your hand UP, your voice naturally follows.
> This isn't silly—it's a proven technique used by voice coaches worldwide.
>
> Exaggerate first, then refine. Big movements help you FEEL the pattern.

**2. English vs. Chinese Intonation**
Mandarin uses pitch for **tone** (each syllable has a fixed tone).
English uses pitch for **intonation** (pitch changes across phrases).
This is why your English might sound flat—your brain is used to syllable-level tone, not phrase-level intonation.

**3. The "Question Check"**
Native speakers rise at the end of yes/no questions.
Many learners don't do this, making questions sound like statements.
Practice: "Really?" ↗ (not ↘)

**4. Emotion Through Pitch**
When you're excited, your pitch goes higher.
When you're serious, your pitch goes lower.
This emotional range is what makes speech human, not robotic.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Flat delivery** | Sounds robotic, bored | Exaggerate with hand gestures first |
| **Only using one pitch** | Monotone, hard to follow | Practice rise-fall patterns deliberately |
| **Rising at the end of everything** | Everything sounds uncertain | Fall at end of statements |
| **No contrast/emphasis** | Hard to know what's important | Stress key words with higher pitch |
| **Ignoring emotional content** | Missed communication meaning | Match pitch to emotion (excited = higher) |
| **Too much variation** | Sounds dramatic, unnatural | Tone down after exaggerating |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Record and compare** - record your shadowing, compare to original
2. **Watch TED Talks** - speakers are masters of intonation
3. **Listen to podcasts** - focus on pitch, not content
4. **Mimic emotional speech** - excited, questioning, agreeing
5. **Practice the "question rise"** - this is the most common error
