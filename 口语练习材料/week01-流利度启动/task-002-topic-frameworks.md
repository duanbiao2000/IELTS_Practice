# Task 2: Create Your 10 High-Frequency Topic Frameworks

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [10 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why are topic frameworks essential?**

In IELTS Speaking Part 2, you'll receive a topic card and have 1 minute to prepare. Without frameworks, you'll waste precious time thinking about WHAT to say. With frameworks, you focus on HOW to say it.

**The 3-Point Framework Advantage:**
- Instant structure for any topic
- Reduces mental load during pressure
- Ensures balanced, organized responses
- Prevents running out of things to say

### Key Concepts

| Concept | Explanation | Benefit |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Framework** | Pre-planned structure for answering | No panic during test |
| **Bullet points** | Keywords, not full sentences | Quick mental access |
| **Universal points** | Works across similar topics | Reusable content |
| **Personal connection** | Add your real experiences | More natural delivery |

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Understand the 3-Point Structure [2 min]

Each topic needs exactly **3 key points**:
```
Point 1: Basic description / what it is
Point 2: Personal experience / why it matters
Point 3: Opinion / feeling / future
```

**Example - Hometown:**
- Location & size (small coastal city)
- Best feature (beaches, relaxed lifestyle)
- Feeling (proud, but want more opportunities)

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Keywords, Not Sentences**
> Your brain can process keywords faster than full sentences. During the actual IELTS test, you only have 1 minute to prepare. Keywords = speed.

**2. The "3-Point Rule"**
Three points is the magic number because:
- It's enough for a full 2-minute response
- It's easy to remember under pressure
- It creates natural structure (beginning, middle, end)

**3. Personal = Memorable**
Examiners hear hundreds of responses. Personal details make yours stand out. Always connect frameworks to YOUR life.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Writing full sentences** | Too slow to memorize | Use only 2-3 words per point |
| **Being too generic** | Sounds rehearsed, boring | Add specific details (names, places) |
| **Making points too similar** | Redundant content | Ensure each point adds NEW info |
| **No personal connection** | Lacks authenticity | Always include "I" statements |
| **Translating from Chinese** | Unnatural English | Think directly in English keywords |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Use visual cues** - Draw simple icons next to points
2. **Group similar topics** - Environment & Health can share points
3. **Review weekly** - Update frameworks as your English improves
4. **Practice from random points** - Pick a topic, start from Point 2 first
