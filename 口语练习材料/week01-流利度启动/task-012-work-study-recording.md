# Task 12: Record Your "Work/Study" Topic

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why "Work/Study" is Essential**

This is the **second most common IELTS topic**:
- **Always asked** in Part 1 (Work or Study, not both)
- **Sets the first impression** with the examiner
- **Safe territory**—you know this topic intimately
- **Good opportunity** to show vocabulary range

**Your Framework from Task 2:**

```
WORK/STUDY FRAMEWORK:
Point 1: What you do (role, field, institution)
Point 2: What you like about it (challenges, rewards)
Point 3: Future plans (career path, further study)
```

### Key Concepts

| Element | For Work | For Study |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| **What** | Job title, company, industry | Major, university, year |
| **Why you like it** | Challenges, team, growth | Subjects, research, campus life |
| **Future** | Promotion, new role, business | Further study, career, research |
| **Vocabulary** | Projects, deadlines, collaboration | Assignments, research, professors |

### Useful Vocabulary

**For Work:**
- demanding, rewarding, challenging, collaborative
- team projects, deadlines, responsibilities
- career progression, professional development

**For Study:**
- engaging, rigorous, specialized, theoretical
- coursework, research, seminar, dissertation
- academic goals, field of study

### Pre-Task Reflection
> **Ask yourself:** Do you work or are you a student?
> I am a: _________________ (student / worker)
> What do you find most interesting about your work/study?
> _________________________________________________

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Response - Work

**Transcript (60 seconds):**

"I work as a software engineer at a technology company here in Shanghai. I've been in this role for about three years now. My main responsibilities involve developing web applications and collaborating with the design team to create user-friendly interfaces.

What I really enjoy about my job is the problem-solving aspect. Every project presents new challenges that require creative solutions. I also appreciate the collaborative environment—my team is incredibly supportive, and we learn from each other constantly. The work can be demanding at times, especially when we have tight deadlines, but I find that kind of pressure motivating rather than stressful.

Looking ahead, my goal is to move into a team leadership position within the next two years. I'm also considering pursuing a master's degree abroad to gain international exposure and bring back new skills to my company. Ultimately, I'd like to start my own tech business, but that's a longer-term plan."

### Sample Response - Study

**Transcript (60 seconds):**

"I'm currently a final-year university student majoring in Business Administration at Zhejiang University. It's a four-year program, and I'm now completing my senior year and working on my thesis.

What I find most engaging about my course is how it connects theory with real-world applications. We study various case studies from actual companies, which makes the learning feel practical and relevant. The professors are quite experienced—some have worked in corporate settings themselves, so they bring valuable insights to the classroom. I also enjoy the group projects, even though they can be challenging with different schedules and personalities.

After graduation, I plan to gain some work experience first, ideally in a multinational company. Then I'm considering pursuing an MBA abroad, perhaps in the UK or Singapore, to specialize in international business. My long-term goal is to work in business development for a global company."

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. The "Why" Matters More Than "What"**
> Many students spend too much time describing their job/major.
> "I work as a software engineer..." (10 seconds)
> Better: "I work as a software engineer, and what I love about it is..." (immediate depth)
>
> Examiners want to hear YOUR thoughts, not a job description.

**2. Specifics > Generalities**
Notice the samples include specifics:
- Work: "three years," "web applications," "team leadership"
- Study: "final year," "thesis," "Business Administration"

Specific details make your answer credible and interesting.

**3. Future Ambition Shows Drive**
Both samples include clear future plans:
- Work: leadership position, master's, own business
- Study: work experience, MBA, international business

Future-oriented answers score higher because they show forward thinking.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Only describing duties** | Sounds like job ad | Add personal feelings and opinions |
| **Being too brief on future** | Misses opportunity | Include concrete future plans |
| **Using only basic language** | Limited vocabulary range | Prepare 5-8 advanced terms beforehand |
| **Sounding rehearsed** | Unnatural delivery | Practice until comfortable, then sound spontaneous |
| **No emotional connection** | Flat, unengaging | Share what you genuinely like/dislike |
| **Talking too long on one point** | Unbalanced, time management | Time each point: ~20 seconds |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Prepare stories** - have 2-3 specific examples ready
2. **Know your "why"** - be ready to explain why you chose this path
3. **Show progression** - mention how you've grown
4. **Connect to IELTS** - mention why you're taking the test
5. **Be honest about challenges** - it shows self-awareness
