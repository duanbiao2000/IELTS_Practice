# Task 4: Record Your 60-Second Self-Introduction

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why start with a self-introduction?**

A self-introduction is the perfect first recording because:
- It's **personal** - you know this topic better than any other
- It's **flexible** - no wrong answers, no right or wrong
- It's **diagnostic** - reveals your natural speaking patterns
- It's **confidence-building** - easier than abstract IELTS topics

**The Golden Rule for This Task:**
> **Don't worry about grammar. Don't worry about vocabulary.**
> **Just. Keep. Talking.**

### Key Concepts

| Element | Description | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| **Fluency first** | Keep speech flowing, even with mistakes | Stopping breaks your momentum |
| **Fillers are OK** | "um," "uh," "like" are better than silence | Buys thinking time |
| **Grammar second** | Fix grammar later, fluency now | You can't improve what you don't try |
| **60 seconds** | Standard Part 2 speaking time | Get used to this duration |

### Self-Introduction Structure

Use this simple 4-part structure:
```
1. WHO you are (name, background)
2. WHERE you're from (hometown)
3. WHAT you do (work/study)
4. WHY you're taking IELTS (goals)
```

## 🎯 GUIDED PRACTICE

### Task Overview
Record a 60-second self-introduction. The goal is **continuous speech** - no pauses longer than 2 seconds.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Prepare Your Points [3 min]

Jot down 4-6 keywords for each part:
```
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
              MY SELF-INTRODUCTION KEYWORDS
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. WHO (name, age, background)
   _________________________________________________

2. WHERE (hometown, location, features)
   _________________________________________________

3. WHAT (work/study, field, experience)
   _________________________________________________

4. WHY (IELTS goals, future plans)
   _________________________________________________

════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

#### STEP 2: Set Up Your Recording Environment [2 min]

- [ ] Find a quiet space
- [ ] Use your phone's voice recorder app
- [ ] Test recording levels (speak at normal volume)
- [ ] Have a timer ready (phone stopwatch works)

#### STEP 3: Practice Run (Optional) [2 min]

Do a quick practice run without recording. Just get comfortable saying the points aloud.

#### STEP 4: RECORD [1 min]

Press record and speak for 60 seconds. Remember:
- **Keep going even if you make mistakes**
- **Use filler words if you need thinking time**
- **Don't stop to correct yourself**
- **Aim for close to 60 seconds**

#### STEP 5: Save and Label [1 min]

Name your recording: `self-intro-day1-[date].m4a`
Save it in a dedicated folder: `IELTS Speaking Recordings`

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Self-Introduction (Transcript)

"Hi, my name is Zhang Wei and I'm 26 years old. I come from Chengdu, which is a city in southwest China famous for its spicy food and pandas. I've been living there my whole life with my parents and younger sister.

Currently, I work as a software engineer at a tech company. I've been in this role for about three years now, and I really enjoy the problem-solving aspect of my job. Before this, I studied computer science at university.

I'm preparing for the IELTS exam because I want to pursue my master's degree abroad, hopefully in the UK or Australia. I believe studying overseas will not only improve my English but also give me international exposure and better career opportunities. My target score is 7.0, and I've been preparing for about two months now."

### Analysis of Sample

| Element | What Worked | Why It Worked |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **Opening** | Clear name, age, origin | Immediate context |
| **Hometown** | Added detail (spicy food, pandas) | Memorable, personal |
| **Work** | Gave timeline and reason | Shows progression |
| **IELTS goals** | Specific target and destination | Clear motivation |
| **Fluency** | Natural transitions | No long pauses |

### Alternative Opening Styles

**Style 1: Direct**
"Hello, I'm [name]. I'm from [city] and I work as a [job]. I'm taking IELTS because..."

**Style 2: Story-based**
"Let me tell you a bit about myself. I grew up in [city], which is known for..."

**Style 3: Interest-first**
"I've always been interested in [field], which is why I became a [job]..."

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Your First Recording Baseline**
> This recording is NOT about perfection. It's about establishing a baseline.
> In Task 5, you'll count your pauses. In Week 4, you'll compare.
> The goal is progress, not immediate excellence.

**2. Why 60 Seconds?**
IELTS Speaking Part 2 requires speaking for 1-2 minutes. Starting with 60 seconds builds your fluency foundation. Once you're comfortable at 60 seconds, extending to 2 minutes is much easier.

**3. The "Keep Talking" Mindset**
The biggest barrier to fluency is **self-editing**—stopping to fix mistakes in real-time. Train yourself to keep flowing. You can work on grammar separately. During speaking, flow first.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Memorizing a script** | Sounds robotic, forget under pressure | Use keywords only, trust yourself |
| **Speaking too fast** | Unclear, runs out of material | Slow down, pace yourself |
| **Over-explaining one point** | Time runs out, missing other parts | Allocate ~15 seconds per section |
| **Getting stuck on a word** | Long pause, lose momentum | Skip it, use synonym, keep going |
| **Sound too formal** | Unnatural, unlike conversation | Imagine talking to a friend |
| **Recording in noisy place** | Poor quality, hard to review | Quiet room, close to microphone |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Stand up while recording** - opens your breathing, improves voice quality
2. **Use hand gestures** - helps with thinking and expression
3. **Smile while speaking** - your voice sounds warmer and more confident
4. **Don't delete your "bad" takes** - save them to track progress
5. **Record at the same time daily** - builds consistency
