## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Response - Technology

**Transcript (60 seconds):**

"Well, I'd say technology is an incredibly important part of my daily life. Like most people, my smartphone is basically indispensable—I use it for everything from communication to entertainment to managing my schedule.

The main way technology helps me is staying connected. Through messaging apps and social media, I can keep in touch with friends and family even when we're far apart. It's also incredibly useful for my work—I can collaborate with colleagues, access documents, and even attend meetings from anywhere. That kind of flexibility would have been unimaginable just a few decades ago.

That said, I do have some concerns. I sometimes feel I'm spending too much time on my phone, especially scrolling through social media. It's somewhat distracting when I'm trying to focus on other things. So I'm trying to find a better balance—using technology intentionally rather than automatically. Overall though, I think the benefits significantly outweigh the drawbacks."

### Analysis of Sample

| Element | What Worked | Why It Worked |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **Degree adverbs** | incredibly, significantly, somewhat | Shows vocabulary range |
| **Framework** | Clear 3-part structure | Organized, easy to follow |
| **Specific examples** | messaging apps, collaboration, documents | Concrete, credible |
| **Balanced view** | Benefits + concerns | Shows critical thinking |
| **Personal** | "I'd say," "I do have concerns" | Authentic, engaged |
| **Advanced vocabulary** | indispensable, unimaginable, outweigh | Precise, sophisticated |

### Useful Sentence Frames for Technology

**Opening:**
- "I'd say technology is..." / "Technology plays a major role in..."
- "My smartphone is basically indispensable..."

**Benefits:**
- "The main way technology helps me is..."
- "It's incredibly useful for..."
- "That kind of [flexibility/convenience] would have been unimaginable..."

**Concerns:**
- "That said, I do have some concerns..."
- "I sometimes feel I'm spending too much time..."
- "It can be somewhat distracting..."

**Conclusion:**
- "Overall though, I think the benefits outweigh the drawbacks."
- "I'm trying to find a better balance..."

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Using Your Degree Adverbs**
Notice how the sample uses your Task 9 words:
- "incredibly important" (strong)
- "somewhat distracting" (mild)
- "significantly outweigh" (strong)

This is **exactly** why you learned those words—to show precise control over language.

**2. The "That Said" Transition**
The sample uses "That said, I do have some concerns..."
This is an excellent transition marker that shows:
- You can see multiple perspectives
- You have sophisticated discourse skills
- You can express balanced opinions

**Add "That said," "However," "On the other hand" to your repertoire.**

**3. Specifics Make It Real**
"Using messaging apps and social media" > "using communication tools"
"Collaborate with colleagues, access documents" > "do work"

Specifics prove you're not just memorizing generic answers.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Being too general** | Sounds rehearsed, unoriginal | Add specific apps/use cases |
| **Only positive or only negative** | Lacks depth, balance | Always show both sides |
| **Forgetting to use new vocabulary** | Wasted learning opportunity | Plan which words to use beforehand |
| **Speaking in a monotone** | Boring, low score | Use intonation practice from shadowing |
| **Running out of things to say** | Incomplete response | Prepare 2-3 specific examples |
| **Making technology sound purely negative** | Misses the question prompt | Acknowledge benefits first |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Connect to your life** - talk about apps/tools you actually use
2. **Prepare balanced view** - 2 benefits, 1 concern (or vice versa)
3. **Use transition words** - however, on the other hand, that said
4. **Practice with real tech** - explain your favorite app to someone
5. **Stay current** - mention recent developments (AI, remote work)
