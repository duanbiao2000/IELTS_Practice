### Key Concepts

|------|---------|-----------|
| **Phoneme** | Individual sound unit | /θ/, /ð/, /æ/, /r/, /v/ |
| **Minimal pair** | Words differing by one sound | think/sink, very/wary |

### 🎤 Detailed Pronunciation Guide

**Most Critical Sounds for Chinese Speakers:**

| Sound | IPA | Mouth Position | Common Error | How to Fix |
|-------|-----|----------------|--------------|------------|
| **th (voiceless)** | /θ/ | Tongue TIP between teeth, blow air out | → /s/ "sink" | Stick tongue out PAST teeth |
| **th (voiced)** | /ð/ | Same + VOICE (vibrate cords) | → /z/ "zis" | Hum while saying th |
| **short a** | /æ/ | Mouth WIDE open, tongue low | → /e/ "men" | Open mouth like at dentist |
| **v sound** | /v/ | Top TEETH on bottom lip | → /w/ "wery" | Bite lip gently, vibrate |
| **r sound** | /r/ | Tongue curled BACK, lips rounded | → /l/ "lice" | Pull tongue back, don't touch |

### Common Problem Phonemes for Chinese Speakers

| Sound | Symbol | Common Error | Practice Words |
|-------|--------|--------------|----------------|
| th (voiceless) | /θ/ | → /s/ (think → sink) | think, third, through |
| th (voiced) | /ð/ | → /z/ or /d/ (this → zis) | this, that, they |
| r/l | /r/ vs /l/ | rice → lice | read/lead, right/light |
| v/w | /v/ vs /w/ | very → wary | very/worry, vine/wine |
| æ/e | /æ/ vs /e/ | man/men | man/men, bat/bet |
| final consonants | -ed, -s | dropped | worked, stopped, asked |

### Self-Correction Techniques

**1. The "Extreme" Method**
- Exaggerate the mouth position
- /θ/: Stick tongue out farther than feels natural
- /æ/: Open mouth wider than feels natural
- /v/: Bite lip more firmly than feels natural
- THEN gradually pull back to normal

**2. The Hand Mirror Check**
- Watch yourself in a mirror
- /θ/ and /ð/: You MUST see your tongue
- /v/: You MUST see teeth on lip
- If you don't see it, you're not doing it

**3. The Recording Comparison**
- Record yourself saying 5 minimal pairs
- Compare with native pronunciation (use online dictionaries)
- Listen specifically for YOUR problem sound

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Practice Session - /θ/ (th sound)

**Mouth Position:**
- Tip of tongue between teeth
- Blow air out (don't use voice)
- Keep teeth slightly apart

**Practice Words (repeat 10x each):**
1. think - thank - thought - through - third
2. thirty - thirteen - thumb - theatre - theme
3. healthy - wealthy - anything - nothing - everything

**Sentences:**
1. "I think thirty-three is the perfect number."
2. "Thank you for the thoughtful gift."
3. "My thumb hurts when I think about it."

### Sample Practice Session - /ð/ (voiced th)

**Mouth Position:**
- Tip of tongue between teeth
- Use your VOICE (vibrate vocal cords)
- Softer than /θ/

**Practice Words (repeat 10x each):**
1. this - that - these - those - they
2. there - their - then - than - though
3. mother - father - brother - weather - rather

**Sentences:**
1. "This is their brother, not that one."
2. "Mother and father are rather worried about the weather."
3. "They'd rather go there than then."

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why 10 Repetitions?**
> Research shows that 8-10 repetitions are needed for muscle memory.
> Your mouth needs to "learn" the new position.
> One or two times isn't enough—repetition is key.

**2. The Progression: Sound → Word → Sentence**
Notice the practice structure:
1. Isolated sound (learn the position)
2. Individual words (practice the position)
3. Sentences (use in context)

This gradual progression ensures lasting improvement.

**3. Don't Chase Perfect Scores**
ELSA can be strict. A score of 80+ is excellent.
Don't repeat the same word 50 times to get 100.
Move on—consistency matters more than perfection.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Practicing only sounds** | Can't use in real speech | Always include sentence practice |
| **Rushing through** | No muscle memory built | Do full 10 reps, don't skip |
| **Ignoring mouth position** | Old habits return | Watch the video, mimic the position |
| **Only doing once** | Forgets quickly | Practice each sound daily this week |
| **Focusing on score only** | Misses the point | Score is feedback, focus on accuracy |
| **Comparing to native** | Unrealistic standard | Aim for clear, not perfect |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Practice at the same time daily** - builds habit
2. **Record yourself** - compare before/after
3. **Use a mirror** - check mouth position visually
4. **Practice minimal pairs** - trains your ear too
5. **Track scores** - celebrate improvement, even small gains
