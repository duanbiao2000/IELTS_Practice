# Task 17: Learn 5 Connecting Words

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin

**Why Are Connecting Words Essential?**

Connecting words (also called **linking words** or **discourse markers**) are the **glue** of fluent speech. They:
- Show relationships between ideas
- Make your speech flow naturally
- Signal what's coming next
- Impress IELTS examiners (Band 7+ feature)
- Buy you thinking time

**The 5 Words You'll Learn Today:**

| Word | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Furthermore** | Adding another point |
| **In contrast** | Showing opposition |
| **What's more** | Adding emphasis |
| **As a result** | Showing cause/effect |
| **That said** | Acknowledging counterpoint |

### Key Concepts

| Type | Function | Examples |
|------|----------|----------|
| **Addition** | Adding more information | Furthermore, What's more, Moreover |
| **Contrast** | Showing opposite view | In contrast, However, On the other hand |
| **Result** | Showing consequence | As a result, Therefore, Consequently |
| **Concession** | Acknowledging other view | That said, Admittedly, While true |

### Connecting Words in Speech vs. Writing

| Word | More Common In | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| Furthermore | Writing | Formal, structured |
| What's more | Speech | More conversational |
| In contrast | Both | Works well everywhere |
| As a result | Both | Clear consequence marker |
| That said | Speech | Conversational, natural |

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Complete IELTS-Style Examples

**1. FURTHERMORE - Adding Points**
> "My hometown has excellent weather throughout the year. **Furthermore**, the cost of living is quite affordable, making it an ideal place for families."

**2. IN CONTRAST - Showing Difference**
> "I used to find English incredibly difficult. **In contrast**, now I actually enjoy speaking it, especially after practicing daily."

**3. WHAT'S MORE - Adding Emphasis**
> "Online courses are convenient because you can study anytime. **What's more**, they're often much cheaper than traditional classes."

**4. AS A RESULT - Cause and Effect**
> "I started shadowing practice every day. **As a result**, my pronunciation has improved significantly, and I feel much more confident."

**5. THAT SAID - Balanced View**
> "Social media helps me stay connected with friends. **That said**, I try to limit my screen time because it can be quite distracting."

### Connecting Word Position Guide

| Word | Position in Sentence | Example |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| **Furthermore** | Beginning of new sentence | "Furthermore, the..." |
| **In contrast** | Beginning of sentence | "In contrast, my..." |
| **What's more** | Beginning of sentence | "What's more, it..." |
| **As a result** | Beginning or middle | "As a result, I..." or "I, as a result,..." |
| **That said** | Beginning of sentence | "That said, I do..." |

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Wrong | Right |
|---------|-------|-------|
| **Comma usage** | "Furthermore it is..." | "Furthermore, it is..." |
| **Overuse** | Using same word repeatedly | Rotate all 5 words |
| **Wrong context** | "In contrast, I also agree" | "Furthermore, I also agree" |
| **Too formal** | In casual conversation | "Also" is fine sometimes |
| **Forced** | Unnatural placement | Only where it fits naturally |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Examiners Love These Words**
> Connecting words are like signposts in a city.
> They tell your listener where you're going:
> - "I'm adding more" (Furthermore)
> - "I'm switching directions" (In contrast)
> - "Here's the consequence" (As a result)
>
> Clear signposting = Clear communication = Higher Score.

**2. Speech vs. Writing Difference**
"Furthermore" sounds very formal in speech.
"What's more" is more conversational.
"That said" works great in speaking—it sounds natural and sophisticated.

**For IELTS Speaking: Use "What's more" and "That said" more often.**

**3. The Pause Effect**
These words create a natural pause:
"FURTHERMORE... [micro-pause] ...the next point"
This pause gives you thinking time while sounding deliberate.
Brilliant for when you need a moment to think!

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Using only "and/but/so"** | Limited range, lower score | Rotate all 5 connecting words |
| **Forgetting the comma** | Run-on sentence feeling | Always pause briefly after the word |
| **Using in wrong context** | Confusing meaning | Check: am I adding? contrasting? showing result? |
| **Overusing one word** | Repetitive, sounds rehearsed | Use variety: all 5 words |
| **Sounding too formal** | Unnatural in conversation | Use "What's more" and "That said" more |
| **Not practicing aloud** | Can't use under pressure | Say each sentence 10+ times |
