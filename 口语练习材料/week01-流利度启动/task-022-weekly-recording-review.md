## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. The Power of Review**
> Most students never listen to their recordings.
> You're doing something powerful: reviewing your progress.
>
> This review does THREE things:
> 1. Shows you concrete progress (motivation!)
> 2. Identifies specific areas to work on (focus!)
> 3. Locks in what you've learned (reinforcement!)
>
> Keep this habit. Review weekly. It's worth the time.

**2. Progress Isn't Linear**
You might notice:
- Some recordings are better than others
- Improvement isn't steady every time
- Some days you're more tired/nervous

**This is NORMAL. Progress happens in steps, not a straight line.**

**3. Celebrate Small Wins**
Did you use ONE connector successfully? That's a win.
Did you reduce pauses by ONE? That's progress.
Did you finish a recording without giving up? That's achievement.

**Celebrate everything. Progress is progress.**

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Skipping the review** | Misses the learning opportunity | Set aside time, make it non-negotiable |
| **Only focusing on weaknesses** | Demotivating, misses progress | Balance wins + areas for work |
| **Comparing to others** | Unfair, discouraging | Compare ONLY to your past self |
| **Not noticing improvement** | Feels like no progress | Look at specific metrics (pause count) |
| **Deleting old recordings** | Can't track progress | Keep ALL recordings, even the bad ones |
| **Being too harsh** | Demotivates, creates anxiety | Be your own supportive coach |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Keep a progress spreadsheet** - track pauses, length, connectors used
2. **Save all recordings** - create a "Week 1" folder
3. **Listen to first vs. last** - most motivating comparison
4. **Share with someone** - accountability and celebration
5. **Set Week 2 goals** - based on what you noticed in review
