# Task 1: ELSA Pronunciation Diagnostic Test

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why does pronunciation matter?**

Pronunciation is not about sounding like a native speaker perfectly. It's about **being understood**. In IELTS Speaking:
- Clear pronunciation = higher Band Score
- You don't need to lose your accent
- Focus on **intelligibility**, not perfection

### Key Concepts

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Phoneme** | Individual sound unit | /θ/ in "think", /ð/ in "this" |
| **Intelligibility** | How well others understand you | Can people follow you easily? |
| **Stress** | Emphasis on syllables/words | PHOtograph vs phoTOgrapher |
| **Intonation** | Rise and fall of voice | Questions go up, statements go down |

### 🎤 Pronunciation Standards for IELTS

**Critical IPA Symbols You Must Know:**

| Symbol | Sound | Common Word | Chinese Error |
|--------|-------|-------------|---------------|
| /θ/ | voiceless th | think | → /s/ (sink) |
| /ð/ | voiced th | this | → /z/ or /d/ (zis) |
| /æ/ | short a | cat, man | → /e/ (ket, men) |
| /ʃ/ | sh sound | she, station | OK - similar to Chinese |
| /tʃ/ | ch sound | chair, match | → /tʃeə/ vs /tʃə/ |
| /dʒ/ | j sound | job, education | → /dʒəb/ vs /ʒəb/ |
| /ŋ/ | ng sound | sing, thinking | → /n/ or missing |
| /v/ | v sound | very | → /w/ (wery) |
| /r/ | r sound | red, right | → /l/ confusion |

**Mouth Position Reference:**
- **/θ/ (th):** Tip of tongue BETWEEN teeth, blow air out
- **/ð/ (th):** Same position, but VOICE your sound (vibrate vocal cords)
- **/æ/:** Mouth WIDE open, tongue low, like at the doctor
- **/v/:** Top teeth TOUCH bottom lip, vibrate vocal cords
- **/r/:** Tongue curled back, NOT touching roof of mouth

### What is ELSA Speak?
ELSA (English Language Speech Assistant) is an AI-powered app that:
- Analyzes your pronunciation in real-time
- Gives immediate feedback on specific sounds
- Tracks your progress over time
- Focuses on problem areas individually

| **90-100** | Excellent | Focus on advanced nuances |
| **75-89** | Good | Target specific problem areas |
| **60-74** | Fair | Regular practice on weak sounds |
| **Below 60** | Needs Work | Systematic sound practice |

### Common Problem Areas for Chinese Speakers

| Sound | Symbol | Common Mistake | Why It Happens |
|-------|--------|----------------|----------------|
| th (voiceless) | /θ/ | → /s/ (think → sink) | No /θ/ in Mandarin |
| th (voiced) | /ð/ | → /z/ or /d/ (this → zis) | No /ð/ in Mandarin |
| r/l distinction | /r/ vs /l/ | rice → lice | Similar position |
| v/w distinction | /v/ vs /w/ | very → wary | No /v/ in some dialects |
| Final consonants | -ed, -s | Often dropped | Mandarin ends in vowels |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. The Purpose of a Baseline**
> "You can't improve what you don't measure."
> Your diagnostic score is NOT a judgment—it's data. You'll compare this with your Week 4 score to see real progress.

**2. Interpreting Your Score**
- A score of 60+ is sufficient for IELTS Band 6.0+
- A score of 75+ correlates with Band 7.0+
- **Don't obsess over the number**—focus on the specific sounds

**3. How ELSA Scores Work**
ELSA evaluates:
- **Accuracy**: Are you producing the right sound?
- **Intonation**: Is your pitch appropriate?
- **Fluency**: Is your speech smooth?

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Why It Happens | Coach's Fix |
|---------|----------------|-------------|
| **Testing in noisy environment** | Background noise confuses AI | Find quiet space, use headphones |
| **Holding phone too close/far** | Audio quality suffers | Hold phone 15-20cm from mouth |
| **Speaking too quietly** | App can't detect sounds | Speak at normal conversation volume |
| **Rushing through test** | Incomplete data | Take your time, read each sentence clearly |
| **Perfectionism paralysis** | Fear of low score prevents starting | Any score is better than no score! |

### 🎤 Pronunciation Self-Monitoring Guide

**How to Hear Your Own Errors:**

1. **The Record-and-Compare Method**
   - Record yourself reading a simple sentence
   - Compare with a native speaker recording
   - Listen SPECIFICALLY for problem sounds (th, v, r/l)

2. **The Mirror Technique**
   - Watch your mouth in a mirror while speaking
   - /θ/ and /ð/: Tongue MUST be visible between teeth
   - /v/: Bottom lip should touch top teeth
   - /r/: Lips should be slightly rounded

3. **The Hand-on-Throat Test**
   - Place hand on throat while speaking
   - VOICED sounds (/ð/, /v/, /z/) = vibration
   - VOICELESS sounds (/θ/, /f/, /s/) = no vibration

**Most Common Chinese Speaker Errors - Quick Reference:**

| Error | You Say | Should Be | Fix |
|-------|---------|-----------|-----|
| th → s | "sink" | "think" | Tongue between teeth |
| th → z/d | "zis" | "this" | Tongue between teeth + VOICE |
| v → w | "wery" | "very" | Teeth on lip, no rounding |
| æ → e | "men" | "man" | Open mouth WIDE |
| r → l | "lice" | "rice" | Curl tongue back, don't touch |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Screenshot everything**—results change, proof doesn't
2. **Don't practice before testing**—you want an honest baseline
3. **Note the time of day**—are you a morning or evening speaker?
4. **Test in the same environment** you'll practice in
