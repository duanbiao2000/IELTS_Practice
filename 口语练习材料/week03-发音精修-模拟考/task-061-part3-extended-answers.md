# Task 61: Part 3 Extended Answers (3+ Sentences, No um/uh)

## ЁЯУЪ PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why extended answers matter in Part 3?**

Part 3 is DISCUSSION, not Q&A. The examiner wants:
- **Extended responses** (not one-sentence answers)
- **Developed ideas** (with reasons and examples)
- **Critical thinking** (exploring multiple perspectives)
- **No fillers** (um/uh break the flow)

### The Part 3 Challenge

Part 3 questions are abstract and require thought:
- "How has technology changed education?"
- "What are the advantages of living in a big city?"
- "Do you think traditional skills are disappearing?"

**Your goal:** 3+ sentences per answer, ZERO "um/uh"

### The 3-Sentence Framework

Every Part 3 answer should follow this structure:

```
Sentence 1: Direct answer (your opinion)
Sentence 2: Reason or explanation
Sentence 3: Example or extension
(Optional) Sentence 4: Alternative perspective
```

## ЁЯУЭ MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Extended Answers

**Question 1:** "Do you think people are reading less nowadays?"

> "**Well**, I don't necessarily think people are reading less, but they're certainly reading differently. **Actually**, the rise of digital media has shifted reading from books to screensтАФpeople now consume articles, social media posts, and online content constantly. **For example**, someone might read dozens of online articles and social posts in a day, which technically counts as reading even if it's not traditional book reading. **From my perspective**, the concern should be less about quantity and more about the quality and depth of what we're reading."

**Question 2:** "What are the benefits of learning a foreign language?"

> "**That's an interesting question**, and I believe there are numerous benefits to learning another language. **First and foremost**, it enhances cognitive abilitiesтАФstudies have shown bilingual people are better at multitasking and problem-solving. **Additionally**, it opens up career opportunities in our increasingly globalized world. **For instance**, knowing a second language can make you a much more attractive candidate for international companies. **Actually**, I think the most profound benefit is cultural: learning a language gives you insight into how other people think and view the world."

**Question 3:** "How can cities become more environmentally friendly?"

> "**Let me think about that**... I believe there are several approaches cities can take to become more sustainable. **Well**, the most obvious would be investing in renewable energy infrastructure, like solar panels on public buildings and wind farms where feasible. **Another important step** would be improving public transportation to reduce reliance on private cars. **For example**, cities like Copenhagen have made cycling so convenient that more than 60% of commuters bike to work. **From my perspective**, we also need to rethink urban planning to create more green spaces and reduce waste through better recycling systems."

### Sentence Structure Templates for Part 3

| Purpose | Template | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| **Direct answer** | "I think/believe that..." | "I think that technology has..." |
| **Adding reason** | "The main reason is that..." | "The main reason is that..." |
| **Giving example** | "For example/instance..." | "For instance, many students..." |
| **Adding contrast** | "However/On the other hand..." | "However, there are also challenges..." |
| **Concluding** | "Overall/In conclusion..." | "Overall, I believe that..." |
| **Hedging** | "It seems to me that..." | "It seems to me that..." |

### Linking Words for Extended Answers

**Adding information:**
- Furthermore, Moreover, In addition, What's more

**Contrasting:**
- However, On the other hand, Having said that, Conversely

**Giving examples:**
- For example, For instance, To illustrate, A case in point

**Concluding:**
- Overall, In conclusion, All things considered, To sum up

### Common Part 3 Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Fix |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| **One-sentence answers** | Too brief for Part 3 | Always aim for 3+ sentences |
| **No examples** | Abstract without evidence | Add "For example..." |
| **No reasoning** | States opinion without support | Always ask "why?" |
| **Being too definite** | Shows limited critical thinking | Use qualifiers like "generally," "it depends" |
| **Not answering the question** | Goes off-topic | Listen carefully, address directly |

## ЁЯТб COACH'S NOTES

### ЁЯОУ Teaching Points

**1. Part 3 Is a Discussion, Not an Interview**
The examiner wants to see you can discuss ideas. This means:
- Developing your answers beyond simple responses
- Exploring different perspectives
- Using abstract and hypothetical language
- Showing you can think critically

**2. The 3-Sentence Minimum**
Three sentences ensures you:
- Give a complete answer
- Show reasoning ability
- Provide evidence
- Don't cut the conversation short

**3. Filler-Free Takes Practice**
You won't achieve this immediately. Track your progress:
- Week 1: 20+ fillers per session
- Week 2: 10-15 fillers per session
- Week 3: 5-10 fillers per session
- Week 4: 0-3 fillers per session (acceptable!)

### ЁЯФз Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Running out of things to say** | Prepare: answer + reason + example + extension. That's 4 sentences automatically |
| **Being too brief** | Imagine you're explaining to a friend who asked "why?" after each sentence |
| **Using "um" when thinking** | Pause instead. A 2-second pause is better than "um" |
| **Not understanding the question** | Ask for clarification: "Could you explain what you mean by...?" |
| **Being too quiet** | Project your voice. Quiet speech + fillers = low score |

### ЁЯОп Pro Tips

1. **The "Why" Test**: After each sentence, ask yourself "why?" and answer it. This naturally extends your response.
2. **Prepare Part 3 phrases**: Have ready phrases like "That's a complex issue..." and "I think there are several factors..."
3. **Practice with variety**: Don't use the same structure for every answer
4. **Record and count**: Be scientific about tracking your um/uh reduction
