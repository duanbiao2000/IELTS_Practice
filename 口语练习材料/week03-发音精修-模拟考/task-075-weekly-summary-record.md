## ЁЯУЪ PRE-STUDY [4 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why write and record a weekly summary?**

This task combines multiple skills:
- **Writing practice** (organizing thoughts in English)
- **Synthesis skills** (summarizing a week of learning)
- **Speaking practice** (reading your own writing aloud)
- **Pronunciation application** (using Week 3 improvements)
- **Progress documentation** (creating a record of your journey)

### The Weekly Summary Framework

A good weekly summary includes:
1. **What you practiced** (specific tasks/skills)
2. **What improved** (real progress noticed)
3. **What was challenging** (honest difficulties)
4. **What you learned** (insights and realizations)
5. **What's next** (Week 4 focus)

### Sample Weekly Summary

> "**This week, I focused primarily on pronunciation refinement** through daily ELSA practice, shadowing exercises, and two complete mock exams. **The most significant improvement I noticed** was a substantial reduction in filler soundsтАФI went from using eighteen 'um' and 'uh' sounds in my first mock exam to only four in the second, which represents a seventy-eight percent reduction. **However, I still find the distinction between /├ж/ and /e/ sounds quite challenging**, and I need to continue practicing minimal pairs like 'bad' versus 'bed' until the distinction becomes automatic. **What I learned most importantly this week** is that pronunciation isn't just about individual soundsтАФit's also about sentence stress, intonation, and the rhythm created through linking and weak forms. **For Week 4, I plan to continue my ELSA practice** on my top five problem words while adding more dialogue shadowing to improve my conversational intonation patterns."

**Analysis:**
- 5 sentences, complex structure
- Specific data included (78% reduction)
- Honest about challenges (/├ж/ vs /e/)
- Shows learning (pronunciation = rhythm, not just sounds)
- Clear Week 4 plan
- Advanced vocabulary (substantial, distinction, automatic, represents)

### Weekly Summary Templates

**Template 1: Progress-Focused**
"This week, I practiced [skills]. I improved most in [area]. [specific evidence]. The biggest challenge was [challenge]. I learned that [insight]. Next week, I'll focus on [Week 4 goal]."

**Template 2: Challenge-Focused**
"Week 3 was challenging but rewarding. I struggled with [challenge], but by practicing [strategy], I improved [specific improvement]. I realized that [insight]. Although [remaining challenge], I'm proud that [win]. For Week 4, I plan to [next steps]."

**Template 3: Learning-Focused**
"The most valuable thing I learned this week is [key learning]. This came from practicing [specific task]. I noticed improvement in [area] when I [what you did]. I still need to work on [challenge], but overall I feel [emotion]. Next week, I'm excited to [Week 4 focus]."

### Common Summary Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Too vague** | "I practiced speaking" vs. "I reduced fillers by 78%" | Be specific with data |
| **Only positives** | Not honest about challenges | Include real difficulties |
| **Looking backward only** | Should also look forward | Include Week 4 focus |
| **Too brief** | 1-2 sentences isn't enough | 5+ sentences minimum |
| **Chinese thinking** | Defeats the purpose | Think and write in English |

### Complex Sentence Structures for Summaries

**Practice these in your summary:**

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **Not only X, but also Y** | "I not only practiced pronunciation but also improved my fluency." |
| **While X, I also Y** | "While I focused on pronunciation, I also improved my vocabulary." |
| **What I learned most is that...** | "What I learned most is that practice makes permanent." |
| **The most significant improvement was...** | "The most significant improvement was my intonation." |

## ЁЯТб COACH'S NOTES

### ЁЯОУ Teaching Points

**1. Writing + Speaking = Double Practice**
This single task practices writing, pronunciation, fluency, AND synthesis. That's efficient. Make weekly summaries a regular habit.

**2. Your Summary Is Your Record**
Months from now, you'll forget the details of Week 3. Your summary captures itтАФstruggles, wins, insights. This is valuable documentation of your journey.

**3. Recording Creates Objective Evidence**
You might FEEL like you didn't improve. But comparing Week 1, Week 2, and Week 3 summaries shows real progress. The recordings don't lie.

### ЁЯФз Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Writing in Chinese, translating** | Think in English from the start. No Chinese allowed. |
| **Being too brief** | 5+ sentences minimum. Include specific data. |
| **Not recording** | The recording is HALF the value. Don't skip it. |
| **Not applying Week 3 skills** | This is your chance to USE weak forms, stress, intonation. Apply them! |
| **Being dishonest** | Real learning includes struggles. Don't pretend it was all perfect. |

### ЁЯОп Pro Tips

1. **Be specific**: "I improved" vs. "I reduced fillers by 78%"
2. **Include data**: Numbers, scores, time measurements
3. **Be honest**: Real challenges make the summary authentic
4. **Save everything**: Written + audio versions
5. **Review monthly**: Compare Week 1, 2, 3, 4 to see big picture
