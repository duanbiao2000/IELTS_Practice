## 🎯 GUIDED PRACTICE

### Task Overview

Shadow a BBC news segment or similar content at normal speed (1.0x) for 3-5 minutes.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Choose Your BBC Content [3 min]

**Options:**
- **BBC Learning English** (6 Minute English - easier)
- **BBC News** (harder, more academic)
- **BBC World Service** (international topics)
- **BBC documentaries** (varied topics)

**Choose:**
- Length: 3-5 minutes
- Topic: Something that interests you
- Speed: Normal (1.0x)

**Good starting points:**
- BBC Learning English "6 Minute English"
- BBC News "World News" (shorter segments)
- BBC "The English We Speak"

#### STEP 2: First Listen - Understand [2 min]

1. Play the content at normal speed
2. Just listen—don't speak yet
3. Focus on understanding:
   - What's the main topic?
   - What are key words?
   - What's the speaker's tone?

#### STEP 3: Second Listen - Notice Patterns [2 min]

1. Play again at normal speed
2. Notice:
   - Where does the speaker pause?
   - Which words are stressed?
   - How does intonation rise and fall?
   - What's the speaking rhythm?

#### STEP 4: Shadow at Normal Speed [5 min]

1. Start the audio at 1.0x speed
2. Begin shadowing
3. Focus on:
   - **Keeping the rhythm**, not every word
   - **Matching the stress** on key words
   - **Following the intonation** (up/down patterns)
   - **Continuing even when you miss words**

4. Repeat 2-3 times at normal speed

#### STEP 5: Shadowing with Recording [4 min]

1. Start your voice recorder
2. Play the audio at 1.0x
3. Shadow for 60-90 seconds
4. Stop recording

#### STEP 6: Review and Compare [3 min]

1. Listen to your recording
2. Compare with original:
   - Where did you keep up well?
   - Where did you fall behind?
   - How was your intonation?
   - What improved from previous days?

### BBC Shadowing Focus Areas

| Focus Area | What to Listen For | How to Shadow |
|------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Stress** | Key words emphasized | Stress CONTENT words (nouns, verbs, adjectives) |
| **Intonation** | Pitch movement | Match the rises and falls |
| **Pausing** | Breath points | Pause where the speaker pauses |
| **Linking** | Word connections | Link words within phrases |
| **Rhythm** | The beat | Match the speaker's timing |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why BBC Is Harder Than TED-Ed**

| Factor | BBC | TED-Ed |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Speaking rate** | Faster (~160-180 wpm) | Moderate (~140-150 wpm) |
| **Vocabulary** | News, academic, formal | Educational, explained |
| **Sentence complexity** | Long, multiple clauses | Medium, broken down |
| **Background knowledge** | Assumes world knowledge | Explains concepts |

**This is GOOD training for IELTS!**

**2. Shadowing Strategy for Fast Speech**

**Don't try to catch every word!** Instead:
- Focus on **content words** (nouns, main verbs, adjectives)
- Let **function words** go (the, a, of, to, etc.)
- Maintain the **rhythm** more than the words
- If you fall behind, **jump back in**—don't stop!

**3. The 70% Rule**

Aim for 70% of words, not 100%:
- 100% = impossible and discouraging
- 70% = challenging but doable
- You'll naturally improve with practice

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | What Happens | Coach's Fix |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| **Trying to say every word** | Can't keep up, frustrated | Focus on key words only |
| **Stopping when you miss** | Freezes up, loses rhythm | Keep going! Rhythm > accuracy |
| **Choosing too-long segments** | 10+ minutes of struggle | Start with 3-5 minutes |
| **News is too fast** | Can't shadow at all | Start with BBC Learning English (slower) |
| **Getting frustrated** | Giving up on shadowing | It's SUPPOSED to be hard! That's why it helps |
| **Not understanding content** | Shadowing without meaning | Read news summary first, then shadow |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Start with BBC Learning English**—it's designed for learners
2. **Read the news first**—understand the content, then shadow
3. **Shorter is better**—3 minutes of focused shadowing > 10 minutes of struggle
4. **Same content twice**—shadow, then shadow again (improves the second time)
5. **Progress tracking**—record yourself weekly to hear improvement
