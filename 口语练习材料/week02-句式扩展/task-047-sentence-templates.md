# Task 47: Create 15 Reusable Sentence Templates

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [15 minutes]

### Before You Begin

**What Are Sentence Templates?**

Sentence templates are **fill-in-the-blank patterns** you can use in ANY IELTS topic:

**Template Example:**
> "One thing **which has significantly changed my life is** [blank], **because it** [blank]."

**Applied to different topics:**
- "...**which has significantly changed my life is** my smartphone, **because it helps me** stay connected."
- "...**which has significantly changed my life is** learning English, **because it has opened** many opportunities."
- "...**which has significantly changed my life is** moving to this city, **because it has given me** independence."

### Why Create Templates?

| Benefit | How It Helps |
|---------|--------------|
| **Instant sophistication** | No thinking required—the structure is already Band 7+ |
| **Reduces pressure** | Fill in the blanks, don't create from scratch |
| **Builds confidence** | You know you have "good" sentences ready |
| **Saves thinking time** | Focus on content, not grammar |
| **Works for any topic** | Universal patterns adapt to everything |

### Key Concepts

**Week 2 Patterns = Perfect Templates**

From Week 2, you've learned:
1. **Relative clauses**: "...which/that/who + verb..."
2. **Conditionals**: "If I had..., I would..."
3. **Contrast patterns**: "While some..., others..."
4. **Modals**: "tend to / be likely to / be inclined to"

**Today: Turn these into 15 ready-to-use templates!**

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### 15 Ready-to-Use Templates

**RELATIVE CLAUSES (5):**
1. "One thing **which has significantly influenced me** is [blank], **because it has taught me** [blank]."
2. "The [topic] **which I'm most passionate about** is [blank], **which is why I** [blank]."
3. "My friend **who has always supported me** is [name], **who encourages me to** [blank]."
4. "The city **where I grew up** is [name], **which is known for** [blank]."
5. "A skill **that I would like to develop** is [blank], **which would help me to** [blank]."

**CONDITIONALS (4):**
6. "**If I had the opportunity to** [blank], I **would definitely** [blank]."
7. "**If I could make one change to** [blank], I **would** [blank]."
8. "**If I could choose any career**, I **would be a** [blank], **because** [blank]."
9. "**If I were in charge of** [blank], I **would** [blank]."

**CONTRAST (3):**
10. "**While some people prefer** [blank], **I tend to enjoy** [blank] **more because** [blank]."
11. "**On one hand**, [blank] **has advantages** such as [blank]. **On the other hand**, there are **disadvantages** like [blank]."
12. "**Although** [blank] **can be challenging**, I believe **that** [blank]."

**MODALS (3):**
13. "People **tend to** [blank] **when they** [blank], **which is why** [blank]."
14. "It **is likely to** [blank] **in the future**, **which means that** [blank]."
15. "I **am inclined to believe that** [blank], **especially since** [blank]."

| Template | Applied to Technology | Applied to Education |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Templates Work for IELTS**

**Under pressure:**
- Your brain focuses on content (WHAT to say)
- Templates handle structure (HOW to say it)
- Result: Sophisticated grammar without thinking

**Example without template:**
> "I like smartphones. They are useful. I use them every day."

**Example with template:**
> "One thing **which has significantly changed my life is** my smartphone, **because it allows me to** stay connected with friends and access information instantly."

**Which scores higher? Obviously the template version!**

**2. How to Memorize Templates**

**Not by rote memorization!** Instead:
- **Use them repeatedly** in practice
- **Apply to different topics** (see how they adapt)
- **Write them down** on a note card
- **Review before speaking practice**
- **They'll become automatic** with use

**3. Customizing Templates**

Make templates YOURS:
- Adjust to your natural speaking style
- Remove overly formal phrases
- Add words you actually use
- Keep the structure, personalize the content

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | What It Looks Like | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Too many templates** | Trying to memorize 50+ patterns | 15 is plenty! Quality over quantity |
| **Templates too formal** | "It is my inclination to believe..." | Adjust to natural speech: "I'm inclined to think..." |
| **Forgetting to use them** | Memorized but never used | Practice using 1 template per day |
| **Using same template repeatedly** | Every answer starts with "While some..." | Mix it up! Use all 15 templates |
| **Templates don't fit topic** | Forcing awkward template use | Templates are flexible—adjust as needed |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Start with 5**—master these before adding more
2. **Write them on a card**—keep it visible during practice
3. **Use one daily**—pick a template, use it in 3 different topics
4. **Record yourself**—notice when you use templates automatically
5. **Update as needed**—remove templates you don't use, add new ones
