# Task 39: ELSA - Word Stress Position Practice

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [10 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why is word stress so crucial?**

Word stress is one of the **most important pronunciation features** in English:
- **Incorrect stress = unclear meaning**
- Some words change meaning with stress (REcord vs. reCORD)
- Multi-syllable words are common in academic English (IELTS!)
- Stress errors can make you hard to understand

### Key Concepts

**What is Word Stress?**

Word stress means emphasizing **one syllable more than the others** in a word.

**Examples:**
```
PHOtograph   (stress on 1st syllable)
phoTOgrapher (stress on 2nd syllable)
photographIC (stress on 3rd syllable)
```

**Why English Word Stress is Tricky:**
1. No consistent rules (unlike some languages)
2. Stress can change with word form (record vs. record)
3. Many multi-syllable words in academic English
4. Learners often stress every syllable equally

**Common Stress Patterns:**

| Pattern | Examples | Stress Position |
|---------|----------|-----------------|
| 2-syllable nouns | PHOtograph, TAble, MUsic | Usually 1st syllable |
| 2-syllable verbs | reCORD, beGIN, proDUCE | Usually 2nd syllable |
| 3-syllable | imPORtant, ecoNOmic, eduCAtion | Varies—must learn! |

| Word Type | General Pattern | Examples |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| **2-syllable nouns** | Stress 1st syllable | TAble, MUsic, PEOple, WAter |
| **2-syllable verbs** | Stress 2nd syllable | beGIN, aRRIVE, preDICT |

| **Words ending in -gy** | Stress before -gy | techNOlogy, phoTOgraphy, bioloGY |
| **Compound nouns** | Stress first part | BOOKstore, TAXI driver, BEDroom |

### Stress Changes Meaning

| Word | Noun (stress 1st) | Verb (stress 2nd) |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **record** | RE-cord (a recording) | re-CORD (to record) |
| **present** | PRE-sent (a gift) | pre-SENT (to show) |
| **project** | PRO-ject (a task) | pro-JECT (to display) |
| **conduct** | CON-duct (behavior) | con-DUCT (to lead) |
| **object** | OB-ject (a thing) | ob-JECT (to disagree) |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. How to Hear Word Stress**

Native speakers show stress on syllables by:
- **Louder volume** (the stressed syllable is pronounced more loudly)
- **Longer duration** (the stressed syllable is held longer)
- **Higher pitch** (the stressed syllable often has a higher pitch)
- **Full vowel sound** (unstressed syllables often reduce to /ə/ "uh")

**2. The Schwa (/ə/) Secret**

Unstressed syllables often become a "schwa" sound (like "uh"):
- "about" = /əˈbaʊt/ (uh-BOUT)
- "supply" = /səˈplaɪ/ (suh-PLY)
- "important" = /ɪmˈpɔːrtənt/ (im-POR-tuhnt)

**This is why unstressed syllables sound "weird" to learners—they're reduced!**

**3. Why Word Stress Matters for IELTS**

- Academic English uses many multi-syllable words
- Incorrect stress can confuse listeners
- Stress affects your overall rhythm
- Band 7+ pronunciation includes "consistent" stress patterns

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Example | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Equal stress on all syllables** | "IM-POR-TANT" (all equal) | Pick ONE syllable to stress |
| **Stressing the wrong syllable** | "IM-portant" instead of "im-POR-tant" | Learn patterns, don't guess |
| **Not hearing the stress** | Can't tell which syllable is stressed | Listen to native speech, tap on stressed syllables |
| **Forgetting stress in sentences** | Correct alone, wrong in sentences | Practice words IN sentences |
| **Same word, different stress** | Not knowing noun vs. verb stress | Record and REcord mean different things! |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Tap it out**—tap your finger on the stressed syllable while speaking
2. **Exaggerate first**—over-pronounce the stress, then naturalize
3. **Learn word families**—phoTOgraph → phoTOgrapher → photographIC
4. **Use dictionary apps**—check stress markings for new words
5. **Record and compare**—your stress vs. native speaker
