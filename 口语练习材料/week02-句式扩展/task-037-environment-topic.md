# Task 37: Environment Topic Using Contrast Patterns

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [12 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why is the Environment topic so important for IELTS?**

Environment is a **high-frequency topic** that appears in all three parts:
- **Part 1**: Recycling, pollution, environmental habits
- **Part 2**: Describe a place in nature, an environmental problem
- **Part 3**: Climate change, government responsibility, individual vs. collective action

**Environment + Contrast Patterns = Perfect Match:**
Environmental issues naturally involve different perspectives:
- Economic growth vs. environmental protection
- Individual responsibility vs. government action
- Present convenience vs. future consequences
- Developed vs. developing nations' responsibilities

### Key Concepts

**Environment Topic Vocabulary:**

| Category | Words & Phrases |
|----------|----------------|
| **Problems** | pollution, global warming, climate change, deforestation, waste, carbon emissions, environmental damage |
| **Solutions** | renewable energy, recycling, sustainable, conservation, reduce carbon footprint, eco-friendly |
| **Actions** | protect, preserve, conserve, reduce, reuse, recycle, minimize impact |
| **Feelings** | concerned, worried, hopeful, responsible, aware, motivated |

**Contrast Patterns for Environment:**

| Pattern | Environment Example |
|---------|---------------------|
| **While some... others...** | "While some prioritize economic growth, others focus on environmental protection." |
| **On one hand... on the other hand** | "On one hand, plastic is convenient. On the other hand, it causes pollution." |
| **Although** | "Although renewable energy is expensive initially, it saves money long-term." |
| **In contrast** | "Developed nations produce more emissions. In contrast, developing nations suffer more consequences." |

### Task Overview
Complete a 1-minute recording on an environmental topic, using at least one contrast pattern.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Choose Your Environmental Question [2 min]

Select one of these questions:
1. "Who is more responsible for protecting the environment: individuals or governments?"
2. "Should we prioritize economic development or environmental protection?"
3. "What is the biggest environmental problem in your country?"
4. "Do you think people are more environmentally aware now than in the past?"

#### STEP 3: Practice with Sentence Patterns [3 min]

**Pattern 1: Individual vs. Government**
> "**While some people argue** that individuals should take more responsibility for recycling, **others believe** that government policies are more effective."

**Pattern 2: Economy vs. Environment**
> "**On one hand**, economic development creates jobs and improves living standards. **On the other hand**, unchecked growth can cause irreversible environmental damage."

**Pattern 3: Present vs. Future**
> "**Although** immediate action might require sacrifices now, I believe **that future generations will benefit** from our environmental efforts."

**Pattern 4: Developed vs. Developing**
> "**While developed nations** often have the resources to invest in green technology, **developing countries** may struggle to balance environmental protection with economic growth."

#### STEP 4: Speak Through Your Framework [3 min]

Practice your complete response:
- Side A (use "While some..." or similar)
- Side B (use "others..." or "on the other hand")
- Your view
- Add specific example

#### STEP 5: Record for 1 Minute [2 min]
- Start recording
- Speak continuously for 60 seconds
- Include at least one contrast pattern

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample 1-Minute Response

**Question:** "Who is more responsible for protecting the environment: individuals or governments?"

> "**While some people argue** that governments should take primary responsibility for environmental protection through policies and regulations, **others believe that** individual actions are equally important.
>
> **On one hand**, governments have the power to implement large-scale changes like investing in renewable energy, setting emission limits for companies, and creating national recycling programs. These actions can have a significant impact on reducing pollution and conserving resources.
>
> **On the other hand**, individual choices matter too. **For example**, when millions of people reduce their plastic use, conserve water, or choose public transport, the collective effect can be substantial. **Although** one person's actions might seem small, many people acting together can create real change.
>
> Personally, I think both sides have a role to play. Governments need to create systems that make environmentally friendly choices easier, while individuals need to take responsibility for their daily decisions."

### Response Breakdown

| Section | Content | Pattern Used |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Introduction | Two perspectives identified | While some... others... |
| Side A | Government responsibility | On one hand... |
| Side B | Individual responsibility | On the other hand... |
| Example | Specific instance | For example / Although |
| Conclusion | Balanced view | Personally, I think... |

### Common Environment Questions & Contrast Answers

| Question | Sample Answer with Contrast |
|----------|----------------------------|
| "Should we focus on economy or environment?" | "**While economic growth is important for** improving people's lives, **environmental protection is essential for** long-term survival." |
| "Are people more aware now?" | "**Although awareness has increased** significantly in recent years, **many people still prioritize** convenience over sustainability." |
| "Is nuclear energy good or bad?" | "**On one hand**, nuclear energy produces low emissions. **On the other hand**, concerns about safety and waste disposal remain." |
| "Should developed countries help developing ones?" | "**While developed nations** have historically contributed more to climate change, **developing countries often face** the worst consequences. **In my view**, international cooperation is essential." |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Environment + Contrast Works So Well**

Environmental topics are **inherently balanced**:
- No single "right" answer
- Multiple valid perspectives
- Complexity and nuance expected
- Perfect for demonstrating sophisticated thinking

**2. Part 3 Environment Questions Usually Ask For:**
- Evaluation (Should X do Y?)
- Comparison (Individual vs. government, now vs. past)
- Speculation (What will happen if...?)
- Solutions (How can we solve...?)

**Contrast patterns handle ALL of these naturally!**

**3. Building Environment Vocabulary**

| Foundation Level | Band 7+ Level |
|------------------|---------------|
| "bad for nature" | "environmentally damaging" |
| "good for earth" | "sustainable / eco-friendly" |
| "stop pollution" | "reduce emissions / minimize impact" |
| "save energy" | "conserve resources" |
| "nature problems" | "environmental challenges" |

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Example | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **One-sided answer** | Only discussing individual action | Always present at least two perspectives |
| **Vocabulary too simple** | "pollution is bad" | Use: "environmental damage," "harmful emissions," "negative impact" |
| **No specific examples** | General statements only | Add: "For example, solar energy..." or "Plastic waste in oceans..." |
| **Forgetting contrast pattern** | Answering without "While... others..." | Plan your contrast before starting |
| **Being too negative** | Everything is terrible/problems | Balance: problems + solutions + hope |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Prepare 3 environment stories** (recycling, public transport, energy saving)
2. **Memorize 5 contrast patterns** for environment topics
3. **Stay balanced**—examiners like nuanced thinking
4. **Use real examples** from your country when possible
5. **End with your view**—after showing both sides, state your opinion
