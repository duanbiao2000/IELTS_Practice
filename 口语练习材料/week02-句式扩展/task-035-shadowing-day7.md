## 🎯 GUIDED PRACTICE

### Task Overview
Shadow a TED-Ed or similar video at normal speed (1.0x) while recording yourself.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Choose Your Content [3 min]

Select a 3-5 minute segment:
- TED-Ed video (continue from Day 6 or choose new)
- OR: 6 Minute English podcast
- OR: Clear speech content you enjoy

**Requirements:**
- Clear, articulate speaker
- Interesting content (helps you focus)
- Transcript available (helpful but not required)

#### STEP 2: Warm-Up Listen [2 min]

1. Listen to your chosen segment at normal speed
2. Just listen—don't speak yet
3. Notice the speaker's:
   - Overall speed
   - Pausing patterns
   - Emotional tone
   - Key emphases

#### STEP 3: Practice Shadowing (No Recording) [4 min]

1. Start the audio at 1.0x speed
2. Shadow alongside the speaker
3. Focus on:
   - Keeping up with the pace
   - Matching the rhythm
   - Not stopping when you miss words
4. Repeat 2 times

#### STEP 4: Shadowing with Recording [5 min]

**Setup:**
- Open your voice recorder
- Start recording
- Play the audio
- Begin shadowing

**While recording:**
- Don't stop if you miss words—keep going!
- Focus on flow over perfection
- Match the speaker's energy
- Stay with the audio even when it's hard

#### STEP 5: Listen and Compare [4 min]

1. Listen to your recording
2. Compare with original:
   - Where did you keep up well?
   - Where did you fall behind?
   - What sounds better than last week?
   - What still needs work?

#### STEP 6: One More Take [4 min]

Record yourself shadowing the same segment one more time.
Notice: Did you improve after hearing your first attempt?

### Common Shadowing Challenges at Normal Speed

| Challenge | Why It Happens | Strategy |
|-----------|----------------|----------|
| **Can't keep up** | Processing overload | It's normal—focus on key words |
| **Miss the beginning of sentences** | Delay in starting | Start immediately with the speaker |
| **Lose focus mid-way** | Mental fatigue | Shorter segments (30-60 seconds) |
| **Sound monotone** | Too focused on words | Focus on emotional tone |
| **Pronunciation suffers** | Speed over accuracy | Some sacrifice is normal at 1.0x |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Recording Matters**

Recording yourself while shadowing:
- **Creates accountability**: You can't fake it
- **Builds stamina**: You learn to keep going
- **Documents progress**: Compare Week 1 to Week 4
- **Trains recovery**: Mistakes are part of the process

**2. The "Keep Going" Rule**

When shadowing at normal speed:
- You WILL miss words—that's expected
- The goal is NOT 100% word accuracy
- The goal IS maintaining rhythm and flow
- **Never stop**—keep shadowing even when lost

**3. What Improves with Practice**

After a week of shadowing, you should notice:
- Faster reaction time (start quicker)
- Better anticipation (guess what's coming)
- Smoother rhythm (less choppy)
- More confidence (less hesitation)
- Better intonation (more natural ups and downs)

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Example | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Stopping when you miss** | Freezing on a difficult word | Keep going! Rhythm > accuracy |
| **Choosing content that's too fast** | Political debates, rapid speech | Stick to educational content |
| **Recording too long** | 10+ minutes of struggle | Keep to 3-5 minutes |
| **Not listening to your recording** | Recording but never reviewing | Always listen—even if it's uncomfortable |
| **Comparing unfairly** | Your recording vs. professional actor | Compare to YOUR previous recordings! |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Shorter is better**—3 minutes of focused shadowing > 10 minutes of struggling
2. **Same content twice**—record, listen, then record again
3. **Accept imperfection**—even native speakers wouldn't shadow perfectly
4. **Mark your calendar**—shadow 3-4 times per week minimum
5. **Save your recordings**—they're progress documents
