# Task 48: Friends Topic Recording (2 Minutes Continuous)

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin

**The Friends Topic**

"Friends" is one of the **most common IELTS topics**:
- **Part 1**: Questions about your friends, friendship, socializing
- **Part 2**: Describe a friend, a time with friends, a friend who helped you
- **Part 3**: Friendship in modern society, online vs. offline friends

**This Task Tests:**
- Your ability to speak continuously for 2 minutes
- Your use of Week 2 patterns (templates from Task 47!)
- Your fluency with a familiar, comfortable topic
- Your automatic use of complex structures

### Key Concepts

**Friends Topic Vocabulary:**

| Category | Words & Phrases |
|----------|----------------|
| **Describing friends** | supportive, trustworthy, understanding, loyal, fun to be with |
| **Activities** | hang out, spend time together, catch up, go out, stay in touch |
| **Friendship qualities** | reliable, honest, good listener, shares similar interests |
| **Meeting friends** | childhood friend, university friend, work colleague, online friend |
| **Maintaining friendship** | keep in touch, stay connected, reach out, make time |

**2-Minute Structure for Friends:**

| Time | Content |
|------|---------|
| 0:00-0:20 | Introduce your friend(s) |
| 0:20-0:50 | How you met, background |
| 0:50-1:30 | What you do together, why you're friends |
| 1:30-2:00 | Why this friendship matters, conclusion |

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample 2-Minute Response

> "**I'd like to talk about my best friend**, whose name is Sarah. We've known each other for over ten years now, **since we first met in** high school.
>
> **We became friends** when we were assigned to the same project in our chemistry class. **What I noticed immediately about** Sarah was **how supportive and encouraging she was**, even though we had just met. She helped me with the difficult chemistry concepts, and we ended up getting an excellent grade on that project.
>
> **Since then, we've done many things together**. We study together, go to movies, and enjoy trying new restaurants in our city. **What I appreciate most about** our friendship is **that we can talk about anything**—from serious problems to silly jokes. She's **the kind of friend who always listens** and gives honest advice, **which I really value**.
>
> **Although we don't see each other every day** now because we attend different universities, we **stay in touch through** video calls and messaging. **One thing which has significantly strengthened our friendship** is the fact **that we make time for each other** despite our busy schedules. **I tend to believe that** true friendship isn't about how often you meet, but about the quality of the connection **you share with someone**.
>
> **Looking back**, I can say **that meeting Sarah was one of the best things** that happened to me in high school. **She's not just a friend**, but more like family, **and I'm grateful to have** her in my life."

### Response Analysis

| Time | Content | Templates Used |
|------|---------|----------------|
| 0:00-0:20 | Introduction (who is Sarah) | None yet |
| 0:20-0:50 | How we met, first impressions | What I noticed about... |
| 0:50-1:30 | What we do together, her qualities | who always listens... / which I really value |
| 1:30-2:00 | Staying in touch, friendship meaning | Although... / One thing which has... / I tend to believe... |

**Templates Identified:**
1. "What I [notice/appreciate] most about..."
2. "...who [verb]..."
3. "...which I [verb]..."
4. "Although..."
5. "One thing which has..."
6. "I tend to believe that..."

### Common Friends Cue Cards

| Cue Card | Prompt | Template Ideas |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| **Describe a friend** | Talk about your best friend | "My friend **who has influenced me** is..." / "One thing **which I appreciate about**..." |
| **Describe a time with friends** | A memorable experience with friends | "**Although** it was challenging..." / "It **is likely to be** a memory **that**..." |
| **Describe a friend who helped you** | How a friend supported you | "My friend **who helped me** is..." / "**If I hadn't met** her, I **would have**..." |
| **Describe your ideal friend** | qualities you want in a friend | "I **tend to prefer** friends **who**..." / "**While some** people like..., **I prefer**..." |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Friends Is an "Easy" Topic**

Friends topics are comfortable because:
- Everyone has friends (or can talk about wanting friends)
- Personal experiences are easy to remember
- Emotional connection = natural to talk about
- Vocabulary is familiar and everyday

**This makes it PERFECT for testing your 2-minute fluency!**

**2. Using Templates Naturally**

**Bad template use:**
> "I would like to describe my friend. **One thing which has significantly changed my life is** my friend. **Because she is nice**."

Sounds forced and awkward!

**Good template use:**
> "My best friend is Sarah. **One thing which has significantly changed my life** is **knowing her**, **because she has taught me** what true friendship means."

Templates flow naturally when integrated into YOUR ideas.

**3. The 2-Minute Challenge**

**What usually happens:**
- 0:00-0:30: Plenty to say
- 0:30-1:00: Still comfortable
- 1:00-1:30: Starting to think "what else?"
- 1:30-2:00: Struggling to fill time

**Solution:**
- Describe specific memories
- Explain WHY she's your friend (multiple reasons)
- Compare her to other friends
- Talk about how you maintain the friendship
- Share what you've learned from friendship

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | What It Looks Like | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Running out of things to say** | Finish at 90 seconds | Add specific stories, describe memories |
| **Forgetting to use templates** | No Week 2 patterns used | Plan 2-3 templates BEFORE starting |
| **Too much introduction** | 60 seconds on "I'd like to talk about..." | Keep intro to 15-20 seconds |
| **Vague descriptions** | "She's nice. We have fun." | WHAT makes her nice? WHAT do you do together? |
| **Forgetting the feeling** | Just describing facts, not emotions | Why does this friendship matter? |
| **No specific details** | Generic statements | Names, places, dates, specific memories |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Have 3 friend stories ready**—best friend, childhood friend, friend who helped you
2. **Use names and details**—"Sarah" is better than "my friend"
3. **Describe specific memories**—"when we went to the beach last summer"
4. **Explain the "why"**—not just WHAT you do, but WHY you're friends
5. **Templates as bridges**—use them when you don't know what to say next
