# Task 31: Modal Patterns - tend to / be likely to / be inclined to

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [12 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why are these modal patterns powerful?**

These three patterns signal **Band 7+ sophistication**:
- They show you can express **tendency and probability**
- They add **nuance** to your statements
- They sound more **academic and formal**
- They're perfect for **Part 3** abstract questions

### Key Concepts

**The Three Patterns:**

| Pattern | Meaning | Usage Level | Example |
|---------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **tend to** | General tendency, habit | Most common/flexible | "People tend to prefer..." |
| **be likely to** | Probability, expectation | Neutral/factual | "It is likely to become..." |
| **be inclined to** | Personal tendency, preference | Slightly more formal | "I am inclined to think..." |

**When to Use Each:**

**tend to** = General patterns
> "People **tend to** be more active in summer."
> "Students **tend to** procrastinate before exams."

**be likely to** = Future probability
> "Technology **is likely to** replace many jobs."
> "It **is likely to** rain tomorrow."

**be inclined to** = Personal preference/opinion
> "I **am inclined to** agree with this view."
> "I **would be inclined to** choose..." (hypothetical)

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Completed Sentences

**Pattern 1: tend to**
1. "People in my country **tend to** value family traditions, especially during festivals when everyone gathers together."
2. "I **tend to** be more productive in the morning when my mind is fresh and there are fewer distractions."

**Pattern 2: be likely to**
3. "Remote work **is likely to** become more common even after the pandemic, as many companies have realized its benefits."
4. "Students **are likely to** perform better when they receive regular feedback from their teachers."

**Pattern 3: be inclined to**
5. "I **am inclined to** believe that practical experience is just as important as theoretical knowledge."
6. "I **would be inclined to** choose a career that allows me to help others, even if the salary isn't the highest."

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. How These Patterns Boost Your Score**

Examiners listen for **"lexical resource"** (vocabulary range):
- Saying "usually" repeatedly = limited range (Band 5-6)
- Using "tend to, be likely to, be inclined to" = flexible, precise (Band 7+)

**2. Subtle Differences**

| Pattern | Emphasis | Best For |
|---------|----------|----------|
| **tend to** | Habit, general truth | Describing what people/usually do |
| **be likely to** | Probability, prediction | Discussing future, expectations |
| **be inclined to** | Personal preference, tendency | Expressing opinions, choices |

**3. Pronunciation Matters**

- tend TO - stress on "to"
- LIKE-ly to - primary stress on "like"
- in-CLINED to - primary stress on "clined"

This stress pattern is important for natural rhythm!

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Example | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Wrong form after "tends"** | "He tend to..." | "He **tends** to..." (3rd person singular) |
| **Forgetting "to"** | "People likely prefer..." | "People **are likely to** prefer..." |
| **Using "inclining"** | "I am inclining to think..." | Use "**inclined**" (adjective), not "inclining" |
| **Overusing one pattern** | All sentences with "tend to" | Mix all three for variety |
| **Using in wrong context** | "I am likely to agree" (uncertain) | "I **am inclined to** agree" (opinion) |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Start with "tend to"**—it's the most versatile
2. **Use "be likely to" for Part 3** predictions and trends
3. **Use "be inclined to" for opinions**—shows nuanced thinking
4. **Practice contractions**: "I'd be inclined to..." (more natural)
5. **Memorize 2-3 go-to phrases** for each pattern
