# Material Extraction Standards

## Material Sourcing Priority (P1→P2→P3)

**P1 (Highest Priority):** Official IELTS Cambridge Test Bank materials, authentic past papers, and verified practice tests from recognized IELTS preparation providers.

**P2 (Medium Priority):** High-quality educational content from reputable publishers, academic textbooks, and established language learning resources with proven effectiveness.

**P3 (Lowest Priority):** General reading materials, supplementary resources, and content that requires significant adaptation or generation.

## Extraction Workflow

1. **Identify Need**: Determine the specific IELTS module (Reading/Writing/Grammar) and task type required for practice materials.

2. **Search Ebooks**: Locate relevant ebooks (.epub/.pdf) in the reference library that contain appropriate content matching the task requirements.

3. **Extract Content**: Convert ebook content to markdown format using automated conversion tools, focusing on relevant sections only.

4. **Generate If Needed**: For gaps in extracted content, use lightweight generation to create supplementary materials that maintain authenticity and educational value.

5. **Validate Amount**: Ensure extracted content meets the 8% time limit constraint and provides appropriate difficulty level and length for the intended practice task.

6. **Record**: Document the source, extraction method, and any modifications made to the original content for future reference and quality control.

## Time Limits

- **Extraction Time**: Must complete material extraction in under 2 minutes per task
- **Task Time Allocation**: Material extraction should not exceed 8% of the total task time
- **Processing Constraint**: Ebook conversion and content extraction must be optimized for speed without compromising quality

# Academic Reading: Environmental Science

## Passage: Climate Change Impacts

Climate change represents one of the most significant environmental challenges of the 21st century. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) reports indicate that global temperatures have risen by 1.1°C since pre-industrial times, with current trends suggesting further increases.

### Key Findings:
- Sea level rise of 3.3mm per year globally
- Increased frequency of extreme weather events
- Shifts in precipitation patterns affecting agriculture
- Ocean acidification impacting marine ecosystems

### Questions:
1. According to the passage, what is the primary cause of the observed temperature increase?
2. How have marine ecosystems been affected by climate change?
3. What measures does the IPCC recommend to mitigate future impacts?

## Grammar Task Examples

### Grammar Focus: Sentence Structure
```markdown
# Grammar Practice: Complex Sentences

## Exercise: Identify and Correct Errors

### Example 1:
*Original:* "Although the project was completed on time, however, the budget was exceeded."
*Correction:* "Although the project was completed on time, the budget was exceeded."

### Example 2:
*Original:* "Students who study regularly tend to achieve better results, this is supported by research."
*Correction:* "Students who study regularly tend to achieve better results, which is supported by research."

### Example 3:
*Original:* "The company implemented new policies, consequently, employee satisfaction increased."
*Correction:* "The company implemented new policies; consequently, employee satisfaction increased."
```

# Grammar Practice: Verb Tenses

1. By the time we arrive, the presentation __________ (already/start).
   *Options:* will have already started / will start / starts

2. If I __________ (know) the answer, I would tell you.
   *Options:* knew / know / will know

3. She __________ (work) in this company for five years by next month.
   *Options:* will have worked / has worked / will work

## Guardrails and Constraints

### Quality Standards
- **Authenticity**: Materials must be derived from genuine sources, not artificially generated content
- **Relevance**: Content must directly align with IELTS test requirements and format
- **Difficulty**: Materials should match the appropriate band level (5.0-9.0)
- **Length**: Adhere to standard IELTS task length requirements

### Ethical Constraints
- **No External Generation**: Avoid using external AI tools for content creation
- **Source Attribution**: Maintain clear documentation of all material sources
- **Copyright Compliance**: Ensure all extracted materials are used within fair use guidelines
- **Quality Control**: Implement verification processes for extracted content accuracy

### Technical Constraints
- **File Format**: Support .epub and .pdf conversion to .md
- **Extraction Speed**: Complete within 2-minute time limit per task
- **Resource Efficiency**: Optimize processing to minimize computational load
- **Storage Management**: Maintain organized storage of extracted materials
