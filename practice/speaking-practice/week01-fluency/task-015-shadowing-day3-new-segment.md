# Task 15: Shadowing Day 3 - New Segment

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin

**Why Switch to a New Segment?**

By Day 3, you've practiced the same segment twice. Moving to a new segment:
- **Prevents memorization** - you stay focused on the sound, not the content
- **Broadens exposure** - different speakers, topics, rhythms
- **Tests your skills** - can you apply rhythm/intonation to fresh material?
- **Builds versatility** - adaptability is key for IELTS

**What You've Practiced So Far:**
- Day 1: Rhythm focus
- Day 2: Intonation focus
- Day 3: Full application + new material

### Key Concepts

| Element | What You've Learned | Today's Focus |
|---------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Rhythm** | Stress-timed English flow | Apply to new material |
| **Intonation** | Pitch changes, expression | Copy new speaker's patterns |
| **Listening** | Active, focused listening | New topic, new speaker |
| **Mimicry** | Echo technique | Immediate imitation |

## 🎯 GUIDED PRACTICE

### Task Overview
Select a NEW 60-second segment from a BBC 6 Minute English episode. Apply all shadowing skills: rhythm, intonation, and flow.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Choose a New Segment [3 min]

Go back to BBC 6 Minute English (or choose a new episode).
Select a different 60-second segment:
- Different episode OR
- Different part of the same episode

**New segment:** Episode ______, _____:_____ to _____:_____

#### STEP 2: First Listen - No Speaking [1 min]

Listen to your new segment once without speaking.
- Notice the speaker(s)
- Notice the topic
- Notice the rhythm and intonation

**Initial impressions:**
- Number of speakers: ______
- Topic: _________________________________________________
- Pace: Fast / Medium / Slow

#### STEP 3: Second Listen - Rhythm Focus [2 min]

Listen again. Focus ONLY on rhythm:
- Tap along to the beat
- Notice stressed words
- Feel the flow

#### STEP 4: Shadowing Round 1 - Copy Rhythm [4 min]

Shadow the segment, focusing on:
- Matching the speed
- Stressing the right words
- Keeping the flow
- Not stopping for mistakes

**Round 1 Reflection:**
Easier / Harder than Day 1? _____________________________

#### STEP 5: Shadowing Round 2 - Copy Intonation [4 min]

Shadow again, this time focusing on:
- Pitch rises ↗
- Pitch falls ↘
- Emotional expression
- Question vs. statement patterns

**Round 2 Reflection:**
This speaker's intonation is: _____________________________

#### STEP 6: Shadowing Round 3 - Full Application [4 min]

Final round. Combine everything:
- Rhythm ✓
- Intonation ✓
- Flow ✓
- Natural delivery ✓

**Round 3 Reflection:**
I feel my shadowing has: _____________________________

### What to Notice in New Segments

| Element | What to Listen For | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Speaker differences** | Voice, speed, style | Adapting to different speakers |
| **Topic vocabulary** | New words in context | Natural word usage |
| **Discussion patterns** | How speakers interact | Turn-taking in conversation |
| **Emotional content** | Excitement, agreement, disagreement | Expressive range |

### Shadowing Skill Check

By Day 3, you should be able to:
- [ ] Start speaking within 0.5 seconds of the speaker
- [ ] Match the speaker's speed (not too slow/fast)
- [ ] Identify and copy stressed words
- [ ] Copy question vs. statement intonation
- [ ] Keep going even when you make mistakes
- [ ] Feel the rhythm, not just hear it

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. The "Fresh Material" Challenge**
> Shadowing a practiced segment feels good—but it's easier.
> Shadowing NEW material tests your actual skills.
>
> If you can shadow new material well, you've truly internalized the rhythm and intonation patterns.
> This is real progress.

**2. Building Your Sound Library**
Each BBC segment you shadow is like adding to your mental "sound library":
- Different speakers = different models
- Different topics = different vocabulary contexts
- Different styles = more versatility

**Aim for 10+ different shadowing segments during your IELTS preparation.**

**3. Quality Over Quantity**
Three focused shadowing sessions > ten distracted ones.
Each round should have a specific focus (rhythm, intonation, flow).
This focused practice is what builds skill.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Choosing too fast a segment** | Impossible to keep up | Select moderate pace segments |
| **Not listening first** | Missing the patterns | Always listen before shadowing |
| **Focusing on words, not sound** | Loses rhythm/intonation | Focus on HOW, not WHAT |
| **Giving up on difficult parts** | Weak spots remain | Struggle through, don't skip |
| **Only doing one round** | Insufficient practice | Minimum 3 rounds per session |
| **Not progressing to new material** | Hits plateau, stops improving | Move to new segments regularly |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Vary your speakers** - male/female, fast/slow, different accents
2. **Use video sources** - TED Talks, interviews (see mouth movement)
3. **Record yourself** - compare your shadowing to original
4. **Do it daily** - even 10 minutes maintains momentum
5. **Combine with ELSA** - use ELSA for specific sounds, shadowing for flow
