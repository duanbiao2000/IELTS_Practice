# Task 3: Review and Memorize Your Topic Frameworks

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why memorization matters (even without rote learning)**

Memorizing frameworks doesn't mean sounding robotic. It means **internalizing structure** so your brain can focus on language quality during the test.

**The Difference:**

| Approach | Result |
|----------|--------|
| **Memorizing full answers** | Robot-like, sounds rehearsed, LOW SCORE |
| **Memorizing frameworks** | Natural structure, room for flexibility, HIGH SCORE |

### Key Concepts

| Technique | How It Works | Why It Helps |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Spaced Repetition** | Review at increasing intervals | Moves to long-term memory |
| **Active Recall** | Test yourself, don't just re-read | Strengthens memory pathways |
| **Association** | Link to images/experiences | Multiple memory anchors |
| **Chunking** | Group related items together | Easier to remember |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Memory Works Through Connections**
> Isolated facts are hard to remember. Connected stories are easy.
> Don't just memorize "beaches, relaxed, proud" for Hometown.
> Remember: "My hometown HAS beaches (point 1), I love the relaxed lifestyle (point 2), and I'm proud but want more career opportunities (point 3)."

**2. The "Teach Back" Technique**
Try explaining your framework to an imaginary student. Teaching forces you to organize information clearly—and that's exactly what helps memory.

**3. Less Is More**
If you're struggling to remember all 10, focus on these 5 first:
- Hometown (almost always asked)
- Work/Study (Part 1 staple)
- Technology (universal topic)
- Travel (common Part 2)
- Future Plans (shows ambition)

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Problem | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Rereading instead of recalling** | Passive, creates illusion of knowing | Test yourself FIRST, then check |
| **Cramming all at once** | Forgets quickly | Space out: morning, evening, tomorrow |
| **Treating all points equally** | Harder to remember | Highlight the MOST memorable point |
| **Not saying it aloud** | Memory weaker | Speaking activates more brain areas |
| **Skipping review** | Lost progress | Set phone reminder for weekly review |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Record yourself** saying all frameworks, listen during commute
2. **Use dead time** - waiting in line, elevator, commercial breaks
3. **Pair practice** - test with a friend or use AI (like me!)
4. **Morning review** - brains are freshest, retention higher
5. **Sleep on it** - memory consolidates during sleep
