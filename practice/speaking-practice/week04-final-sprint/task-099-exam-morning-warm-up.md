# Task 99: Exam Morning – 15-Minute Shadowing Warm-Up

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin

**Why a shadowing warm-up on exam morning?**

Just as athletes warm up before competition, you need to:
- **Activate your mouth muscles** – English sounds use different muscles
- **Engage your English brain** – switch from native language mode
- **Establish your rhythm** – set your optimal pace
- **Build confidence** – hear yourself speaking well immediately

### The Morning Warm-Up Effect

| No Warm-Up | With Warm-Up |
|------------|--------------|
| Mouth feels "stiff" | Muscles activated and ready |
| Native language accent | English sounds flowing |
| Slow to start speaking | Immediate English mode |
| Nervousness builds | Confidence from speaking well |

#### STEP 3: Physical Warm-Up Additions [2 min]

**Activate your whole body:**

```
PHYSICAL WARM-UP (can do during shadowing)

□ Jaw stretches (open wide, move side to side)
□ Shoulder rolls (release tension)
□ Deep breathing (3 deep breaths)
□ Face warm-up (exaggerated expressions)
□ Tongue stretches (move it around)

Voice activation:
□ Humming (low to high)
□ Lip trills (if you can)
□ Sirens (low to high and back)
□ "Me me me" at different pitches

Physical state:
□ Posture check (stand/sit straight)
□ Energy check (awake and alert)
□ Nerves check (acknowledge, then breathe)
```

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Warm-Up is Standard Practice**
> Professional speakers, athletes, and performers ALL warm up. You're not "over-practicing" – you're preparing properly.

**2. The 15-Minute Sweet Spot**
15 minutes is enough to activate but not tire. Less than 10 = insufficient activation. More than 20 = mental fatigue.

**3. Morning of the Exam**
Your warm-up should be the ONLY intensive practice on exam morning. Everything else should be light review or rest.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Skipping warm-up (no time)** | Make time. 15 minutes is worth it. Wake up earlier if needed. |
| **Warming up too much** | Stop at 15 minutes. Don't exhaust yourself. |
| **Using unfamiliar material** | Use what you KNOW. This isn't time to learn new things. |
| **Warm-up is too intensive** | Keep it moderate. Save your peak energy for the exam. |
| **Getting nervous during warm-up** | If you sound bad, STOP. Switch to something easy. The goal is confidence. |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Do it in private** – your room, outside, wherever you won't be overheard
2. **Stand up** – better breath support when standing
3. **Use a mirror** – see yourself speaking confidently
4. **Have water nearby** – stay hydrated, voice ready
5. **End on confidence** – make your last phrase your BEST one
