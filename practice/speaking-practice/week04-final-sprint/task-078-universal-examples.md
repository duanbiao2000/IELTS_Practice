# Task 78: Prepare 10 "Universal Examples"

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [10 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why "universal examples"?**

In IELTS Speaking, you can often use the **same story for different topics**. This strategy:
- Reduces content preparation burden
- Ensures you have well-practiced, fluent stories
- Saves mental energy during the exam
- Demonstrates sophisticated language through repeated use

### How One Story Fits Multiple Topics

| Your Universal Story | Can Answer These Topics |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| **Your smartphone experience** | Technology, Communication, Change, Modern life |
| **A friend's achievement** | Success, Admiration, Friendship, Goals |
| **COVID-19 pandemic experience** | Challenge, Change, Global issues, Resilience |
| **Learning a skill online** | Education, Technology, Self-improvement |
| **A family tradition** | Culture, Family, Memories, Celebration |
| **A public place you visit** | Leisure, Environment, Social spaces, Relaxation |

| **A gift you received** | Generosity, Objects, Memories, Relationships |
| **A journey you took** | Travel, Experiences, Adventure, Discovery |
| **A childhood memory** | Past, Nostalgia, Family, Personal history |

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Universal Example: "My Smartphone"

**Core Story:**
"I got my first smartphone when I started university in 2020. It was a basic model, but it completely changed how I communicated with my family who lived in another city. Before, I could only call them once a week. After getting the phone, we could video chat daily, share photos instantly, and stay connected through messaging apps. It made me feel much closer to home despite the distance."

**Topic Adaptations:**

| For This Topic | How to Adapt |
|----------------|--------------|
| **Technology** | Emphasize the device features and how they helped |
| **Communication** | Focus on the relationship aspect and staying connected |
| **Change** | Contrast before/after the phone, highlight the transformation |
| **Modern life** | Discuss how this represents today's interconnected world |
| **Important object** | Explain why this particular phone matters to you |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. The Principle of Content Reuse**
> You don't need 100 different stories for 100 possible topics. You need 10 well-developed stories you can tell fluently and adapt on the spot.

**2. How to Adapt Quickly**
When you get a cue card, ask yourself:
- "Which of my 10 stories fits BEST?"
- "What's the connection between my story and this topic?"
- "Which angle should I emphasize?"

**3. Why This Works for IELTS**
Examiners are listening for:
- **Fluency** – you speak more confidently about familiar content
- **Vocabulary** – repeated use allows sophisticated language
- **Coherence** – you know the story structure by heart

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Stories too specific** | Keep core details broad enough to adapt. Avoid "that ONE time when X happened on Y date at Z place" |
| **Making up stories** | Use YOUR real experiences. Truth comes across more naturally |
| **Forgetting which story fits what** | Create a simple mapping list to reference before exam |
| **Not practicing adaptations** | Having the story isn't enough – practice using it for DIFFERENT topics |
| **Choosing "impressive" stories over personal ones** | Simple stories told well beat "impressive" stories told poorly |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Write your stories in bullet points first**, then practice speaking them
2. **Record yourself telling each story** – you'll catch repetitive phrases
3. **Practice time compression** – can you tell your story in 30 seconds? 2 minutes?
4. **Create a "story index"** – one line per story for quick mental reference
