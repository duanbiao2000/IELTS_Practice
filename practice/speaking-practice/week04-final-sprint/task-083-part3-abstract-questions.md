## ЁЯУЪ PRE-STUDY [10 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why are abstract questions the hardest?**

Part 3 questions move from personal (Part 1) to storytelling (Part 2) to **abstract** (Part 3):
- **Part 1:** "Do you like reading?" (personal, concrete)
- **Part 2:** "Describe a book you enjoyed" (personal story)
- **Part 3:** "Should governments encourage reading?" (abstract, societal)

Abstract questions require:
- **Generalization** тАУ moving from "I" to "people/society"
- **Hypothetical thinking** тАУ discussing what "should" happen
- **Broader vocabulary** тАУ words about society, systems, trends

### The Abstract Question Patterns

| Pattern Type | Example | Key Indicator |
|--------------|---------|---------------|
| **Should** questions | "Should government...?" | Policy, obligation |
| **Effect** questions | "What are the effects of...?" | Consequences, impact |
| **Comparison** | "How is X different from...?" | Contrast, evolution |
| **Prediction** | "How will X change in...?" | Future, speculation |
| **Value** questions | "Is X important? Why?" | Significance, priority |

## ЁЯУЭ MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Part 3 Responses

**Q: "Should governments ban plastic bags?"**

**Band 6 Response:**
"I think governments should ban plastic bags. Because plastic is very bad for environment. Many animals die from eating plastic. So we should use paper bags instead."

**Band 7+ Response:**
"I believe governments should implement restrictions on single-use plastic bags, though perhaps not a complete ban immediately. The environmental impact of plastic pollution is well-documented, particularly in marine ecosystems where wildlife suffers from ingestion and entanglement. For instance, many countries have seen significant reductions in plastic waste after implementing bag fees or bans. However, I think a phased approach would be more practical, giving businesses and consumers time to adapt to alternatives."

### Abstract Question Opening Phrases

| Purpose | Phrases to Use |
|---------|---------------|
| **Stating opinion** | "I believe...", "In my view...", "From my perspective..." |
| **Acknowledging complexity** | "It's a complex issue...", "There are valid arguments on both sides..." |
| **Adding reasons** | "The main reason is...", "This is because...", "Primarily..." |
| **Giving examples** | "For instance...", "A clear example is...", "Take..., for example..." |
| **Conceding points** | "While it's true that...", "Admittedly...", "On the other hand..." |
| **Concluding** | "Overall...", "In conclusion...", "To sum up..." |

### Part 3 Vocabulary Toolkit

**For "Should" Questions:**
- obligation, responsibility, duty, imperative, crucial
- implement, enforce, regulate, legislate, mandate
- balance, weigh, consider, evaluate

**For "Effect" Questions:**
- consequence, impact, influence, result, outcome
- significant, considerable, substantial, profound
- positive, negative, beneficial, detrimental, adverse
- immediate, long-term, short-term, lasting

## ЁЯТб COACH'S NOTES

### ЁЯОУ Teaching Points

**1. The "I" to "We" Shift**
> Part 1 and Part 2 are about YOU. Part 3 is about SOCIETY. Notice the difference:
> - Part 1: "I like reading because..."
> - Part 3: "People read because...", "Society benefits when..."

**2. Why 3+ Sentences Matters**
A one-sentence answer in Part 3 signals to the examiner that you can't develop abstract ideas. Three sentences shows you can:
- State a position
- Support it with reasoning
- Illustrate with examples

**3. It's Okay to Generalize**
In Part 3, you're expected to generalize. Phrases like:
- "Generally speaking..."
- "In most cases..."
- "Typically..."

These show you understand the abstract nature of the discussion.

### ЁЯФз Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Answering with "I"** | Shift to "people," "society," "many," "most" |
| **One-sentence answers** | Force yourself to count: 1-2-3-4 sentences minimum |
| **Going blank** | Use the formula: Answer тЖТ Reason тЖТ Example тЖТ Extension |
| **Being too absolute** | Use qualifying language: "generally," "tend to," "in many cases" |
| **Running out of ideas** | Remember: you can speculate! Part 3 allows (and expects) opinions |

### ЁЯОп Pro Tips

1. **Buy thinking time** тАУ "That's an interesting question. Let me think about that for a moment..."
2. **Use the question words** тАУ repeat parts of the question in your answer
3. **Reference the Part 2 topic** тАУ if related, say "As I mentioned earlier about..."
4. **Practice abstract thinking** тАУ read opinion pieces and notice how arguments are structured
