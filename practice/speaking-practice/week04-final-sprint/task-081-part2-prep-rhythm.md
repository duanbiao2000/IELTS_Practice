# Task 81: Part 2 Preparation Rhythm – Master the 60-Second Prep

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [10 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why focus on Part 2 preparation?**

Part 2 gives you exactly **60 seconds** to prepare. How you use this time determines your success:
- **Poor prep** → Running out of things to say, panic
- **Good prep** → 2 minutes of fluent, structured speech

This is the ONLY part of IELTS where you get dedicated preparation time. Master it.

### The 60-Second Reality

| What Many Students Do | What You Should Do |
|-----------------------|-------------------|
| Write full sentences | Write ONLY keywords |
| Try to plan every word | Plan the STRUCTURE |
| Waste time deciding | Start writing immediately |
| Panic about time | Practice a routine |

> **Ask yourself:** What do I usually do during the 60-second prep? Do I feel rushed or prepared?

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample 60-Second Preparation

**Cue Card:** Describe a piece of technology you use

**What to Write (3 Keywords):**
```
1. Smartphone
2. Daily communication
3. Life-changing
```

**What NOT to Write (full sentences):**
```
❌ I want to talk about my smartphone because I use it every day
❌ It helps me communicate with family and friends
❌ Technology has changed my life significantly
```

**Why Keywords Work:**
- "Smartphone" → triggers the whole story
- "Daily communication" → reminds you to focus on that aspect
- "Life-changing" → gives you the conclusion angle

### 60-Second Time Allocation

| Seconds | What to Do |
|---------|------------|
| 0-5 | Read cue card quickly |
| 5-15 | Write keyword 1 (main subject) |
| 15-25 | Write keyword 2 (key detail) |
| 25-35 | Write keyword 3 (extension) |
| 35-45 | Add optional 4th keyword if needed |
| 45-55 | Take a breath, mentally prepare opening |
| 55-60 | Pick up pen, get ready to speak |

### Cue Card Keyword Templates

By Topic Type:

| Topic Type | Keyword 1 | Keyword 2 | Keyword 3 |
|------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Person** | Who | Relationship | Why important |
| **Place** | Where | Atmosphere | Memory |
| **Object** | What | Use/meaning | Value |
| **Event** | When | What happened | Impact |
| **Activity** | What | How learned | Enjoyment |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Less is More in Prep Time**
> The more you write, the more you'll read. The less you write, the more you'll speak naturally. Your 3 keywords are anchors, not scripts.

**2. Trust Your Universal Examples**
You've developed 10 universal examples. Your prep time is just about choosing WHICH ONE fits this cue card and adjusting your 3 keywords accordingly.

**3. The 60-Second Mindset**
60 seconds feels short, but it's actually enough if:
- You don't waste time reading the cue card twice
- You start writing immediately
- You write keywords, not thoughts
- You trust yourself to fill in the gaps

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Writing full sentences** | Draw a tiny box on your paper – only 3 words fit inside |
| **Running out of time** | Practice until you can write 3 keywords in 30 seconds |
| **Forgetting what keywords mean** | Make them specific. "Experience" is too vague. "Graduation day" is clear |
| **Reading from notes** | Put the paper aside after 10 seconds of speaking |
| **Panic blanking** | Have a default structure: What → When → Why → Feeling |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Use abbreviations** – "smrtphone" saves time
2. **Draw symbols** – a heart for "love", a star for "important"
3. **Number your points** – ①②③ guides your speaking flow
4. **Practice with NO prep** – builds confidence that you can handle less time
