# Task 30: Shadowing Day 6 - TED-Ed at 0.85x Speed

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [10 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**What is Shadowing and Why TED-Ed?**

Shadowing is **active imitation**—you speak simultaneously with the audio, mimicking:
- Rhythm and pacing
- Intonation and stress
- Pausing and phrasing
- Emotional expression

**Why TED-Ed?**
- Short, focused content (3-6 minutes)
- Clear, articulate speakers
- Educational vocabulary (great for IELTS!)
- Interesting topics keep you engaged

**Why 0.85x Speed?**
- Slightly slower = easier to catch pronunciation details
- Gives your brain more processing time
- Helps build accuracy before speed
- You'll speed up naturally with practice

### Key Concepts

**Shadowing vs. Passive Listening:**
| Passive Listening | Shadowing |
|-------------------|-----------|
| You just hear | You SPEAK with the audio |
| No muscle memory | Builds muscle memory |
| Low retention | High retention |
| Easy | Challenging but effective |

**The Shadowing Process:**
1. Listen once to understand
2. Read transcript (if available)
3. Shadow at reduced speed
4. Shadow at normal speed
5. Record yourself for comparison

## 🎯 GUIDED PRACTICE

### Task Overview
Shadow a TED-Ed video at 0.85x speed, focusing on rhythm, intonation, and clear pronunciation.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Choose Your TED-Ed Video [3 min]

Visit **YouTube.com/TED-Ed** and choose a video:
- Length: 3-6 minutes
- Topic: Something that interests you
- Speaker: Clear, articulate voice

**🎧 Finding TED-Ed Videos:**
- Visit: https://www.youtube.com/user/TEDEd or https://ed.ted.com
- Search topics that interest you
- Choose videos 3-6 minutes long
- Most include transcripts

#### STEP 2: First Listen - Just Understand [2 min]

1. Play the video at **normal speed (1.0x)**
2. Just listen—don't speak yet
3. Focus on understanding the content
4. Note interesting vocabulary words

#### STEP 3: Second Listen - Notice Patterns [2 min]

1. Play again at **normal speed**
2. Focus on:
   - Where does the speaker pause?
   - Which words are stressed?
   - How does intonation rise and fall?
   - What's the speaking rhythm?

#### STEP 4: Shadow at 0.85x Speed [5 min]

1. Click settings (gear icon) → Playback speed → 0.75x or 0.85x
2. Start shadowing:
   - Speak WITH the speaker, not after
   - Mimic everything: speed, pauses, emphasis
   - Don't worry about every word—keep going if you miss
   - Focus on the FLOW more than perfection

3. Repeat 2-3 times at 0.85x

#### STEP 5: Shadow at Normal Speed [3 min]

1. Increase to 1.0x speed
2. Shadow again—you should feel more comfortable
3. Notice what's easier at normal speed now

#### STEP 6: Record and Compare [3 min]

1. Choose a 30-second segment
2. Record yourself shadowing it
3. Listen to your recording vs. original

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Shadowing Focus Areas

| Focus Area | What to Listen For | How to Mimic |
|------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Pausing** | Where speaker stops for breath | Mark pauses with slashes in transcript |
| **Stress** | Which words get emphasis | Stress content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives) |
| **Intonation** | Rise and fall of pitch | Questions rise; statements fall |
| **Linking** | How words connect | "turn it on" → "tur-ni-ton" |
| **Rhythm** | The beat of speech | English is stress-timed, not syllable-timed |

### Sample Shadowing Exercise

**Original Text:**
> "Have you ever wondered why we dream? For centuries, humans have been fascinated by the mysterious world of dreams. Some believe dreams are messages from a higher power, while others think they're merely random brain activity."

**Marked for Shadowing:**
> "Have you ever WONdered / WHY we DREAM? / For CENturies, / HUmans have been FAScinated by / the mySTERious WORLD of DREAMS. / Some beLIEVE dreams are MESsages / from a HIGher POWer, / while OTHers THINK / they're MEREly RAn-dome BRAIN acTIvity."

**Notes:**
- CAPS = stressed words
- / = brief pause
- Stretch out stressed syllables slightly
- Connect words within phrases

### Common Shadowing Challenges

| Challenge | Why It Happens | Coach's Fix |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| **Can't keep up** | Processing speed | Use slower speed, focus on key words only |
| **Missing words** | Trying to say everything | It's okay—focus on rhythm, not perfection |
| **Losing place** | No visual guide | Read transcript first, mark pauses |
| **Sound monotone** | Not mimicking intonation | Exaggerate—be dramatic! |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Slower Speed Works**
- At 1.0x, you're in "catch-up mode"
- At 0.85x, you have processing time
- You notice pronunciation details you miss at full speed
- Muscle memory forms better with accuracy first

**2. The Progressive Approach**
```
Day 1-3: Shadow at 0.75x
Day 4-6: Shadow at 0.85x  ← You are here
Day 7-9: Shadow at 0.95x
Day 10+: Shadow at 1.0x
```

**3. What You're Training**
Shadowing develops:
- **Prosody**: The "music" of English (rhythm, stress, intonation)
- **Muscle memory**: Your mouth learns English movement patterns
- **Ear training**: You hear English as native speakers produce it
- **Confidence**: You know you CAN produce these sounds

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Example | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Starting too fast** | Jumping to 1.0x immediately | Start at 0.75x—speed builds naturally |
| **Stopping when you miss** | Freezing on a difficult word | Keep going! Rhythm > perfection |
| **Shadowing without listening first** | Jumping straight to speaking | Listen 2-3 times before shadowing |
| **Only shadowing once** | Doing it once and moving on | Repeat 3-5 times for muscle memory |
| **Choosing too-long content** | 20+ minute videos | Start with 3-5 minute segments |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Choose topics you genuinely enjoy**—you'll shadow longer
2. **Stand up while shadowing**—energy improves voice quality
3. **Use headphones**—hear details more clearly
4. **Mark your transcript**—slash marks for pauses, caps for stress
5. **Record yourself weekly**—track your progress

### 🎤 Pronunciation Self-Monitor for Shadowing

**Critical Sounds to Focus On:**

| IPA Symbol | Sound | Words to Watch | Your Check |
|------------|-------|-----------------|------------|
| **/θ/** | voiceless th | think, thought, anything | □ Heard correctly |
| **/ð/** | voiced th | this, that, these, those | □ Heard correctly |
| **/æ/** | short a | that, man, ran, can | □ Mouth wide open |
| **/v/** | v sound | very, have, over | □ Teeth on lip |
| **-ed** | past ending | watched, decided, asked | □ Not dropped |
| **/ŋ/** | ng sound | thinking, during, something | □ Back of tongue |

**Common Chinese Shadowing Errors:**

| Error Type | What Happens | Why It's a Problem | How to Fix |
|-------------|--------------|-------------------|------------|
| **Dropping -ed** | "walk" instead of "walked" | Grammar AND pronunciation unclear | Exaggerate the /t/ or /d/ |
| **TH → S/Z** | "sink" instead of "think" | Very noticeable to examiners | Stick tongue between teeth |
| **No schwa /ə/** | "A-nd" instead of "ənd" | Sounds robotic, unnatural | Reduce vowel sound in unstressed words |
| **Final consonants** | Dropping /s/, /t/, /k/ at word ends | Words unclear, cut off | Practice ending each word firmly |
| **Monotone** | Flat intonation | Sounds bored, uninterested | Exaggerate pitch changes |

**Self-Correction Checklist During Shadowing:**
- [ ] Am I pronouncing TH sounds correctly?
- [ ] Are my -ed endings clear?
- [ ] Am I using weak forms (schwa /ə/)?
- [ ] Is my intonation varied (not flat)?
- [ ] Are final consonants clear?
