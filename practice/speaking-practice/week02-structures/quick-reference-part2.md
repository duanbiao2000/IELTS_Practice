# Part 2 Quick Reference

> The Long Turn - Master the 2-minute speaking task with these proven strategies!

---

## 📋 Part 2 Format Overview

| Time | What You Do |
|------|-------------|
| **1 minute** | Read topic card & make notes |
| **2 minutes** | Speak continuously (don't stop!) |
| **Total** | 3-4 minutes including examiner transitions |

**Key Rule**: You MUST speak for the FULL 2 minutes. Stopping early = lower score.

---

## 🎯 The 1-1-1 Formula (Never Run Out of Things to Say)

For ANY Part 2 topic, structure your answer with:

| Element | Time | What to Say |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **What** | ~30 sec | What it is, what it looks like |
| **When/Where/Who** | ~45 sec | Context and story |
| **Why/How** | ~45 sec | Reasons, feelings, opinions |
| **Extension** | ~30 sec | Future plans, comparison, or memory |

**Total**: ~2 minutes

---

## 📝 Note-Taking Template (1 Minute Prep)

Draw this quick diagram on your paper:

```
        ┌─────────────────┐
        │   WHAT IS IT?   │
        └─────────────────┘
                 ↓
        ┌─────────────────┐
        │  WHEN/WHERE/WHO  │
        └─────────────────┘
                 ↓
        ┌─────────────────┐
        │  WHY IMPORTANT  │
        └─────────────────┘
                 ↓
        ┌─────────────────┐
        │  FEELINGS/OPINION│
        └─────────────────┘
```

**Key Points Only** - NOT full sentences!
- Each box: 2-3 keywords
- Example: `grandmother house`, `childhood summers`, `comfortable`, `safety`

## 🎤 Extension Techniques (When You Need More Time)

| Technique | Formula | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **Add Description** | "It looks like..." / "It has..." | "It has a beautiful garden with..."
| **Tell a Story** | "I remember when..." | "I remember when I first visited..."
| **Compare** | "Unlike X, Y is..." | "Unlike modern apartments, it has..."
| **Express Feeling** | "What I love about it is..." | "What I love most is the atmosphere..."
| **Add Future** | "In the future, I hope to..." | "I hope to visit again next summer..."
| **Add Detail** | "Specifically..." / "The main feature is..." | "The main feature is the wooden balcony..." |

## 🎯 Common Part 2 Topics & Quick Frameworks

### People

**Template**:
```
- Who: [name, relationship]
- Appearance: [what they look like]
- Personality: [key traits]
- Why important: [impact on you]
- Story: [specific memory together]
```

**Keywords to prepare**:
- appearance: tall, short, glasses, smile
- personality: kind, patient, humorous, strict
- qualities: helpful, knowledgeable, supportive

---

### Places

**Template**:
```
- What: [name of place]
- Location: [where it is]
- Description: [what it looks like]
- Why you go there: [reasons]
- Feelings: [atmosphere, emotions]
```

**Keywords to prepare**:
- atmosphere: peaceful, bustling, cozy, spacious
- features: modern, traditional, crowded, quiet
- activities: read, relax, meet friends, exercise

---

### Objects

**Template**:
```
- What: [object]
- When got: [how you acquired it]
- Appearance: [physical description]
- Why important: [significance]
- Without it: [how life would change]
```

**Keywords to prepare**:
- condition: brand new, worn out, pristine
- features: portable, compact, heavy, delicate
- value: expensive, priceless, sentimental

---

### Activities

**Template**:
```
- What: [activity name]
- When: [frequency, time]
- Where: [location]
- Why enjoy: [reasons]
- Future: [plans to continue]
```

**Keywords to prepare**:
- frequency: daily, weekly, occasionally, rarely
- feelings: relaxing, challenging, exciting, boring
- benefits: healthy, educational, social, creative

---

### Events

**Template**:
```
- What: [event name]
- When: [date, time]
- Where: [location]
- Who with: [people involved]
- Why memorable: [highlights, emotions]
```

**Keywords to prepare**:
- emotions: exciting, nervous, proud, disappointed
- outcome: successful, challenging, unexpected
- aftermath: celebrated, learned, reflected

## 🔄 Universal Examples (Adapt Any Story)

### The "Grandmother's House" Story

```
"I'd like to talk about my grandmother's house,
where I spent many summers as a child.

The house is located in a small village about two hours
from the city. It's a traditional building with a beautiful
garden full of fruit trees.

What makes this place special to me is the memories.
Every summer, my cousins and I would play in the garden,
pick fresh fruits, and listen to our grandmother's stories.

The atmosphere is so peaceful and comforting. Unlike
the busy city with all its noise and traffic, this place
offers a sense of tranquility that I really appreciate.

I hope to visit again next summer and maybe bring my
own children someday to experience the same joy."
```

### The "Old Book" Story (can be any object)

```
"I'd like to describe an old book that has special meaning
to me. It's called [Book Title], which I received as a
gift from my [family member] when I was [age].

The book is quite old now, with yellowed pages and a
worn cover. But despite its condition, it holds great
sentimental value.

What I love about this book is not just the content,
but the memories associated with it. Reading it reminds
me of [specific memory/person].

Without this book, my childhood would have been different.
It sparked my love for [topic: reading/science/history],
which continues to influence me today."
```

## 🚫 Common Part 2 Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| **Stopping before 2 min** | Lower fluency score | Always add more details |
| **Reading from notes** | Sounds unnatural | Notes = keywords only |
| **Memorized speech** | Sounds robotic | Tell a real story |
| **Being too brief** | Doesn't show range | Add descriptions, feelings |
| **Going off-topic** | Confusion | Stick to the prompt points |
| **Speaking too fast** | Unclear pronunciation | Slow down, be clear |

---

## 🎯 Time Management During Speaking

| Time Marker | Your Progress | What to Do |
|-------------|---------------|------------|
| **0:00-0:30** | Introduction | Describe "what it is" |
| **0:30-1:15** | Context | Add "when/where/who" |
| **1:15-1:45** | Depth | Explain "why important" |
| **1:45-2:00** | Extension | Add feelings or future |
| **After 2:00** | Examiner stops | You did well! |

**If examiner doesn't stop**: Keep going with:
- "Another thing I should mention is..."
- "I'd also like to add that..."
