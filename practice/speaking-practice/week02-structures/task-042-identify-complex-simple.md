# Task 42: Identify Complex vs. Simple Sentences in Recording

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [15 minutes]

### Before You Begin

**This Task Builds Self-Awareness**

You can't improve what you don't notice. This task trains you to:
- **Hear the difference** between simple and complex sentences
- **Recognize your patterns** (what do you ACTUALLY do?)
- **Identify improvement areas** (where should you focus?)
- **Track progress** (compare to future recordings)

### Key Concepts

**Sentence Types for IELTS:**

| Type | Structure | Example | Band Score Impact |
|------|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **Simple** | One independent clause | "I like reading." | Band 5-6 if overused |
| **Compound** | Two independent clauses joined | "I like reading **and** I learn a lot." | Band 6 |
| **Complex** | Independent + dependent clause | "I like reading **because it helps me learn**." | Band 7+ |
| **Compound-Complex** | Multiple clauses | "I like reading, **which helps me learn**, **so I read daily**." | Band 7+ |

**Week 2 Patterns That Create Complex Sentences:**
- **Relative clauses**: "...which/that/who + verb..."
- **Conditionals**: "If I had..., I would..."
- **Contrast**: "While some..., others..."
- **Modals**: "...tend to / be likely to / be inclined to"

**The Goal Ratio:**
For Band 7+:
- 50% simple sentences (for clarity)
- 50% complex sentences (for sophistication)
- Not 90% simple, 10% complex!

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Analysis

**Transcript:**
> "I like The Shawshank Redemption. It's a great movie. The acting is excellent. The story is about hope. Andy is the main character. He goes to prison. He becomes friends with Red. Red helps him survive. The ending is amazing. I've watched it many times. It always makes me emotional."

**Sentence Analysis:**
1. "I like The Shawshank Redemption." [S]
2. "It's a great movie." [S]
3. "The acting is excellent." [S]
4. "The story is about hope." [S]
5. "Andy is the main character." [S]
6. "He goes to prison." [S]
7. "He becomes friends with Red." [S]
8. "Red helps him survive." [S]
9. "The ending is amazing." [S]
10. "I've watched it many times." [S]
11. "It always makes me emotional." [S]

**Statistics:**
- Total: 11 sentences
- Simple: 11 (100%)
- Complex: 0 (0%)

**This is EXTREME simple sentence stacking!** Band 5-6 level.

**Improved Version with Complex Sentences:**
> "I like The Shawshank Redemption, **which is a great movie** with excellent acting. The story is about hope, **following a man named Andy** who goes to prison. There he becomes friends with Red, **who helps him survive** his incarceration. The ending is amazing, **and although I've watched it many times**, it always makes me emotional."

**New Statistics:**
- Total: 4 sentences
- Simple: 1 (25%)
- Complex: 3 (75%)

**This is Band 7+ level!**

### How to Convert Simple to Complex

| Simple | Complex | Method |
|--------|---------|--------|
| "I like reading. It is fun." | "I like reading, **which is fun**." | Add relative clause |
| "The movie was good. I enjoyed it." | "**Although** the movie was long, I enjoyed it." | Add concession |
| "People like cities. Others prefer nature." | "**While some** like cities, **others prefer** nature." | Add contrast |
| "I think this. I believe that." | "I think this **because** I believe that." | Add reason |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why This Analysis Matters**

You can't improve what you don't measure:
- **Seeing the numbers** is more powerful than "feeling" you use simple sentences
- **Patterns become obvious** once you mark them
- **Progress is trackable**—do this again in Week 4!
- **Target is clear**—aim for 50% complex sentences

**2. The 50/50 Rule**

Why not 100% complex sentences?
- Too complex = hard to follow
- Some ideas are best expressed simply
- Variety creates natural rhythm
- Simple sentences provide clarity and emphasis

**Good rhythm alternates:**
- Complex → Complex → Simple (emphasis)
- Simple → Complex → Complex → Simple (conclusion)
- NOT: Simple → Simple → Simple → Simple...

**3. Automatic vs. Planned Complex Sentences**

**Planned** (what you do when you prepare):
- "I will use a relative clause here."
- "I'll add a contrast pattern."

**Automatic** (what happens when you don't prepare):
- Your default patterns take over
- This is what Task 41 revealed!
- This analysis shows your TRUE baseline

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | What It Looks Like | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Denial** | "I use complex sentences!" (but analysis shows 10%) | Trust the data—your feeling ≠ reality |
| **Perfectionism** | "90% of my sentences must be complex!" | Aim for 50%—quality over quantity |
| **Forgetting the analysis** | Never looking at it again | Save it! Compare to Week 4 |
| **Over-correcting** | Next recording is 100% complex (unnatural) | Balance is key—50/50 is ideal |
| **Not marking correctly** | Missing dependent clauses | Learn the indicators: which, that, because, although, while |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Save your transcript**—you'll use it again in Week 4
2. **Circle the dependent clauses**—make them visible
3. **Set a weekly goal**—"I'll add 2 more complex sentences"
4. **Practice ONE pattern** until it becomes automatic
5. **Celebrate progress**—even 40% complex is better than 20%!
