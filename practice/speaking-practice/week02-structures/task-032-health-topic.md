# Task 32: Health Topic with Relative Clause

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [12 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why is the "Health" topic essential for IELTS?**

Health appears in ALL parts of IELTS Speaking:
- **Part 1**: Your health habits, exercise, diet
- **Part 2**: Describe a healthy activity, a health-related experience
- **Part 3**: Public health, healthcare systems, lifestyle trends

**This task combines two powerful skills:**
1. Health vocabulary (high-frequency topic)
2. Relative clauses (complex sentence structure)

### Key Concepts

**Health Topic Vocabulary:**

| Category | Words & Phrases |
|----------|----------------|
| **Exercise** | workout, jogging, swimming, gym, fitness, keep fit, active lifestyle |
| **Diet** | balanced diet, nutritious, organic, processed food, home-cooked, calories |
| **Mental Health** | stress, anxiety, meditation, mindfulness, relax, unwind, mental wellbeing |
| **Sleep** | insomnia, quality of sleep, early riser, night owl, sufficient rest |
| **Habits** | sedentary, active, health-conscious, routine, consistency |

**Relative Clause Review (from Task 26):**
```
[Person/Thing] + which/that/who + [verb]...
```

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample 1-Minute Response

> "When it comes to health, I try to maintain an active lifestyle. The exercise **which I enjoy most** is jogging, **which I do** three times a week in a park near my home. It's an activity **that not only keeps me physically fit** but also helps clear my mind after a long day.
>
> As for diet, I've been trying to eat healthier recently. The foods **that I've been cutting back on** are processed snacks and sugary drinks, **which can be** really harmful if consumed regularly. Instead, I try to cook more meals at home, **which allows me to** control the ingredients and eat more fresh vegetables.
>
> For mental health, I believe relaxation is crucial. One practice **that has really helped me** is meditation. I spend about 10 minutes every evening meditating, **is a technique** that my friend recommended to me last year. It's something **that has significantly reduced** my stress levels and improved my sleep quality."

### Response Breakdown

| Point | Content | Relative Clause Used |
|-------|---------|---------------------|
| 1. Exercise | Jogging 3x/week | "which I enjoy most" / "which I do" / "that not only keeps..." |
| 2. Diet | Less processed food | "that I've been cutting back on" / "which can be" / "which allows me to" |
| 3. Mental health | Meditation | "that has really helped me" / "which has significantly reduced" |

### Common Health Questions & Sample Answers

| Question | Sample Answer with Relative Clause |
|----------|-----------------------------------|
| "Do you exercise regularly?" | "Yes, I jog regularly. **It's an activity which helps me stay** fit and relieve stress." |
| "What do you usually eat?" | "I prefer home-cooked meals. **The dishes which I enjoy most** are stir-fried vegetables with rice." |
| "How do you handle stress?" | "I use several methods. The one **that works best for me** is exercise, **which releases endorphins**." |
| "Is health important to you?" | "Absolutely. I believe health **is the foundation which supports** everything else in life." |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Health + Relative Clauses Work Well**

Health topics naturally involve:
- Describing habits (perfect for relative clauses)
- Explaining causes and effects (relative clauses connect these)
- Giving reasons (relative clauses add detail efficiently)

**2. Common Health Question Types**

| Part | Question Style | How to Answer |
|------|----------------|---------------|
| **Part 1** | Personal habits | "What do you do to stay healthy?" |
| **Part 2** | Describe an experience | "Describe a healthy activity you enjoy" |
| **Part 3** | Abstract/General | "Should the government promote healthy lifestyles?" |

**3. Relative Clause Variety in Health Topics**

| Type | Example |
|------|---------|
| **which** (things/activities) | "Jogging, **which is a great cardio exercise**, is my favorite." |
| **that** (preferences/choices) | "The diet **that works for me** is low-carb." |
| **who** (people) | "My friend, **who is a fitness trainer**, gave me advice." |

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Example | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Simple sentence stacking** | "I like running. It is good. It helps me." | "I like running, **which is good and helps me**." |
| **No specific details** | "I eat healthy food." | "I eat vegetables **which are rich in vitamins**." |
| **Forgetting relative clauses** | Entire answer without any | Plan 1-2 relative clauses before speaking |
| **Repetitive vocabulary** | Using "good" and "healthy" repeatedly | Use: nutritious, beneficial, essential, valuable |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Prepare 3 health stories** you can adapt to different questions
2. **Use relative clauses to explain WHY** something is good/bad
3. **Learn 5 health collocations** and use them naturally
4. **Connect physical and mental health**—examiners like this depth
5. **Be honest**—authentic answers sound more natural
