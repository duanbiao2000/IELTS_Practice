# Task 43: Learn Part 2 Structure (Topic + Background + Detail + Feeling)

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [12 minutes]

### Before You Begin

**Part 2 is the "Long Turn"**

In IELTS Speaking Part 2:
- You get a cue card with a topic
- You have **1 minute to prepare**
- You must speak for **2 minutes**
- You speak alone (examiner doesn't interrupt)

### The 4-Part Structure

This structure is PROVEN to work for Band 7+:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ PART 2: THE 4-PART STRUCTURE                               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                             │
│ 1. TOPIC (15-20 seconds)                                   │
│    → Directly answer what is asked                         │
│                                                             │
│ 2. BACKGROUND (30-40 seconds)                              │
│    → Context, when/where/who involved                      │
│                                                             │
│ 3. DETAIL (60 seconds)                                     │
│    → Main content, description, specific details           │
│                                                             │
│ 4. FEELING (20-30 seconds)                                 │
│    → Your opinion, why it matters, conclusion              │
│                                                             │
│ TOTAL: 2 minutes                                           │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Key Concepts

**Why This Structure Works:**

| Part | Function | Time | What It Does |
|------|----------|------|--------------|
| **Topic** | Direct answer | 15-20s | Tells examiner you understood the question |
| **Background** | Set the scene | 30-40s | Provides context naturally |
| **Detail** | Main content | 60s | Fills most of the time with substance |
| **Feeling** | Personal connection | 20-30s | Shows sophisticated thinking |

## 🎯 GUIDED PRACTICE

### Task Overview
Learn and practice the 4-Part Part 2 structure using a sample topic.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Understand Each Part [4 min]

**Part 1: TOPIC (Direct Answer)**
- Immediately answer the main question
- 1-2 sentences
- Don't over-explain yet
- Example: "I'm going to talk about my favorite restaurant, which is called..."

**Part 2: BACKGROUND (Context)**
- When did this happen?
- Where did it take place?
- Who was involved?
- How did you discover it?
- Example: "I first went there last year with my friends. It's located in the city center, near the main square..."

**Part 3: DETAIL (Main Content)**
- What does it look like?
- What specifically happened?
- Describe the experience thoroughly
- This should be your longest part
- Example: "The restaurant has a cozy atmosphere with warm lighting and wooden tables. The food is incredible, especially their pasta dishes. I remember ordering the seafood risotto, which was perfectly cooked with fresh ingredients..."

**Part 4: FEELING (Why It Matters)**
- How did you feel?
- Why is it memorable?
- Would you recommend it?
- What does it mean to you?
- Example: "What I love most about this place is how it makes me feel relaxed and happy. The combination of good food, great company, and welcoming atmosphere creates the perfect dining experience. I've recommended it to many friends, and they've all loved it too."

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Full Response Using 4-Part Structure

**Topic:** Describe a restaurant you enjoy.

> **[TOPIC - 15 seconds]**
> "I'm going to talk about my favorite restaurant, which is called Bella Italia, located in the downtown area of my city."
>
> **[BACKGROUND - 35 seconds]**
> "I first discovered this restaurant about two years ago when my colleague recommended it for a team lunch. It's situated on a quiet street just off the main avenue, so it's easily accessible but not too crowded. The restaurant is family-owned and has been operating for over 20 years, **which gives it a** authentic and welcoming atmosphere."
>
> **[DETAIL - 60 seconds]**
> "Bella Italia serves traditional Italian cuisine, specializing in homemade pasta and wood-fired pizzas. The interior is decorated with warm colors, vintage photographs of Italy, and checkered tablecloths **that create a cozy**, traditional ambiance. What really stands out is the open kitchen, **where you can watch** the chefs preparing fresh pasta daily.
>
> My favorite dish is their carbonara, **which is made with** authentic Italian ingredients including guanciale, pecorino cheese, and fresh eggs. The pasta is perfectly al dente, and the sauce is creamy without being too heavy. They also serve an amazing tiramisu **that is the best I've ever tasted**—light, creamy, and not too sweet."
>
> **[FEELING - 30 seconds]**
> "What I enjoy most about Bella Italia is **how it makes me feel** like I'm transported to Italy every time I visit. The combination of delicious food, warm hospitality, and authentic atmosphere creates a truly memorable dining experience. **Although I don't go there every week**, I try to visit at least once a month, and I've recommended it to all my friends and family. For me, it represents the perfect balance of quality, tradition, and comfort **that makes** a restaurant truly special."

### Structure Breakdown

| Part | Time | Key Phrases Used | Function |
|------|------|------------------|----------|
| **Topic** | 15s | "I'm going to talk about..." | Direct answer |
| **Background** | 35s | "first discovered," "situated," "family-owned" | Context |
| **Detail** | 60s | "specializing in," "what really stands out," "my favorite" | Main content |
| **Feeling** | 30s | "what I enjoy most," "for me it represents" | Personal meaning |

### Common Part 2 Topics & Structure Application

| Topic | Topic Sentence | Background | Detail | Feeling |
|-------|----------------|------------|--------|---------|
| **Person** | "I'll describe my grandmother..." | Who she is, relationship | Her qualities, specific memories | Why she's important to me |
| **Place** | "I'm going to talk about a park..." | Location, when I go | What's there, what I do | Why I like it |
| **Activity** | "I'd like to describe swimming..." | When/where I started | How I do it, benefits | Why I continue |
| **Object** | "I'm going to describe my phone..." | When I got it, what type | Features, how I use it | Why it's valuable |
| **Event** | "I'll talk about my graduation..." | When/where it happened | The ceremony, who attended | What it meant to me |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why This Structure Is So Effective**

**It manages time automatically:**
- Topic = quick start
- Background = natural expansion
- Detail = main content (longest part)
- Feeling = meaningful conclusion

**It prevents common problems:**
- Running out of things to say → Detail section is meaty
- Losing track → Structure keeps you organized
- Rambling → Each part has a clear purpose
- Forgetting the question → Topic directly addresses it

**2. The 1-Minute Preparation**

During your 1 minute prep time:
- **Write ONLY keywords** (not full sentences)
- **Organize by the 4 parts**
- **Mentally walk through** what you'll say

**Sample preparation notes:**
```
Bella Italia
- Where: downtown, quiet street
- What: Italian, pasta, pizza
- Atmosphere: cozy, family-owned
- Why: authentic, makes me feel transported
```

**3. Adapting the Structure**

The 4 parts work for EVERY Part 2 topic:
- People: Who → Background → Description of person → Why they matter
- Places: Where → Location details → What you do there → Your feelings
- Things: What → When you got it → Features/uses → Why important
- Events: What → When/where → What happened → Why memorable

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | What Happens | Coach's Fix |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| **Skipping the Topic** | Jumping straight into details | Always start with "I'm going to talk about..." |
| **Background too long** | Spending 1 minute on context | Keep background to 30-40 seconds max |
| **Detail too short** | Running out of things to say | Detail should be your LONGEST part (60 seconds) |
| **Forgetting Feeling** | Ending abruptly with "that's it" | Always conclude with why it matters to you |
| **Not timing yourself** | Speaking for 1 minute or 3 minutes | Practice with a timer! |
| **Writing full sentences** | Can't find notes during speech | Use keywords only! |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Memorize the 4 parts**—T, B, D, F
2. **Time each part**—know when to move on
3. **Detail = description**—use your senses (sight, sound, taste, etc.)
4. **Feeling = sophisticated thinking**—this is where Band 7+ scores come from
5. **Practice with many topics**—the structure becomes automatic
