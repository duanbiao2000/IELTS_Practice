# Task 34: ELSA - TH Sounds (/θ/ and /ð/)

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [10 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why are TH sounds so important?**

The TH sounds are among the **most mispronounced sounds** for Chinese speakers:
- **/θ/** (voiceless) as in "think"
- **/ð/** (voiced) as in "this"

These sounds appear in **extremely common words**:
- think, this, that, those, these, the, there, their, they, them
- something, anything, nothing, with, both, through, thought, though

**Why focus on TH?**
- TH sounds are among the **top 10 most frequent sounds** in English
- Mispronouncing them makes your speech unclear
- They don't exist in Mandarin, so they need dedicated practice
- Mastering them = immediate clarity improvement

### Key Concepts

**The Two TH Sounds:**

| Symbol | Name | Voicing | Example Words |
|--------|------|---------|---------------|
| **/θ/** | Voiceless TH | No vocal cord vibration | think, three, through, thought, thin, thank |
| **/ð/** | Voiced TH | Vocal cord vibration | this, that, these, those, the, there, their, them |

**How to Make TH Sounds:**

**Step 1: Tongue Position**
- Place the tip of your tongue **between your teeth**
- Stick it out slightly—don't be shy!
- Lightly touch your upper teeth with your tongue

**Step 2: Air Flow**
- For /θ/ (voiceless): Blow air continuously (like a soft whisper)
- For /ð/ (voiced): Make a buzzing sound with your vocal cords

**Test it:**
- Touch your throat while saying "this"—feel vibration? That's /ð/
- Touch your throat while saying "think"—no vibration? That's /θ/

### Step-by-Step Instructions

**Do this exercise:**

### Why This Matters for IELTS

**Words with TH appear everywhere:**
> "I **think that** **this** **is the best** approach **because** **these** methods **work better than** **those**."

If you mispronounce every TH:
> ❌ "I sink zat dis is ze best approach because zese methods vork better zan zose."

The examiner might still understand, but it sounds:
- Unclear
- Less educated
- Potentially affecting your pronunciation score

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. The Tongue Position is Non-Negotiable**

You **must** put your tongue between your teeth:
- This feels unnatural at first
- Native speakers DO this (look at them closely!)
- It's the only way to make the sound correctly
- Don't be self-conscious—everyone learning English does this

**2. Voiceless vs. Voiced**

| Aspect | Voiceless /θ/ | Voiced /ð/ |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| **Examples** | think, thin, through | this, that, those |
| **Vocal cords** | No vibration | Vibration |
| **Throat test** | No feeling | Buzzing feeling |
| **Common in** | Content words | Function words (the, this, that, they) |

**3. Frequency Awareness**

**The top 20 words in English include:**
1. the (voiced TH!)
2. ... (many more with TH!)

You simply CANNOT speak clear English without mastering TH. It's not optional!

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Example | Coach's Fix |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Using /s/ instead of /θ/** | think → sink | Put tongue BETWEEN teeth—not behind! |
| **Using /d/ instead of /ð/** | this → dis | Tongue between teeth + VOICE (vibrate!) |
| **Using /f/ instead of /θ/** | three → free | Tongue between teeth, not on lower lip |
| **Being shy about tongue position** | Not sticking tongue out enough | Exaggerate at first—it's correct! |
| **Forgetting to voice /ð/** | that → "th-at" (no voice) | Touch throat, feel buzz when saying that |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Mirror practice**—watch your tongue between your teeth
2. **The "buzz" test**—touch throat for /ð/, should feel vibration
3. **Start slow**—say each word 5 times carefully, then speed up
4. **Daily practice**—TH sounds need constant reinforcement
5. **Record yourself**—compare your "think" with a native speaker
