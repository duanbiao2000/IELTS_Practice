### Key Concepts

**What "Unprepared" Means:**
- No writing down answers
- No practicing sentences first
- Start speaking within 5 seconds of seeing the topic
- Keep going for the full 2 minutes

**The Challenge:**
You may:
- Run out of things to say
- Fall back on simple sentences
- Pause or say "um" a lot
- Forget sophisticated patterns

**ALL OF THIS IS NORMAL!** This is your baseline.

## 🎯 GUIDED PRACTICE

### Task Overview
Speak for 2 minutes about your favorite movie or book WITHOUT preparation.

### Step-by-Step Instructions

#### STEP 1: Choose Your Topic [1 min]

Pick ONE:
- Your favorite movie
- Your favorite book
- A movie/book that made a big impression on you

**DO NOT:**
- Write down what you'll say
- Practice sentences
- Plan your structure

#### STEP 2: Start Recording Immediately [2 min]

When you're ready:
1. Start your voice recorder
2. Start speaking within 5 seconds
3. Speak for 2 full minutes
4. Stop only when timer beeps

**If you get stuck:**
- Keep talking! Even "let me think..." counts
- Add details: describe characters, scenes, your feelings
- Explain WHY you like it
- Talk about when you first saw/read it

#### STEP 3: Listen to Your Recording [3 min]

Listen to the full 2 minutes and note:
- How many times did you pause significantly?
- Did you use any complex sentences?
- Which Week 2 patterns did you use?
- What sounded good?
- What needs improvement?

#### STEP 4: Self-Assessment [3 min]

Complete the self-assessment form below.

### Sample Response (Excerpt)

> "I'd like to talk about my favorite movie, which is called The Pursuit of Happyness, starring Will Smith. I first watched it about five years ago, and it really made a deep impression on me.
>
> The movie is based on a true story about a man named Chris Gardner **who struggles with** homelessness while trying to build a better life for himself and his young son. What I love most about this film is **how it shows** the resilience of the human spirit. There's this one scene **where the main character** is sleeping in a subway bathroom with his son, and he's trying to protect him from the reality of their situation.
>

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Why Unprepared Speaking is Hard**

- **No planning time** = no structure
- **No vocabulary preparation** = word-finding pauses
- **No practice** = more mistakes
- **Mental processing** = harder to think AND speak simultaneously

**This is exactly what Part 2 feels like!**

**2. The 2-Minute Reality**

2 minutes feels like:
- **0:00-0:30**: Plenty to say
- **0:30-1:00**: Still comfortable
- **1:00-1:30**: Starting to think "what else?"
- **1:30-2:00**: Struggling to fill time

**Solution in the moment:**
- Add details (describe feelings, appearances, specific moments)
- Explain WHY multiple times
- Share personal reactions
- Compare to other movies/books

**3. What This Recording Reveals**

Your unprepared 2-minute recording shows:
- Your **true speaking level** (not your practiced level)
- Your **fallback patterns** (what you do when you don't plan)
- Your **fluency baseline** (how long you can speak without stopping)
- Your **natural vocabulary** (words you use without thinking)

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | What Happens | Coach's Fix |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| **Running out of things to say** | Finish in 60-90 seconds | Add details, describe more, explain feelings |
| **Simple sentence stacking** | "It was good. I liked it. The acting was good." | Plan 2-3 complex structures before starting |
| **Forgetting patterns** | No relative clauses, conditionals, etc. | Write 3 patterns on paper to glance at |
| **Repetitive vocabulary** | Using "good," "nice," "interesting" repeatedly | Use Task 38 adjective upgrades |
| **Panic when you lose your train** | Long silence, "um, um, um" | Have filler phrases ready: "Let me think...", "What I mean is..." |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Have 3 "go-to" patterns** ready (relative clause, conditional, contrast)
2. **Name-check frequently**—say character names, actor names, specific scenes
3. **Describe, don't just tell**—instead of "it was good," describe WHY
4. **Use your senses**—what did you see/hear/feel?
5. **It's okay to repeat**—if you run out, circle back to add more detail
