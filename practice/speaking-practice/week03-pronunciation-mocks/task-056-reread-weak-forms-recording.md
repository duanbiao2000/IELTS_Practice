# Task 56: Re-Read 5 Sentences with Weak Forms (Before/After Recording)

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [3 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why record before and after?**

Hearing your own progress is motivating. By recording the same sentences:
1. **Before**: Using your natural pronunciation (probably strong forms)
2. **After**: Consciously applying weak form rules

...you'll hear the immediate difference. This builds:
- **Awareness** of how weak forms sound
- **Confidence** that you can produce them
- **Motivation** to continue practicing

### The 5 Practice Sentences

These sentences include multiple opportunities for weak forms:

| # | Sentence | Weak Form Opportunities |
|---|----------|-------------------------|
| 1 | "I went to the shop and bought a book." | to, the, and, a |
| 2 | "It's a question of time and money." | a, of, and |
| 3 | "The end of the day is the best part." | The (×2), of, the |
| 4 | "A friend of mine is coming for dinner." | A, of, for |
| 5 | "The book and the movie are both available." | The (×2), and, the |

#### STEP 3: Mark the Weak Forms [3 min]

**Sentence 5:**
"̬The ̃book ̬and ̬the ̃movie are ̃both ̃available."

( ̬ = weak form,  ̃ = stressed content word)

#### STEP 4: Practice Each Sentence [5 min]
For each sentence:
1. Read it silently, marking stressed vs. unstressed
2. Say it slowly 3 times, consciously using weak forms
3. Check: Are the small words "swallowed"?
4. Repeat until it feels somewhat natural

#### STEP 5: "AFTER" Recording [3 min]
Record all 5 sentences again, this time **consciously using weak forms**.
- Focus on the schwa sound /ə/
- Keep function words short and unstressed
- Stress content words longer and louder

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sentence-by-Sentence Analysis

#### Sentence 1: "I went to the shop and bought a book."

**Weak forms to apply:**
- to: /tə/
- the: /ðə/
- and: /ən/
- a: /ə/

**Stressed words (longer, louder):**
- went, shop, bought, book

**Transcription:**
/aɪ went tə ðə ʃɒp ən bɔːt ə bʊk/

#### Sentence 2: "It's a question of time and money."

**Weak forms:**
- a: /ə/
- of: /əv/
- and: /ən/

**Stressed words:**
- question, time, money

**Transcription:**
/ɪts ə kwestʃən əv taɪm ən mʌni/

#### Sentence 3: "The end of the day is the best part."

**Weak forms:**
- The (first): /ðə/
- of: /əv/
- the (second): /ðə/

**Stressed words:**
- end, day, best, part

**Transcription:**
/ðə end əv ðə deɪ ɪz ðə best pɑːrt/

#### Sentence 4: "A friend of mine is coming for dinner."

**Weak forms:**
- A: /ə/
- of: /əv/
- for: /fər/

**Stressed words:**
- friend, mine, coming, dinner

**Transcription:**
/ə frend əv maɪn ɪz kʌmɪŋ fər dɪnər/

#### Sentence 5: "The book and the movie are both available."

**Weak forms:**
- The (×2): /ðə/
- and: /ən/

**Stressed words:**
- book, movie, both, available

**Transcription:**
/ðə bʊk ən ðə muːvi ər boʊθ əˈveɪləbl/

### Sample Student Comparison

**BEFORE recording:**
"I went TO THE shop AND bought A book."
(Sounded: /aɪ went tuː ðiː ʃɒp ænd bɔːt eɪ bʊk/)

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. This Is About Rhythm, Not Individual Sounds**
Weak forms aren't just about the sounds themselves—they create English rhythm. The contrast between stressed (long, loud) and unstressed (short, quiet) syllables gives English its distinctive "beat."

**2. The "Swallowed" Feeling**
When you use weak forms correctly, the small words feel like they disappear. They're still there, but they're quick and quiet. This is exactly what native speakers do.

**3. Don't Overthink the Schwa**
The schwa /ə/ is just a relaxed "uh" sound. Don't try to make it perfect—just relax your jaw and let the sound happen. It's the most natural sound your mouth makes.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Exaggerating weak forms too much** | They should be subtle, not caricature |
| **Forgetting which words to stress** | Content words = stressed. Function words = weak |
| **Applying weak forms inconsistently** | Every time these words are unstressed, use the weak form |
| **Feeling self-conscious** | Weak forms are CORRECT English, not "lazy" |
| **Not hearing the difference** | Record a native speaker from a podcast and compare |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Slow down**—it's harder to use weak forms when rushing
2. **Use a mirror**—watch your jaw stay relaxed on weak forms
3. **Practice with real speech**—listen for weak forms in podcasts, TED talks
4. **Come back to these sentences** in a few days and re-record. Retention matters!
