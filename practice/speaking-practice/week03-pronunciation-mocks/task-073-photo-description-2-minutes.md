# Task 73: Recording - Describe a Favorite Photo

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [4 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why practice describing photos?**

"Describe a photo/graph" is a common IELTS task type. Practicing this builds:
- **Descriptive vocabulary** (visual details, emotions, settings)
- **Fluency** (speaking continuously for 2 minutes)
- **Structure** (organized description, not random)
- **Part 2 skills** (long turn with no prompts)

### The 2-Minute Challenge

Speaking continuously for 2 full minutes is HARD. Most students run out of things to say after 60-90 seconds. This task builds:
- **Content generation** (finding things to say)
- **Pacing awareness** (not rushing)
- **Extension skills** (adding details)

### How to Describe a Photo Effectively

Use the **WHAT-WHERE-WHO-WHY-HOW** framework:

| Element | Questions to Ask |
|---------|-----------------|
| **WHAT** | What is in the photo? What's happening? |
| **WHERE** | Where was it taken? What's the setting? |
| **WHO** | Who is in the photo? What are they doing? |
| **WHEN** | When was it taken? What was the occasion? |
| **WHY** | Why is this photo special to you? |
| **HOW** | How did you feel when it was taken? How do you feel now? |

Record a 2-minute continuous description of a favorite photo, using structure and descriptive vocabulary.

#### STEP 3: Practice Your Description [5 min]

Go through your description aloud, timing yourself.

**Don't record yet**—just practice.

**Check:**
- Do you have enough content for 2 minutes?
- Where are you running short?
- What details can you add?

**Add details if needed:**
- Colors, lighting, mood
- Facial expressions, body language
- Background details
- Personal memories
- Emotions and feelings

#### STEP 4: Record Your Description [3 min]

**Rules:**
- Speak continuously for 2 minutes
- NO pausing for more than 1 second
- Use linking words to connect ideas
- Include descriptive vocabulary
- NO "um" or "uh" (use replacement phrases)

**After recording:**
- Check your timing: _____ minutes / 2:00 target
- Did you speak for the full 2 minutes? YES / NO

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Photo Description

**Photo:** A candid shot of the speaker's family at the beach during sunset

> "**I'd like to describe a photo that's very special to me**—it's a candid photograph of my family taken during our summer vacation last year.
>
> **In the photo**, you can see all four of us—my parents, my younger sister, and myself—standing on a beautiful beach just as the sun was setting. **What strikes me most** about this image is the golden quality of the light—the entire scene is bathed in this warm, orange glow that makes everything look almost magical.
>
> **My parents are standing on either side**, with my mother wearing a light blue summer dress and my father in a white shirt with rolled-up sleeves. **In the center**, my sister and I are laughing at something—**I can't remember now what it was**, but the genuine joy on our faces is unmistakable. My sister has her arm around my shoulders, and we're both looking slightly away from the camera, as if someone just called our names.
>
> **In the background**, you can see the ocean stretching to the horizon, with gentle waves rolling onto the shore. The sky is painted in shades of pink, orange, and deep purple—**it was honestly one of the most beautiful sunsets I've ever witnessed**.
>
> **This photo was taken** in July of last year when we visited the coast for a week-long holiday. **It was actually our mother's birthday**, and we'd spent the entire day at the beach. **I think** what makes this photo so meaningful is that it captures a rare moment when all four of us were genuinely relaxed and happy together.
>
> **Whenever I look at this photo**, I'm immediately transported back to that evening. I can feel the warm sand beneath my feet, smell the salt in the air, and hear the distant sound of children playing. **Most importantly**, it reminds me of how precious these family moments are, especially now that my sister and I are both away at university and don't get to see our parents as often as we'd like.
>
> **Overall**, this photograph represents not just a beautiful memory, but the strong bond my family shares. **Every time I look at it**, I feel grateful for those moments of togetherness that I'll cherish forever."

### Descriptive Vocabulary for Photos

**Describing People:**
- smiling, laughing, looking thoughtful, posing candidly
- in the foreground/background, on the left/right, in the center
- wearing [clothing], with [accessories/hair style]
- facial expressions, body language, gestures

**Describing Settings:**
- indoor/outdoor setting, natural light, artificial lighting
- beach, mountain, cityscape, countryside, room
- in the background, in the foreground, to the side

**Describing Mood/Atmosphere:**
- joyful, peaceful, nostalgic, energetic, somber
- warm, cool, bright, dim, golden hour
- candid, posed, spontaneous, arranged

**Describing Emotions:**
- happy, sad, nostalgic, excited, contemplative
- reminds me of, brings back memories, captures the moment

### Common Photo Description Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Fix |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| **Listing without describing** | "There's a person, a tree, a car..." | Describe WITH detail: "A person wearing..." |
| **Forgetting the personal connection** | Photos are meaningful for a reason | Explain WHY it's special to you |
| **Running out of things to say** | 2 minutes requires content | Prepare more points than you need |
| **Being too vague** | "It's nice" vs. "The golden light creates warmth" | Be specific and descriptive |
| **Speaking too quietly** | Description should be engaging** | Project your voice, show enthusiasm |

### Time Management for 2-Minute Description

| Section | Target Time | What to Include |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Intro** | 15 seconds | What photo, basic context |
| **What's in photo** | 30 seconds | Visual details, people, setting |
| **When/where** | 20 seconds | Context, occasion |
| **Why special** | 30 seconds | Personal meaning, memories |
| **Feelings** | 20 seconds | Then vs. now emotions |
| **Conclusion** | 15 seconds | Summary, final thoughts |
| **Buffer** | 10 seconds | Extra details or extension |

**Total:** 2:20 (target 2:00, aim slightly over)

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. 2 Minutes Is Longer Than You Think**
Most students underestimate how long 2 minutes actually is. When you prepare, aim for 2:20 of content. It's better to have slightly too much than run short.

**2. The Personal Connection Matters**
IELTS examiners want to hear YOUR connection to the topic. A photo description isn't just visual—it's emotional and personal. Share WHY it matters.

**3. Structure Prevents Rambling**
Without a framework, descriptions become random lists. Use WHAT-WHERE-WHO-WHY-HOW to organize your thoughts and the examiner's understanding.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Running out of things to say** | Prepare 7-8 points, not 5. Better to have extra content. |
| **Being too vague** | Use specific descriptive words. "Beautiful" → "Golden light creates warmth" |
| **Forgetting the personal aspect** | This isn't just visual—it's meaningful. Share the emotions. |
| **Speaking too fast** | You'll run out of content in 60 seconds. Pace yourself. |
| **Not practicing timing** | Time your practice. If it's 90 seconds, you need more content. |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Choose a photo with stories**: The more you remember, the more you can say
2. **Use all your senses**: Not just visual—smell, sound, feeling too
3. **Practice with different photos**: Build the skill, not just memorize one description
4. **Time yourself**: Always practice with a timer. Build awareness of 2 minutes.
