## 🎯 Common Chinese Speaker Errors

### TH Sounds (/θ/ and /ð/)

| Error | You Say | Correct | Fix |
|-------|---------|---------|-----|
| th → s | "sink" | "think" (/θɪŋk/) | Tongue BETWEEN teeth, blow air |
| th → z/d | "zis" / "dis" | "this" (/ðɪs/) | Tongue between teeth + VOICE |

**Quick Practice**: "Think **th**rough **th**is. **Th**ose **th**ree **th**ings."

### V Sound (/v/)

| Error | You Say | Correct | Fix |
|-------|---------|---------|-----|
| v → w | "wery" | "very" (/vɛri/) | Top teeth ON bottom lip |

**Quick Practice**: "Very **v**ia **v**ideo **v**oice."

### A Sound (/æ/)

| Error | You Say | Correct | Fix |
|-------|---------|---------|-----|
| æ → e | "men" (for man) | "man" (/mæn/) | Open mouth WIDE |

**Words to watch**: man, cat, that, have, can, back

### R vs L Confusion

| Sound | Position | Fix |
|-------|----------|-----|
| **/r/** | Tongue curled BACK, don't touch roof | "Pull back!" |
| **/l/** | Tongue tip BEHIND front teeth | "Tap forward!" |

**Minimal Pairs**:
- rice / lice
- read / lead
- right / light
- long / wrong

### Word Endings (-ed, -s)

| Error | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Dropping -ed | Exaggerate: "watch**t**", "ask**t**" |
| Dropping -s | "book**s**", "want**s**" |
| -ed confusion | watch**t** (/t/), need**ed** (/ɪd/), play**d** (/d/) |

| Sound | Mouth Position | Self-Check |
|-------|---------------|------------|
| **/θ/** (think) | Tongue BETWEEN teeth | See tongue in mirror |
| **/ð/** (this) | Tongue between teeth + VOICE | Feel throat vibration |
| **/æ/** (man) | Mouth WIDE open | 2-finger width |
| **/v/** (very) | Top teeth on bottom lip | Feel lip vibration |

## 🔉 Schwa /ə/ - The Secret to Natural English

### What is Schwa?

The **most common sound** in English - a weak "uh" sound found in unstressed syllables.

### Common Schwa Words

| Word | Strong | Weak (Schwa) |
|------|--------|---------------|
| a | /eɪ/ | /ə/ (a book) |
| the | /ðiː/ | /ðə/ (the cat) |
| and | /ænd/ | /ən/ / /ənd/ |
| for | /fɔːr/ | /fər/ |
| to | /tuː/ | /tə/ |
| at | /æt/ | /ət/ |

### Quick Practice

**Without schwa** (robotic): "I want **to** go **to** **the** park."

**With schwa** (natural): "I want **tə** go **tə** **ðə** park."

## 🎤 Mock Exam Structure & Timing

### Full Exam Breakdown

| Part | Duration | What to Do | Time Management |
|------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **Part 1** | 4-5 min | Quick Q&A, familiar topics | 2-3 sentences per answer |
| **Part 2** | 3-4 min | 1 min prep + 2 min speak | Must speak FULL 2 minutes |
| **Part 3** | 4-5 min | Discussion, abstract ideas | 3-4 sentences per answer |

### Part 2 Timing Strategy

```
0:00-0:30: What it is + basic description
0:30-1:00: When/Where/Who with + story
1:00-1:30: Why important + details
1:30-2:00: Feelings + opinion + future
```

**If you finish early**: Add more details using:
- "Additionally..."
- "What I also love about it is..."
- "Another reason is..."

## 🎯 Part 3 Answer Templates

### Opinion + Reason + Example + Extension

```markdown
**Opinion**: "From my perspective, [opinion]."
**Reason**: "This is because [reason]."
**Example**: "For instance, [example]."
**Extension**: "So, ultimately, [extended thought]."
```

**Full Example**:
> "From my perspective, remote work **has both advantages and disadvantages**. This is mainly because **it offers flexibility but can lead to isolation**. For instance, **people can work from anywhere, but might miss social interactions**. So, ultimately, **companies need to find a balance**."

### Common Part 3 Question Types

| Question Type | Quick Formula |
|---------------|---------------|
| **Compare** | "Both X and Y have similarities, but X... whereas Y..." |
| **Predict** | "It's likely that... which could lead to..." |
| **Cause/Effect** | "There are several factors. Firstly... Additionally..." |
| **Agree/Disagree** | "I tend to agree because... However,..." |

## 🎼 Prosody: The "Music" of English

### Stress Patterns

**Sentence Stress**: Stress 1-2 words per sentence

❌ "I **want** to **go** to **the** **market**." (robotic)

✅ "I **WANT** to go to the **MAR**ket." (natural)

### Intonation Patterns

| Type | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Questions** | RISE at end | "Do you like **coffee**?" ↗ |
| **Statements** | FALL at end | "I like **tea**." ↘ |
| **Lists** | RISE, RISE, FALL | "Apples, bananas, and **pears**." ↗↗↘ |

### Thought Groups

Break long sentences into chunks with brief pauses:

> "In my opinion [pause] reading is essential [pause] because it improves vocabulary [pause] and enhances critical thinking."

## 🔧 Self-Correction Techniques

### Real-Time Monitoring

| When to Check | What to Do |
|---------------|------------|
| **Before speaking** | Quick mouth position check (1 second) |
| **While speaking** | If you miss a sound, keep going - rhythm > perfection |
| **After speaking** | Note problem sounds for ELSA practice |

### The "Anchor Word" Method

Choose an easy word for each problem sound as your reference:

- /θ/: "think" → say before hard words
- /æ/: "cat" → reset your mouth
- /v/: "very" → feel the position

**Practice**: "I **think** (touch teeth)... **cat** (open wide)... **very** (bite lip)... that works!"
