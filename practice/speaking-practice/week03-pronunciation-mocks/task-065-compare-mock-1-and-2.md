# Task 65: Compare Mock ① and Mock ② Scores

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [4 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why compare mock exams?**

Week 3 focused on pronunciation and exam skills. Comparing your two mock exams shows:
- **Real progress** (not just perceived progress)
- **What strategies worked** (filler replacement, weak forms, etc.)
- **Areas needing more work** (not everything improves at once)
- **Confidence growth** (often the biggest improvement)

### What You're Looking For

| Dimension | Mock ① | Mock ② | What Changed? |
|-----------|--------|--------|---------------|
| **Fluency** | Baseline score | New score | Fillers reduced? |
| **Pronunciation** | Baseline score | New score | Problem sounds improved? |
| **Vocabulary** | Baseline score | New score | Range increased? |
| **Part 2 length** | Under 2 min? | Closer to 2 min? | Better pacing? |
| **Part 3 depth** | Brief answers? | Extended answers? | Structure applied? |

### The Progress Mindset

**Important:** Some scores may stay the same or even drop slightly. This is normal! Focus on:
- Overall trends (not small fluctuations)
- Specific improvements (e.g., fewer fillers)
- Confidence and comfort (hard to measure but real)

### What Good Progress Looks Like

**Substantial Improvement:**
- Fluency score up 1+ points
- Filler count down 50%+
- Part 2 consistently reaching 2 minutes
- Part 3 answers averaging 3+ sentences

**Moderate Improvement:**
- Fluency score up 0.5-1 points
- Filler count down 25-50%
- Part 2 getting closer to 2 minutes
- Part 3 showing more structure

**Small Improvement (Still Progress!):**
- Fluency score up 0.2-0.5 points
- Filler count down slightly
- More aware of problems (even if not fixed)
- Feeling more comfortable

**No Improvement (or Regression):**
- This happens! Don't panic. Ask:
- Was I tired/stressed during one exam?
- Was one topic much harder?
- Am I being too critical?

### Interpreting Score Changes

| Score Change | Interpretation | What It Means |
|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| +2 points | Major improvement | Excellent progress! |
| +1 point | Solid improvement | On track! |
| +0.5 point | Modest improvement | Keep working |
| 0 change | Maintaining level | Not regression, but need new focus |
| -0.5 point | Small drop | Normal fluctuation, don't worry |
| -1 point | Noticeable drop | Investigate cause (fatigue? harder topic?) |

### Progress Celebration Guide

**Celebrate these wins:**
- Filler reduction of any amount
- Part 2 hitting 2 minutes
- Any pronunciation sound improvement
- Feeling more confident
- Using replacement phrases successfully
- Extended Part 3 answers

**Remember:** Progress isn't always linear. Some weeks show big gains, others show consolidation. Both are valuable!

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Comparison Is Motivation**
Seeing your progress in black and white is incredibly motivating. Even small improvements are real progress. Celebrate them!

**2. Identifying What Works**
By comparing, you see which strategies had real impact:
- Did filler replacement actually reduce "um/uh"?
- Did extended answer structure improve Part 3?
- Did ELSA practice fix specific sounds?

This helps you focus on what WORKS, not just what you "should" do.

**3. Regression Is Normal**
If one score dropped slightly, don't panic. Many factors affect a single exam:
- Time of day
- Energy level
- Topic familiarity
- Nerves

Look at overall trends, not single data points.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Being too critical** | Look at overall trends, not every tiny fluctuation |
| **Ignoring small improvements** | A 0.3 point improvement IS progress. Count it! |
| **Comparing to perfection** | Compare to YOUR baseline, not to Band 9 |
| **Focusing only on scores** | Confidence and comfort matter too |
| **Discouraged by plateau** | Scores sometimes plateau before the next breakthrough. Keep going! |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Track metrics**—filler count, Part 2 length, sentences per answer. Numbers don't lie.
2. **Celebrate wins**—write down your improvements. Positive reinforcement works.
3. **Analyze what worked**—if you improved, what caused it? Do more of that.
4. **Plan Week 4 focus**—based on areas still needing work
