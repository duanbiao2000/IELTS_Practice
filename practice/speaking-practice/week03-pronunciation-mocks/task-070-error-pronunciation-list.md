# Task 70: Compile Error Pronunciation List

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [3 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why track pronunciation errors?**

You can't fix what you don't track. By creating a personal error list:
- **See your patterns** (which sounds you struggle with)
- **Target your practice** (focus on YOUR problem words)
- **Measure progress** (see the list shrink over time)
- **Build awareness** (know your weaknesses)

### What Counts as an "Error Pronunciation"?

- Words ELSA marks as low score
- Words you mispronounce regularly
- Words you avoid saying because you're unsure
- Words others have corrected you on
- Words you've noticed sound wrong when recorded

### Your Error List Categories

Organize by sound type:
- **Vowel errors** (æ, e, ʌ, etc.)
- **Consonant errors** (th, r/l, v/w, etc.)
- **Stress errors** (wrong syllable emphasis)
- **Weak form errors** (not using schwa)
- **Other** (linking, intonation, etc.)

### Common Error Patterns by Sound

**/θ/ (think) vs /s/ (sink):**
- think, three, through, thought, anything
- Common for Chinese speakers

**/æ/ (cat) vs /e/ (bed):**
- bad, man, that, happy, family
- Common for Chinese speakers

**/ʌ/ (cup) vs /ɑː/ (car):**
- much, cup, love, come, some
- Common for Chinese speakers

**/r/ vs /l/:**
- world, girl, really, grill, collect
- Common for Chinese speakers

**/v/ vs /w/:**
- very, video, view, versus, village
- Common for some dialects

### How to Use Your Error List

**Weekly Practice:**
1. Pick your top 5 priority words
2. Practice each 20 times in ELSA
3. Use the words in sentences
4. Re-test after 1 week
5. If improved → remove from list
6. If not improved → keep for next week

**Monthly Review:**
- How many words removed? _____
- How many new words added? _____
- Which sound patterns are most problematic? _____

### Error List Maintenance

**When to REMOVE a word:**
- ELSA score 85%+
- You can use it naturally in speech
- You've tested it 3+ times with consistent high scores

**When to ADD a word:**
- New error discovered
- Word appears consistently low in ELSA
- Someone corrects your pronunciation
- You notice it in recordings

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Your Error List Is Personal**
Everyone has different pronunciation errors. Your list should reflect YOUR specific problems, not generic lists from textbooks. Track what's hard for YOU.

**2. Focus on High-Frequency Words**
Your error list should prioritize words you actually use. Don't waste time fixing low-frequency words you'll never say in IELTS.

**3. The List Should Shrink Over Time**
If you're practicing correctly, words should come OFF your list faster than new ones go ON. If the list only grows, you need to change your practice approach.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Not tracking specific errors** | Be specific: "think → /θ/ not /s/" not just "think is wrong" |
| **Listing too many words** | Focus on 15-20. Quality over quantity. Fix these first. |
| **Not updating the list** | Review weekly. Remove fixed words, add new problems. |
| **Only listing, not fixing** | The list is for TARGETING, not just tracking. Practice these words! |
| **Including words you never use** | Prioritize high-frequency words you'll actually need in IELTS |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Categorize by sound**—not randomly. This reveals your problem patterns.
2. **Prioritize ruthlessly**—fix top 5 first, then move to next 5.
3. **Test with ELSA**—don't guess. Get real data.
4. **Use words in sentences**—isolation practice isn't enough.
5. **Celebrate removals**—when a word comes off the list, that's victory!
