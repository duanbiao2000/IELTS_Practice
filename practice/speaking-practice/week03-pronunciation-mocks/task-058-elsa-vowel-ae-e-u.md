## рЯУЪ PRE-STUDY [4 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why these three vowels matter?**

For Chinese speakers, /√¶/ (cat), /e/ (bed), and / М/ (cup) are among the most confused vowel sounds. Mastering this distinction is crucial because:
- **Minimal pairs** are everywhere: man vs. men, bad vs. bed, cat vs. cut
- **Mispronunciation changes meaning**: "I'm bad" vs. "I'm bed"
- **These are HIGH-FREQUENCY vowels**вАФthey appear constantly in English

### The Three Vowels Explained

| Symbol | Example Word | Sound Description | Common Mistake | IPA Details |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|----------------|-------------|
| **/√¶/** | cat, bad, man | Mouth wide open, tongue low and forward | Often confused with /e/ | Front vowel, open |
| **/e/** | bed, men, head | Mouth half-open, tongue mid-position | Often confused with /√¶/ or /e…™/ | Front vowel, mid-open |
| **/ М/** | cup, run, love | Mouth relaxed, tongue central | Often confused with /…СЋР/ (car) | Central vowel, mid-open |

### Chinese Speaker Error Patterns for These Vowels

| Error | You Say | Should Be | Why It Happens | Physical Fix |
|-------|---------|-----------|----------------|--------------|
| **/√¶/ вЖТ /e/** | "bed" instead of "bad" | /b√¶d/ | No /√¶/ in Mandarin | Open mouth WIDE like at dentist |
| **/√¶/ вЖТ /e/** | "men" instead of "man" | /m√¶n/ | Same as above | Stretch mouth corners, lower jaw |

| **/ М/ вЖТ /√¶/** | "cap" instead of "cup" | /k Мp/ | Similar mouth positions | Don't open as wide as /√¶/ |

### Mouth Position Reference - Physical Cues

**For /√¶/ (cat, bad, man):**
- Jaw: OPEN WIDE (like saying "ah" at doctor)
- Tongue: LOW and FORWARD
- Lips: Spread (slight smile)
- Physical cue: Put 2 vertical fingers between teeth

**For /e/ (bed, men, head):**
- Jaw: HALF open (relaxed)
- Tongue: MID position (not high, not low)
- Lips: Neutral
- Physical cue: Put 1 horizontal finger between teeth

**For / М/ (cup, run, love):**
- Jaw: Relaxed (neutral position)
- Tongue: CENTER of mouth (not forward, not back)
- Lips: Neutral (no rounding, no spreading)
- Physical cue: Say "uh" naturally - that's / М/

## рЯТ° COACH'S NOTES

### рЯОУ Teaching Points

**1. The /√¶/ Sound Doesn't Exist in Mandarin**
This is why it's so difficult. Your brain has never needed to make this sound before. You're building a new muscle movementвАФexpect it to feel strange at first.

**2. Physical Cues Help**
- /√¶/: Put your fingers vertically between your teeth. Your jaw should open wide.
- /e/: Put two fingers horizontally. Your jaw opens to that width.
- / М/: Jaw relaxed, like you're about to say "uh..." while thinking

**3. Listen Before You Speak**
For each word, listen to ELSA's model 2 times BEFORE you record. If you can't hear the difference, you can't produce it.

### рЯФІ Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Not opening wide enough for /√¶/** | Physical cue: pretend you're at the dentist |
| **Confusing /e/ with /e…™/** | /e/ is SHORT. /e…™/ is a diphthong (two sounds). Keep /e/ short |
| **Making / М/ too long** | / М/ is always short. Quick and relaxed |
| **Only practicing in isolation** | Practice in words, then sentences. Context matters |
| **Giving up after low scores** | These are hard sounds! 20+ reps needed. Keep going |
