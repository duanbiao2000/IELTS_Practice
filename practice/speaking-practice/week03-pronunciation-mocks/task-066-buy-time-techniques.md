# Task 66: "Buy Time" Techniques

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [4 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why "buy time" techniques matter?**

IELTS examiners ask complex questions. Sometimes you need 2-3 seconds to think. "Buy time" phrases:
- **Sound professional** (not "um... uh...")
- **Give you thinking space** (2-3 seconds legally)
- **Impress examiners** (shows sophisticated language)
- **Reduce anxiety** (you have a plan for thinking time)

### The "Buy Time" Strategy

Instead of: "Um... uh... let me see..."
Use: "That's an interesting question. Let me think about that for a moment."

**The difference:**
- Um/uh = sounds unprepared, lowers fluency score
- Buy time phrases = sounds thoughtful, professional

### 6 Essential "Buy Time" Phrases

| Phrase | When to Use | Time Gained |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| "That's an interesting question..." | Starting any answer | 2 seconds |
| "Let me think about that..." | Genuinely processing | 2-3 seconds |
| "From my perspective..." | Introducing opinion | 1-2 seconds |
| "That's a complex issue..." | Big, abstract questions | 2 seconds |
| "I've never thought about that before, but..." | Truly stumped | 3 seconds |
| "Well, there are several factors to consider..." | Questions with multiple parts | 2 seconds |

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Buy-Time Phrase Usage Examples

**Example 1: Complex Question**
**Q:** "How will artificial intelligence change job markets?"

> "**That's a complex issue** with many dimensions to consider. **From my perspective**, AI will likely eliminate some routine jobs while creating new types of employment we can't yet imagine. **For example**, self-driving technology might reduce truck driving jobs but create demand for AI maintenance specialists. **I believe** the key will be adaptability—workers will need to continuously learn new skills throughout their careers."

**Example 2: Totally Unexpected Question**
**Q:** "If animals could talk, which species would be the rudest?"

> "**I've never thought about that before**, but that's a fascinating question. **Let me think about it for a moment**... **Well**, I suppose cats might be quite rude if they could talk, given how aloof and dismissive they can be with humans. **Then again**, maybe certain birds like crows would be sarcastic, given how intelligent and mischievous they are in nature. **That's an interesting thought experiment**!"

**Example 3: Opinion-Based Question**
**Q:** "Should university education be free for everyone?"

> "**That's an interesting question** that touches on social equity and economic policy. **From my perspective**, making university education free would increase accessibility and allow more people to reach their potential regardless of their financial background. **However**, we'd also need to consider how to fund such a system—probably through higher taxes. **There are several factors to consider**, including the long-term economic benefits of a more educated population versus the immediate costs."

### Phrase Selection Guide

| Question Type | Best Buy-Time Phrase | Why |
|---------------|---------------------|-----|
| **Complex/abstract** | "That's a complex issue..." | Acknowledges difficulty |
| **Compliment to examiner** | "That's an interesting question..." | Professional and polite |
| **Opinion needed** | "From my perspective..." | Signals personal view |
| **Genuinely stumped** | "I've never considered that..." | Honest, not defensive |
| **Multi-part question** | "There are several factors..." | Shows you understand complexity |
| **Need thinking time** | "Let me think about that..." | Explicit but professional |

### Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Better Approach |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Using "um" after buy-time phrase** | Defeats the purpose | Use the phrase, then SILENCE (2-3 seconds) |
| **Rushing after the phrase** | Don't buy time if you won't use it | Actually pause and think |
| **Overusing one phrase** | Sounds repetitive | Rotate all 6 phrases |
| **Sounding defensive** | "I don't know, but..." | Sound curious: "I've never considered that, but..." |
| **Forgetting to actually answer** | The phrase is a bridge, not the destination | Phrase → Think → Answer |

### Silent Pauses vs. Buy-Time Phrases

| Situation | Best Approach |
|-----------|---------------|
| Need 1 second | Silent pause (no phrase needed) |
| Need 2-3 seconds | Buy-time phrase |
| Need 3+ seconds | "Let me think about that for a moment" + pause |
| Totally stuck | "I've never considered that..." + honest attempt |

### Sample Timeline

**Without buy-time phrase:**
> Examiner: "How will AI change society?"
> Student: "Um, uh, well, I think..." (sounds unprepared)

**With buy-time phrase:**
> Examiner: "How will AI change society?"

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Silence Is Better Than "Um"**
When you need thinking time, a brief silence is better than "um." But a buy-time phrase is better than both—it shows you're consciously processing, not stuck.

**2. These Phrases Sound Sophisticated**
Buy-time phrases aren't just functional—they're impressive. They show you have professional language resources. Examiners notice this.

**3. Practice Until Automatic**
You won't remember these in the moment unless you practice. Each phrase should feel automatic—no thinking required to say it.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Using "um" right after the phrase** | The phrase REPLACES "um." If you use both, you missed the point |
| **Not actually taking the thinking time** | Pause for 2-3 REAL seconds after the phrase |
| **Using only one phrase** | Rotate all 6. Variety sounds more natural |
| **Sounding robotic** | Practice until phrases sound natural. Record and check |
| **Forgetting in the moment** | Start EVERY answer with "Well..." for one week. Build the habit |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **The "Well" Default**: If you forget everything else, start with "Well..." It works for almost any question.
2. **Practice with a timer**: Give yourself exactly 2 seconds of thinking time after the phrase.
3. **Body language**: Use a thoughtful facial expression when buying time. It reinforces the message.
4. **Combine phrases**: "That's an interesting question. Let me think about that..."
