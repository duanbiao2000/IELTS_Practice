# Part 3 Quick Reference

> The Discussion - Handle abstract questions with extended, reasoned answers!

---

## 📋 Part 3 Format Overview

| Time | What You Do |
|------|-------------|
| **4-5 minutes** | Discussion with examiner |
| **Topic** | Related to Part 2, but more abstract |
| **Questions** | Usually 3-4 questions |
| **Expectation** | Extended answers (3-4 sentences minimum) |

**Key Difference from Part 1**: Part 1 = personal experience; Part 3 = abstract ideas, broader scope

---

## 🎯 The ORE Method (Opinion + Reason + Example)

For most Part 3 questions, use this structure:

```
O - Opinion: "From my perspective..." / "I believe that..."
R - Reason: "The main reason is..." / "This is because..."
E - Example: "For instance..." / "Take... for example..."
```

### Full Example

**Question**: "Do you think reading habits are changing in your country?"

```
O: "From my perspective, reading habits are indeed changing
   significantly in my country."

R: "The main reason is the widespread use of digital devices.
   Most people now consume content through smartphones and
   tablets rather than physical books."

E: "For instance, e-book apps and social media have become
   the primary source of information for younger generations."
```

**Total**: 3 sentences, ~30 seconds → Good answer!

## 🔄 Common Question Types & Formulas

### 1. Compare Questions

**Formula**: "Both X and Y have similarities, but X... whereas Y..."

| Example | Response |
|---------|----------|
| Living in city vs countryside | "Both city and countryside living have advantages. The city offers convenience and job opportunities, whereas the countryside provides peace and a healthier environment." |
| Online vs traditional shopping | "Both online and traditional shopping serve different needs. Online shopping offers convenience and variety, whereas traditional shopping allows you to see and touch products before buying." |

**Key phrases**:
- "Unlike X, Y..."
- "Compared to X, Y..."
- "While X is..., Y is..."

---

### 2. Predict/Future Questions

**Formula**: "It's likely that... which could lead to... For example..."

| Example | Response |
|---------|----------|
| Future of transport | "It's likely that electric vehicles will become more common, which could lead to reduced pollution in cities. For example, many countries are already planning to ban gasoline cars by 2030." |
| Future of work | "Remote work will probably continue growing, which could lead to changes in urban planning. We might see fewer office buildings and more shared workspaces in residential areas." |

**Key phrases**:
- "In the future, I expect..."
- "It's probable that..."
- "We might see..."
- "...could potentially lead to..."

---

### 3. Cause/Effect Questions

**Formula**: "There are several factors. Firstly... Additionally... This means..."

| Example | Response |
|---------|----------|
| Why do people change jobs? | "There are several factors. Firstly, better salary and career growth are major motivations. Additionally, some people seek better work-life balance or more meaningful work. This means companies need to offer competitive packages to retain talent." |
| Effects of technology on education | "Technology has impacted education in multiple ways. Firstly, it provides access to vast information resources. Additionally, it enables personalized learning experiences. However, it also raises concerns about screen time and social interaction." |

**Key phrases**:
- "One major factor is..."
- "Another reason is..."
- "This leads to..."
- "As a result..."
- "Consequently..."

---

### 4. Agree/Disagree Questions

**Formula**: "I tend to agree/disagree because... However, I also understand..."

| Example | Response |
|---------|----------|
| Should university be free? | "I tend to agree that university should be free because it ensures equal access to education regardless of financial background. However, I also understand that free education requires significant government investment, which might impact other areas of the budget." |
| Is tourism always good? | "I'd say tourism is not always beneficial. While it brings economic benefits and cultural exchange, overtourism can damage local environments and disrupt communities. So it needs to be managed carefully." |

**Key phrases**:
- "I completely agree..."
- "I partially agree..."
- "I'm inclined to think..."
- "On the other hand..."
- "That said, I also..."

---

### 5. Speculation Questions

**Formula**: "It's hard to say for certain, but I imagine... Possibly..."

| Example | Response |
|---------|----------|
| Will handwriting disappear? | "It's hard to say for certain, but I imagine handwriting might become less common in daily life. However, I don't think it will disappear entirely because it's still important for education and personal expression." |
| What will future cities look like? | "I can only speculate, but future cities might be more eco-friendly with more green spaces and sustainable transportation. They'll likely be smarter with integrated technology for efficient resource management." |

**Key phrases**:
- "It's difficult to predict..."
- "I can only speculate..."
- "It's possible that..."
- "...might potentially..."
- "One possibility is..."

## 🌟 Band 7+ Vocabulary & Phrases

### Opening Your Response

| Band 6 | Band 7+ |
|--------|---------|
| "I think..." | "From my perspective..." / "As I see it..." |
| "I agree..." | "I couldn't agree more..." / "I tend to agree..." |
| "In my opinion..." | "Ideally speaking..." / "Generally speaking..." |

### Giving Reasons

| Band 6 | Band 7+ |
|--------|---------|
| "Because..." | "The primary reason is..." / "This is mainly because..." |
| "So..." | "Consequently..." / "As a result..." / "Therefore..." |

### Adding Examples

| Band 6 | Band 7+ |
|--------|---------|
| "For example..." | "To illustrate..." / "Take... for example..." |
| "Like..." | "A case in point is..." |

### Showing Contrast

| Band 6 | Band 7+ |
|--------|---------|
| "But..." | "Having said that..." / "On the other hand..." / "Conversely..." |
| "However..." | "Nevertheless..." / "That said..." |

---

## 🎯 Extending Your Answer

When you finish your main points, add:

| Technique | Phrase |
|-----------|--------|
| **Add another perspective** | "Another thing to consider is..." |
| **Add a personal connection** | "From my own experience..." |
| **Add a condition** | "This is especially true when..." |
| **Add a consequence** | "This leads to..." |
| **Add a nuance** | "Of course, this depends on..." |

---

## 🚫 Common Part 3 Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | Fix |
|---------|--------------|-----|
| **1-sentence answers** | Doesn't show range | Aim for 3-4 sentences minimum |
| **Too brief** | Lower Task Achievement score | Always explain WHY with reasons |
| **Too personal** | Part 3 is abstract, not about YOU | Connect personal to broader issues |
| **No examples** | Less convincing | Always add "For instance..." |
| **Going off-topic** | Confusion | Stick to the question asked |
| **Being silent** | Appears unprepared | Say "That's an interesting question, let me think..." |

## 💡 Quick Templates for Common Topics

### Technology & Society

```
"Technology has significantly impacted [topic]. On one hand,
it has [benefit]. However, it also has [drawback]. I believe
we need to [balance]."
```

### Education

```
"Education is crucial for [purpose]. While traditional methods
have value, I think modern approaches like [approach] can
enhance learning. The key is finding the right balance."
```

### Environment

```
"Environmental issues are becoming increasingly urgent.
I believe both individuals and governments have responsibilities.
Individuals can [actions], while governments should [policies]."
```

### Work & Careers

```
"The world of work is changing rapidly. Factors like [factor 1]
and [factor 2] are reshaping how we work. I think this trend
will continue, which means [consequence]."
```

### Culture & Traditions

```
"Traditions play an important role in [context]. They provide
[benefits]. However, I also believe traditions should evolve
to reflect modern values and lifestyles."
```
