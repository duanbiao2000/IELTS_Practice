### Pronunciation Dimensions Explained

| Dimension | What It Is | How to Measure Progress |
|-----------|------------|------------------------|
| **Individual sounds** | Vowels, consonants (/├ж/, /╬╕/, /r/ vs /l/) | ELSA scores, fewer corrections needed |
| **Word stress** | Emphasis on syllables (PHO-to-graph) | Recording yourself, comparing to native |
| **Sentence stress** | 1-2 keywords stressed per sentence | ELSA feedback, rhythm awareness |
| **Intonation** | Pitch variation (questions, emotion) | Less flat speech in recordings |
| **Rhythm** | Linking, weak forms, timing | Flowing speech, not choppy |
| **Fluency** | Fillers reduced, smooth delivery | Filler count, continuous speech |

## ЁЯТб COACH'S NOTES

### ЁЯОУ Teaching Points

**1. Review Is Essential for Progress**
You can't improve what you don't measure. This comprehensive review shows you what actually worked, not just what you think worked. Use this data to plan Week 4.

**2. Focus on What Worked**
If ELSA practice gave you +20 points on /├ж/, do MORE ELSA practice. If filler replacement cut your fillers by 70%, keep using those phrases. Double down on what works.

**3. Pronunciation Takes Time**
Week 3 was just the start. Pronunciation improvements continue for months. But you've built the foundationтАФawareness, tools, and practice habits. That's huge.

### ЁЯФз Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Only looking at scores** | Look at skills too. Confidence, comfort, awareness matter. |
