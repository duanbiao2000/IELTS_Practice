# Task 72: Shadowing Day 14 - Normal Speed + Answer Related Question

## 📚 PRE-STUDY [4 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why add questions after shadowing?**

Shadowing builds copying skills. Answering questions builds RESPONSE skills. By combining them:
- **Test comprehension** (did you understand what you shadowed?)
- **Practice speaking** (responding to content)
- **Bridge input and output** (hearing → speaking)
- **Simulate IELTS** (hear → respond)

### The Shadow + Respond Protocol

1. **Shadow a segment** (copy the speech)
2. **Answer a question about it** (respond to content)
3. **Check accuracy** (did you understand correctly?)

This mirrors IELTS:
- Examiner asks (input)
- You process (understand)
- You respond (output)

### Normal Speed Shadowing

By Day 14, you should shadow at **normal speed**:
- No slowing down the audio
- No pausing between phrases
- Continuous flow shadowing
- Native-like pace

## 📝 MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Sample Shadowing + Question Session

**Segment:** TED Talk intro about "The Power of Sleep"

**Shadowing (normal speed):**
[Copied speech about importance of sleep for brain function]

**Comprehension Questions & Answers:**

**Q1:** What was the speaker's main point?
> **A:** The speaker argued that sleep is essential for brain health and cognitive function, not just rest.

**Q2:** What example did they give?
> **A:** They mentioned research showing that people who sleep less than 7 hours have reduced concentration and memory problems.

**Q3:** What was their opinion?
> **A:** They believe society needs to take sleep more seriously and prioritize it as much as diet and exercise.

**Opinion Questions & Responses:**

**Q4:** Do you agree?
> **A:** **Well**, I definitely agree that sleep is important. **From my perspective**, I've noticed that when I don't get enough sleep, my focus and productivity definitely suffer. **However**, I also think some people naturally need less sleep than others.

**Q5:** How does this relate to you?
> **A:** **Actually**, this directly relates to my experience as a student. I've found that pulling all-nighters before exams is counterproductive—**what works better** is getting good sleep and studying consistently over time.

**Q6:** What would you add?
> **A:** **I think** I would add that the quality of sleep matters just as much as the quantity. **For example**, creating a dark, quiet sleep environment has made a huge difference for me personally.

### Comprehension Level Guide

| Comprehension | What It Looks Like | Band Score Equivalent |
|--------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **100%** | All main points and details captured | Band 8+ |
| **80%** | Main points clear, some details missed | Band 7 |
| **60%** | General idea understood, specifics unclear | Band 6 |
| **40%** | Only vague sense of topic | Band 5 |
| **20%** | Minimal understanding | Below Band 5 |

### Question Types for Practice

**Comprehension Questions (test understanding):**
- What was the main topic?
- What examples did they give?
- What was their conclusion?
- What was their opinion?
- How did they support their argument?

**Opinion Questions (practice response):**
- Do you agree or disagree? Why?
- How does this relate to your experience?
- What would you add to this?
- Can you think of a counterexample?
- How is this relevant to your country/situation?

**Extension Questions (build fluency):**
- What are the implications of...?
- How might this apply to...?
- What would happen if...?
- Why do you think that is?

### Common Shadowing Challenges at Normal Speed

| Challenge | Why It Happens | Fix |
|-----------|----------------|-----|
| **Falling behind** | Can't keep pace with speaker | Start with shorter segments, build stamina |
| **Missing words** | Processing too slow | Focus on key words, not every word |
| **Losing focus** | Attention drifts | Choose engaging content, shorter segments |
| **Pronunciation suffering** | Speed affects accuracy | Slow is better than sloppy. Find right speed. |
| **Not understanding** | Too focused on copying | Listen first, understand, THEN shadow |

## 💡 COACH'S NOTES

### 🎓 Teaching Points

**1. Shadowing + Questions = Input + Output**
Shadowing is input (copying). Answering questions is output (original speech). IELTS requires both—you need to understand the examiner AND respond. This practice bridges the gap.

**2. Normal Speed Is the Goal**
By Day 14, you should be approaching normal speed. If you're still slowing down the audio, that's OK—but keep pushing toward real-time copying. That's what exam day requires.

**3. Comprehension Matters**
If you can shadow perfectly but don't understand what you're saying, that's not full skill. The questions test whether you're processing meaning, not just sounds.

### 🔧 Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Shadowing without understanding** | Listen FIRST for comprehension, THEN shadow |
| **Rushing through questions** | Take time to formulate good answers. Quality over speed. |
| **Not recording responses** | Record and review. Are you actually improving? |
| **Choosing too-difficult content** | If you can't understand, choose simpler material |
| **Only doing one segment** | Two segments minimum. Build stamina. |

### 🎯 Pro Tips

1. **Content first, shadowing second**: Understand what they're saying, then copy it
2. **Varied content**: Mix topics—news, stories, opinions, explanations
3. **Question variety**: Mix comprehension and opinion questions
4. **Record your responses**: Compare Day 1 vs. Day 14. You'll hear progress
