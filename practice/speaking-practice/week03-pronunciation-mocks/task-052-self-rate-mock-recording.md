# Task 52: Self-Rate Your Mock Recording

## ЁЯУЪ PRE-STUDY [5 minutes]

### Before You Begin
**Why self-rating matters?**

You cannot improve what you don't measure. Self-rating your recording develops:
- **Metacognitive awareness**: Noticing what you actually sound like
- **Objective evaluation**: Moving from "I felt bad" to "I hesitated 8 times in Part 2"
- **Targeted improvement**: Identifying specific skills to focus on

### The Three Rating Dimensions

| Dimension | What It Measures | IELTS Band Descriptor Connection |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Fluency** | Speech flow, hesitation, self-correction, pace | "Speaks fluently with only rare repetition or self-correction" |
| **Pronunciation** | Sound production, intonation, stress, rhythm | "Uses a full range of pronunciation features with sustained control" |
| **Vocabulary** | Word choice, range, precision, collocation | "Uses a wide range of vocabulary flexibly and accurately" |

## ЁЯУЭ MODEL ANSWER & REFERENCE

### Rating Rubric: What Each Score Means

#### FLUENCY RUBRIC

| Score | What It Sounds Like |
|-------|---------------------|
| **9-10** | Smooth speech, rare hesitation, natural pace, no long pauses |
| **7-8** | Generally fluent, some hesitation but doesn't disrupt meaning |
| **5-6** | Noticeable pauses, some restarts, occasional slow-downs |
| **3-4** | Frequent pauses, several restarts, slow pace affects communication |
| **1-2** | Very halting, many long pauses, difficult to follow |

#### PRONUNCIATION RUBRIC

| Score | What It Sounds Like |
|-------|---------------------|
| **9-10** | All sounds clear, varied intonation, natural stress and rhythm |
| **7-8** | Mostly clear, some minor sound issues, reasonable intonation |
| **5-6** | Some unclear sounds, somewhat flat intonation, inconsistent stress |
| **3-4** | Frequent unclear sounds, monotonous, stress problems |
| **1-2** | Many sounds unclear, difficult to understand |

#### VOCABULARY RUBRIC

| Score | What It Sounds Like |
|-------|---------------------|
| **9-10** | Wide range, precise choices, natural collocations, some idiomatic language |
| **7-8** | Good range, generally appropriate, some attempts at sophisticated vocabulary |
| **5-6** | Adequate for task, some repetition, mostly simple vocabulary |
| **3-4** | Limited range, frequent repetition, basic vocabulary only |
| **1-2** | Very limited vocabulary, struggles to express ideas |

## ЁЯТб COACH'S NOTES

### ЁЯОУ Teaching Points

**1. The "Cringe Factor" is Normal**
> Everyone hates hearing their own voice at first. It sounds higher and stranger than you expect. This is universalтАФdon't let it prevent you from analyzing objectively.

**2. Score Honestly, Not Harshly**
- Don't score yourself 3/10 just because you're not perfect
- Score 7/10 means "good with room for improvement"
- Use the rubric, not your feelings

**3. Count Specific Problems**
Instead of "I paused too much," count: "I paused 8 times in Part 2."
Specific data = targeted improvement

### ЁЯФз Common Pitfalls & Fixes

| Pitfall | Coach's Fix |
|---------|-------------|
| **Being too harsh on yourself** | Use the rubric objectively. 7/10 is a solid score! |
| **Focusing only on vocabulary** | Fluency and pronunciation count for 50% of your score |
| **Not listening to the full recording** | Every part matters. Don't skip Part 3 analysis |
| **Rating based on how you FELT** | Rate what you actually HEARD on the recording |
| **Comparing to native speakers** | Compare to the rubric, not to perfection |

### ЁЯОп Pro Tips

1. **The pause button is your friend**тАФpause after every question to rate it
2. **Take notes of specific phrases** you want to improve
3. **Save this rating**тАФyou'll compare it with Mock тСб in Task 65
4. **Be kind but honest**тАФthis is data, not a judgment
