## Task 1: Bar Chart

**Topic:** Internet Users by Age Group in Four Countries (2024)

**Chart Type:** Bar Chart

### Sample Essay (Band 7.5+)

The bar chart illustrates the percentage of internet users across four age groups (18-29, 30-49, 50-64, and 65+) in four countries (UK, Germany, Japan, and South Korea) in the year 2024.

**[Annotation]** 
- *Vocabulary:* "illustrates" (synonym for "shows"), "across" (preposition for distribution), "age groups" (clear categorization)
- *Structure:* Overview sentence using present simple for chart description
- *Band 7.5+:* Accurate introduction with all key parameters (time, countries, categories)

Overall, South Korea exhibited the highest internet penetration across all age demographics, whereas Japan recorded the lowest figures, particularly among older generations. Notably, the 18-29 age group demonstrated the highest usage rates in every country.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "exhibited" (formal), "penetration" (specialized term for adoption rate), "demographics" (population segments), "whereas" (contrast connector), "particularly" (emphasizer)
- *Structure:* Complex sentence with contrast and specification
- *Band 7.5+:* Strong overview identifying main patterns without specific data (reserves for body)

Looking at the youngest age bracket (18-29), South Korea led with 98% internet usage, followed closely by Germany (96%) and the UK (95%). Japan lagged slightly behind at 88%. Among middle-aged users (30-49), South Korea maintained its dominant position with 95%, while the UK and Germany showed comparable figures of 90% and 89% respectively. Japan's rate dropped significantly to 72%.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "bracket" (category), "led" (was ahead), "lagged" (fell behind), "dominant" (predominant), "comparable" (similar)
- *Structure:* Compound-complex sentences with multiple clauses
- *Band 7.5+:* Precise data reporting with comparative language

In the 50-64 age group, a dramatic decline in internet usage became evident. South Korea (78%) and the UK (75%) maintained relatively high rates, while Germany (62%) and Japan (48%) showed substantial drops. The most striking disparity emerged among elderly users (65+), where South Korea (62%) and the UK (55%) achieved moderate penetration, whereas Germany (35%) and especially Japan (18%) struggled to engage older populations effectively.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "dramatic decline" (strong descriptor), "evident" (clear), "striking disparity" (notable difference), "emerged" (appeared), "struggled to engage" (formal phrasing)
- *Structure:* Complex sentences with cause-effect relationships
- *Band 7.5+:* Sophisticated comparative analysis identifying generational gaps

In conclusion, the chart reveals a clear inverse correlation between age and internet adoption across all four nations, with South Korea demonstrating superior digital inclusion strategies that successfully engaged older demographics compared to its counterparts.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "inverse correlation" (statistical relationship), "adoption" (acceptance), "superior" (better), "inclusion" (making accessible), "counterparts" (equivalents in other countries)
- *Structure:* Formal conclusion summarizing key trends
- *Band 7.5+:* Academic tone with analytical insight beyond mere description

## Task 2: Agree/Disagree Essay

**Topic:** Some people believe that universities should focus on providing academic knowledge, while others argue that they should also prepare students for employment. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

**Essay Type:** Discussion + Opinion

### Sample Essay (Band 7.5+)

The role of higher education has become a subject of intense debate in recent years. While some argue that universities should prioritize academic knowledge transmission, others contend that vocational preparation is equally important. This essay will examine both perspectives before presenting my own view.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "subject of intense debate" (formal opening), "prioritize" (emphasize), "transmission" (passing on), "contend" (argue strongly), "vocational" (career-related)
- *Structure:* Standard introduction with thesis statement outlining essay structure
- *Band 7.5+:* Clear thesis sentence previewing discussion approach

Proponents of academic-focused education emphasize that universities are fundamentally institutions of intellectual development. They argue that exposure to theoretical frameworks, critical thinking methodologies, and abstract concepts cultivates the cognitive flexibility necessary for navigating an increasingly complex world. For instance, studying philosophy or pure mathematics may not have direct occupational applications, yet it nurtures analytical skills that transcend specific professions. Furthermore, by maintaining a distance from immediate market demands, universities preserve their role as bastions of independent thought and knowledge creation.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "fundamentally" (at core), "theoretical frameworks" (conceptual structures), "methodologies" (systematic methods), "abstract concepts" (theoretical ideas), "cognitive flexibility" (mental adaptability), "cultivates" (develops), "navigating" (managing), "transcend" (go beyond), "bastions" (strongholds), "independent thought" (autonomous thinking)
- *Structure:* Complex paragraph with multiple subordinate clauses, examples, and counter-arguments
- *Band 7.5+:* Sophisticated argument development with nuanced vocabulary

Conversely, advocates for employment-oriented curricula assert that higher education must address contemporary economic realities. In an era of skyrocketing tuition fees and mounting student debt, students reasonably expect tangible returns on their educational investment. Practical skills acquisition, industry-specific training, and career development programs enhance graduates' employability and facilitate smoother transitions into the workforce. Moreover, businesses increasingly demand graduates who possess both theoretical understanding and practical competence, suggesting that a purely academic approach may leave students ill-equipped for professional challenges.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "advocates" (supporters), "employment-oriented" (career-focused), "contemporary" (current), "skyrocketing" (rapidly increasing), "tangible returns" (measurable benefits), "competence" (capability), "ill-equipped" (unprepared)
- *Structure:* Balanced argumentation with logical progression and concession
- *Band 7.5+:* Acknowledges validity of opposing view while maintaining position

In my opinion, these perspectives should not be viewed as mutually exclusive but rather as complementary elements of a holistic educational approach. Universities that successfully integrate academic rigor with practical relevance produce graduates who are both intellectually sophisticated and professionally adaptable. The optimal model might involve a strong core of theoretical study supplemented by optional internships, industry partnerships, and career counseling services. This balanced approach preserves universities' traditional role as centers of scholarship while acknowledging students' legitimate concerns about employability and economic security.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "mutually exclusive" (cannot coexist), "complementary" (enhancing each other), "holistic" (comprehensive), "rigor" (thoroughness), "relevance" (applicability), "sophisticated" (advanced), "adaptable" (flexible), "legitimate" (valid), "optimal" (best)
- *Structure:* Synthesis paragraph combining both views, proposing solution
- *Band 7.5+:* Nuanced position avoiding simplistic either/or thinking

In conclusion, while academic knowledge remains the cornerstone of university education, practical preparation for employment cannot be disregarded. A balanced curriculum that nurtures intellectual growth alongside career readiness ensures that higher education continues to fulfill its vital role in society, serving both individual students and the broader economy effectively.

**[Annotation]**
- *Vocabulary:* "cornerstone" (foundation), "disregarded" (ignored), "nurtures" (fosters), "fulfill" (satisfy), "vital" (essential)
- *Structure:* Restatement of opinion, balanced conclusion
- *Band 7.5+:* Formal conclusion reinforcing main argument without introducing new ideas

## Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 Success Strategies
1. **Start with a clear overview** - Identify main trends without specific numbers
2. **Group data logically** - Compare similarities before highlighting differences
3. **Use precise language** - Avoid vague terms like "big" or "small"
4. **Vary sentence structure** - Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences
5. **Report exact figures** - Include numbers and percentages in body paragraphs

### Task 2 Success Strategies
1. **Address all parts of the prompt** - Discuss both views AND give your opinion
2. **Develop balanced arguments** - Acknowledge opposing views respectfully
3. **Use topic sentences** - Start each paragraph with a clear main idea
4. **Support with examples** - Use hypothetical or general examples to illustrate
5. **Maintain formal tone** - Avoid contractions, colloquialisms, and overly casual language

### Advanced Techniques
- **Nominalization:** Convert verbs to nouns for more academic style (e.g., "demonstrate" → "demonstration")
- **Passive voice:** Use sparingly for emphasis on results rather than agents
- **Cohesive devices:** Employ a variety of linking words (however, therefore, consequently)
- **Lexical resource:** Build topic-specific vocabulary through reading academic texts
- **Grammatical range:** Practice conditionals, relative clauses, and inversion structures
