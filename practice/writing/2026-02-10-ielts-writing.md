**Task Type:** Bar Chart

### The Chart

The bar chart illustrates the percentage of people using smartphones across three age groups in Country A, Country B, and Country C in 2024. Overall, younger age groups show significantly higher smartphone adoption rates, while the oldest age group has the lowest usage in all three countries.

In Country A, smartphone usage reaches 95% among the 16-24 age group, making it the highest usage rate across all age groups and countries. The 25-34 age group follows with 88%, while the 35-44 age group records 70%. The oldest age group (45-64) shows the lowest adoption at only 25%.

Country B displays a similar pattern, though with slightly lower overall rates. The 16-24 age group achieves 90% smartphone ownership, while the 25-34 and 35-44 age groups show 82% and 65% respectively. Notably, the oldest age group in Country B demonstrates the lowest smartphone usage among all three countries, with only 15%.

Country C exhibits a different trend with higher adoption rates across all age groups. The youngest age group leads with 98% usage, followed by 92% for the 25-34 group and 85% for the 35-44 group. However, even the oldest age group maintains a relatively high adoption rate of 55%, which is more than double that of Country B's oldest demographic.

A clear North-South divide is evident from the data. Country C consistently outperforms the other two nations across all age categories, suggesting greater overall digital infrastructure or smartphone affordability. The gap between age groups remains consistent across all countries, with younger generations being significantly more connected than older populations.

## 💡 Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 (Bar Chart)

1. **Overview Positioning:** Place overview in the introduction or immediately after. State main trends clearly.

2. **Data Accuracy:** Report numbers precisely. Use "approximately," "nearly," "around" when estimates are needed.

3. **Comparative Language:** Use varied comparative structures ("higher than," "the highest," "outperforms," "surpasses").

4. **Sentence Variety:** Mix simple and complex sentences. Use different sentence beginnings.

5. **Beyond Description:** Add analysis and explanation of patterns, not just data reporting.

6. **Vocabulary Sophistication:** Use academic and precise vocabulary ("adoption rates," "digital infrastructure," "North-South divide").

7. **Avoid:** Describing every single data point. Select key features and trends.
