**Topic:** Renewable Energy Consumption in Four Countries (2010-2024)

**Type:** Line Graph

**Instructions:** The line graph below shows the percentage of energy consumption from renewable sources in four countries (Germany, Japan, Brazil, and Sweden) from 2010 to 2024.

### Full Essay (187 words)

The line graph illustrates the proportion of energy derived from renewable sources in four distinct nations—Germany, Japan, Brazil, and Sweden—over a 14-year period between 2010 and 2024.

Overall, Sweden consistently maintained the highest renewable energy consumption throughout the period, while Japan showed the lowest figures. Both Germany and Brazil demonstrated upward trends, albeit at different rates.

Sweden began with a substantial 52% renewable energy share in 2010, which gradually climbed to reach an impressive 68% by 2024. This steady increase suggests successful long-term environmental policies and investments in sustainable infrastructure. Germany's trajectory was similarly positive, starting at 18% and nearly tripling to 49% by the end of the period, reflecting the country's ambitious Energiewende initiative.

Brazil displayed a more modest but consistent growth, rising from 35% to 42% over the 14 years. The country's substantial hydroelectric capacity likely contributed to this relatively stable position. Conversely, Japan's renewable energy consumption fluctuated between 12% and 15% throughout most of the period, showing only marginal improvement to 17% in 2024, possibly due to limited land area and continued reliance on nuclear and fossil fuels.

## Task 2: Two-Part Question

**Topic:** Work-Life Balance

**Instructions:** In many countries, people are working longer hours than ever before, leading to increased stress and reduced time for family and personal activities.

Why do you think this trend is occurring?
What measures could governments and employers take to address this problem?

### Full Essay (298 words)

The modern workplace has witnessed a troubling escalation in working hours, with employees across numerous nations dedicating more time to their professions at the expense of personal well-being and family relationships. This essay will examine the underlying causes of this phenomenon and propose practical solutions that governments and employers could implement.

Two primary factors contribute to the intensification of work hours in contemporary society. Firstly, the advent of globalization and digital communication technologies has created an expectation of constant availability, blurring the traditional boundaries between professional and personal life. Employees frequently respond to emails and messages outside conventional working hours, effectively extending their workday without additional compensation. Secondly, economic uncertainty and competitive job markets have fostered a culture of overwork, where individuals feel compelled to demonstrate their commitment through long hours to secure employment or advancement. This psychological pressure, often exacerbated by social comparisons on professional platforms like LinkedIn, perpetuates a cycle of unsustainable work habits.

To mitigate these issues, governments should introduce legislation that protects workers' rights to disconnect. Several European nations have successfully implemented "right to disconnect" laws, prohibiting employers from contacting employees outside specified working hours. Such policies could significantly reduce work-related stress and restore work-life balance. Furthermore, governments could incentivize companies that prioritize employee well-being through tax benefits or certification programs, encouraging a broader cultural shift away from glorifying overwork.

Employers, for their part, must recognize that productivity and long hours are not synonymous. Companies should establish clear policies that respect boundaries, such as automatic email filters that prevent work communications during evenings and weekends. Additionally, offering flexible working arrangements, including compressed workweeks or remote work options, can empower employees to manage their time more effectively. Regular training for managers to recognize signs of burnout and promote healthy work habits would also prove invaluable.

In conclusion, the trend toward excessive working hours stems from technological and economic pressures that have eroded the separation between work and personal life. However, through collaborative efforts between governments implementing protective legislation and employers fostering a culture that values employee well-being alongside productivity, societies can restore a healthier equilibrium and reduce the detrimental effects of chronic overwork.

## Vocabulary Summary

### Task 1 - Academic Terms
- **illustrate** - to show or demonstrate
- **derive** - to obtain from a specified source
- **substantial** - of considerable importance, size, or worth
- **trajectory** - the path followed by a projectile or object moving under the action of given forces
- **modest** - relatively moderate, limited, or small
- **fluctuate** - rise and fall irregularly in number or amount

### Task 2 - Advanced Vocabulary
- **intensification** - the action of making something become more intense
- **advent** - the arrival of a notable person, thing, or event
- **exacerbate** - make a problem, bad situation, or negative feeling worse
- **perpetuate** - make something continue indefinitely
- **mitigate** - make less severe, serious, or painful
- **incentivize** - provide with an incentive
- **synonymous** - closely associated with or suggestive of something
- **equilibrium** - a state in which opposing forces or influences are balanced

### Useful Phrases
- **at the expense of** - with loss or damage to
- **conversely** - introducing a statement or idea which reverses one that has just been made
- **albeit** - although; even if
- **for their part** - regarding someone's contribution or role in a situation

## Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 Tips:
1. **Always write an overview paragraph** — This is crucial for higher bands
2. **Group data logically** — Don't describe everything chronologically
3. **Use precise numbers** — "approximately 17%" not "about 17%"
4. **Interpret trends, don't just describe** — Explain WHY something happened
5. **Avoid personal opinion** — Task 1 is about reporting facts

### Task 2 Tips:
1. **Answer both parts of the question** — For two-part questions, address each equally
2. **Use clear topic sentences** — Each paragraph should have a clear main idea
3. **Provide specific examples** — Don't just say "governments should help" — say HOW
4. **Balance your argument** — Show you understand different perspectives
5. **Link paragraphs clearly** — Use transition words between ideas

### General Tips:
- **Plan before writing** — Spend 3-5 minutes outlining
- **Monitor word count** — Task 1: 150-180 words, Task 2: 250-320 words
- **Use varied sentence structures** — Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences
- **Proofread** — Leave 2 minutes to check for errors
- **Practice regularly** — Write at least 3-4 essays per week
