### Model Answer: Task 1 (~180 words)

The line graph illustrates the growth of global renewable energy production across four major types (hydroelectric, wind, solar, and biomass) over a fifteen-year period from 2010 to 2025.

Overall, all four renewable energy sources experienced significant growth during this period, with solar energy demonstrating the most dramatic expansion. While hydroelectric power remained the dominant source throughout the period, wind and solar energy showed the steepest upward trajectories, narrowing the gap with traditional hydroelectric generation.

In 2010, hydroelectric power was by far the largest renewable energy source, accounting for approximately 1200 terawatt-hours (TWh) of global production. Wind energy was the second-largest at around 400 TWh, followed by biomass at 250 TWh, and solar energy at a modest 100 TWh. The production levels for hydroelectric, wind, and biomass remained relatively stable in the early years, with hydroelectric showing slight growth.

Between 2015 and 2020, a clear divergence in growth patterns emerged. Solar energy production accelerated dramatically, rising from 150 TWh in 2015 to 400 TWh in 2020, a 167% increase. Wind energy also showed substantial growth, climbing from 500 TWh to 800 TWh over the same period. Hydroelectric power continued steady growth, reaching 1400 TWh by 2020, while biomass demonstrated modest increases, rising from 300 TWh to 350 TWh.

The most remarkable transformation occurred in the final five years (2020-2025). Solar energy production skyrocketed, surging from 400 TWh in 2020 to 1100 TWh in 2025, representing a 175% increase and positioning it as the second-largest renewable energy source, surpassing wind and biomass. Wind energy also maintained strong growth, reaching 1000 TWh by 2025. Hydroelectric power continued its steady rise, achieving 1600 TWh, while biomass showed slower but consistent growth, reaching 450 TWh.

This data indicates a fundamental shift in the global renewable energy landscape, with solar energy emerging as a transformative technology capable of challenging traditional hydroelectric dominance. The rapid growth of both solar and wind energy reflects the increasing affordability and efficiency of these technologies, as well as growing global commitment to decarbonization efforts.

### Model Answer: Task 2 (~300 words)

The question of whether university education should be universally free or funded by students themselves remains a contentious issue in many societies. While proponents of free education argue that it promotes equality of opportunity and social mobility, opponents maintain that paid education ensures quality and personal responsibility. This essay will examine both perspectives before presenting my own view on this important matter.

Advocates of free university education emphasize its potential to create a more equitable society. They argue that when education is accessible regardless of financial background, talented individuals from disadvantaged backgrounds can realize their potential and contribute to society. This perspective holds that education is a public good that benefits society as a whole, not just individual students. Furthermore, free education can reduce the burden of student debt, allowing graduates to pursue careers in public service, education, or other lower-paying fields without the pressure of loan repayment. Countries like Germany and the Nordic nations demonstrate that free higher education systems can produce highly educated populations and strong economies while maintaining social cohesion.

On the other hand, those who support paid university education contend that funding mechanisms influence educational quality and student commitment. They argue that when students pay for their education, universities have resources to invest in state-of-the-art facilities, research opportunities, and qualified faculty. Additionally, paying for education may foster a greater sense of responsibility and motivation among students, as they have a personal stake in their academic success. Critics also point to the economic reality that free education requires substantial public funding, which may necessitate higher taxes or reduced spending in other critical areas such as healthcare, infrastructure, or social welfare programs.

In my opinion, the most effective approach is a hybrid system that combines free access with targeted support. Free education should be available to students from low-income backgrounds through scholarships, grants, or income-contingent loans, ensuring that financial constraints do not prevent talented individuals from pursuing higher education. Meanwhile, students from more affluent families can contribute to their education costs, helping to fund the system while still maintaining access. This balanced approach maximizes both equity and sustainability, allowing universities to maintain quality while ensuring that the most deserving students can attend regardless of their financial circumstances.

Furthermore, universities should be encouraged to diversify their funding sources through industry partnerships, research grants, and alumni donations, reducing reliance on either government funding or student fees alone. This model, exemplified by countries like Australia and the United Kingdom, demonstrates that mixed funding systems can provide both accessibility and quality in higher education.

In conclusion, while both free and paid education systems have merit, a hybrid approach that balances accessibility with sustainability offers the best solution. By ensuring free access for those who need it most while maintaining appropriate contributions from those who can afford it, societies can create higher education systems that are both equitable and financially viable.

## 💡 Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 Tips
1. **Structure is key:** Introduction → Overview → Details → Conclusion
2. **Overview comes second:** Don't bury the most important paragraph at the end
3. **No opinions:** Task 1 is purely descriptive; never say "I think"
4. **Compare, don't just list:** Show relationships between data points
5. **Varied sentence starters:** Use "Overall," "Specifically," "Notably," "In particular"

### Task 2 Tips
1. **Clear thesis:** State your position in the introduction
2. **Topic sentences:** Every paragraph should start with its main idea
3. **Examples matter:** Specific examples strengthen your arguments
4. **Logical flow:** Use transition words to connect ideas smoothly
5. **Conclusion insight:** End with something broader than just a summary
