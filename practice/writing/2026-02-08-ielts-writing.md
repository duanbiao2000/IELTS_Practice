## Task 1: Bar Chart (Academic)

### Topic
The bar chart below shows the percentage of electricity generated from renewable energy sources in five countries (Germany, Spain, Denmark, Portugal, and Italy) in 2020.

### The Chart
```
Germany:   ████████████ 46%
Spain:     ████████████ 42%
Denmark:   ████████████████ 60%
Portugal:  ██████████ 38%
Italy:     ███████ 26%
```

---

### Model Essay (~180 words)

The bar chart illustrates the proportion of electricity produced from renewable sources across five European nations in 2020. Overall, Denmark generated the highest percentage of renewable electricity, while Italy recorded the lowest figure among the countries surveyed.

Denmark led the way with 60% of its electricity coming from renewable sources, substantially exceeding the other nations. Germany followed with 46%, and Spain ranked third at 42%. Portugal produced 38% of its electricity from renewables, whereas Italy trailed behind with only 26%.

Notably, there is a clear north-south divide evident in the data. The two Scandinavian countries (Denmark and the northern European nations including Germany) outperformed their southern counterparts (Spain, Portugal, and Italy) in renewable energy adoption. The disparity between the highest and lowest figures is substantial, with Denmark's renewable electricity share being more than double that of Italy.

**Annotations:**

**Paragraph 1 (Introduction + Overview)**
- **Vocabulary:** "illustrates" (synonym for "shows"), "proportion" (more precise than "percentage"), "nations surveyed" (formal)
- **Sentence structure:** Complex sentence with multiple clauses
- **Writing technique:** Includes overview immediately (highest/lowest), which is essential for Band 7+
- **Band 7.5+ feature:** "Overall" signals the overview clearly

**Paragraph 2 (Body - Detailed data)**
- **Vocabulary:** "led the way" (idiomatic, natural), "substantially exceeding" (precise comparison), "ranked third" (clear ordering)
- **Sentence structures:** Mixed simple and complex sentences for variety
- **Data accuracy:** All percentages mentioned precisely
- **Writing technique:** Logical ordering from highest to lowest for clarity
- **Band 7.5+ feature:** "whereas" for contrast, "only" for emphasis on the lowest figure

**Paragraph 3 (Body - Analysis/Trends)**
- **Vocabulary:** "notably" (attention-grabbing transition), "evident" (academic), "outperformed" (formal comparison), "substantial" (emphasizes difference), "disparity" (sophisticated for "difference")
- **Sentence structure:** Complex sentences with multiple clauses
- **Writing technique:** Adds insight beyond simple data reporting (north-south divide)
- **Band 7.5+ feature:** "more than double" for precise quantitative comparison, analytical thinking shows higher-level skills

## Task 2: Problem-Solution (Academic)

### Topic
In many large cities around the world, traffic congestion has become a major problem.

**Discuss the causes of this problem and propose some solutions.**

---

### Model Essay (~300 words)

Traffic congestion in metropolitan areas has emerged as a critical issue affecting millions of urban residents worldwide. This essay will examine the primary causes of this phenomenon and propose viable solutions to alleviate the problem.

The principal cause of urban traffic congestion is the over-reliance on private vehicles. As economic prosperity has grown, car ownership has surged dramatically, particularly in developing nations. Consequently, the road infrastructure in many cities has failed to keep pace with this exponential increase in vehicle numbers, creating bottlenecks during peak commuting hours. Moreover, inadequate public transportation systems exacerbate the issue, as commuters lack viable alternatives to driving. When buses and trains are infrequent, overcrowded, or unreliable, residents are forced to use their personal vehicles, thereby perpetuating the cycle of congestion.

To address these challenges, a multi-faceted approach is required. Firstly, governments must invest significantly in upgrading and expanding public transportation networks. This would involve increasing the frequency of services, extending routes to underserved areas, and ensuring reliability through modern infrastructure. For instance, cities like Copenhagen and Amsterdam have successfully reduced congestion by investing in comprehensive metro and light rail systems. Secondly, implementing congestion pricing schemes has proven effective in several cities. London's congestion charge, for example, has reduced traffic volumes in the city centre by approximately 30% since its introduction. Furthermore, cities could promote cycling and walking through dedicated infrastructure and incentives, encouraging residents to adopt more sustainable commuting habits.

In conclusion, traffic congestion stems from excessive private vehicle use and inadequate public transport alternatives. However, through strategic investment in public infrastructure and innovative policies such as congestion pricing, cities can mitigate this problem and create more livable urban environments for future generations.

**Annotations:**

**Paragraph 1 (Introduction)**
- **Vocabulary:** "metropolitan areas" (formal), "emerged as" (academic phrasing), "critical issue" (strong emphasis), "viable solutions" (sophisticated alternative to "good solutions")
- **Sentence structure:** Complex opening with multiple clauses
- **Writing technique:** Clear thesis statement outlining the essay structure
- **Band 7.5+ feature:** "This essay will examine..." - clear signposting of essay direction

**Paragraph 2 (Body - Causes)**
- **Vocabulary:** "over-reliance" (academic), "surged dramatically" (dynamic verb phrase), "exponential" (precise technical term), "inadequate" (formal negative), "exacerbate" (sophisticated for "make worse"), "perpetuating" (advanced verb), "viable alternatives" (academic)
- **Sentence structures:** Three main sentences, each building on the previous
- **Writing technique:** Logical flow from economic causes to infrastructure causes to public transport causes
- **Band 7.5+ features:**
  - "As... has grown, ... has surged" (correlative structure)
  - "Consequently" (causal link)
  - "Moreover" (adding information)
  - "thereby" (result clause, very Band 7+)
  - Analytical depth - connects causes to effects

**Paragraph 3 (Body - Solutions)**
- **Vocabulary:** "multi-faceted approach" (sophisticated), "significantly" (adverb for emphasis), "underserved areas" (academic), "comprehensive" (descriptive), "proven effective" (evidence-based), "mitigate" (formal for "reduce")
- **Sentence structures:** Varied sentence lengths, complex constructions
- **Writing technique:** Multiple solutions presented with specific examples
- **Band 7.5+ features:**
  - "Firstly... Secondly... Furthermore..." (clear organization)
  - Specific real-world examples (Copenhagen, Amsterdam, London)
  - Precise data reference ("approximately 30%")
  - "For instance" (introducing evidence)
  - "encouraging residents to adopt" (causative structure)

**Paragraph 4 (Conclusion)**
- **Vocabulary:** "stems from" (formal for "comes from"), "mitigate" (academic), "livable" (urban planning term)
- **Sentence structure:** Two well-developed sentences
- **Writing technique:** Summarizes main points and provides forward-looking statement
- **Band 7.5+ features:**
  - "In conclusion" (clear transition)
  - "However" (contrast marker)
  - "and create more livable urban environments for future generations" (expansive, visionary conclusion)
  - No new information introduced (conclusion best practice)

## 💡 Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1
1. **Always include an overview** - Paraphrase the question + mention key trends (highest/lowest, increases/decreases)
2. **Group data logically** - Don't just list numbers; find patterns and comparisons
3. **Use a variety of vocabulary** - Avoid repeating the same words (e.g., use "illustrate," "depict," "show")
4. **Accurate data reporting** - Include all key numbers and percentages
5. **Add analysis** - Don't just describe; explain what the data means (e.g., "north-south divide")

### Task 2
1. **Clear paragraph structure** - Introduction → Body Paragraphs (topic sentences) → Conclusion
2. **Varied sentence structures** - Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences
3. **Cohesive devices** - Use linking words (However, Moreover, Consequently, Furthermore)
4. **Specific examples** - Real-world examples strengthen arguments
5. **Precision** - Use exact numbers and specific vocabulary instead of vague terms
6. **No new info in conclusion** - Summarize only what you've already discussed

### General
1. **Academic tone** - Use formal vocabulary (e.g., "mitigate" instead of "fix")
2. **Avoid personal opinion** - Unless the question asks for it ("To what extent do you agree?")
3. **Time management** - Task 1: 20 minutes, Task 2: 40 minutes
4. **Word count** - Task 1: 150+ words, Task 2: 250+ words
