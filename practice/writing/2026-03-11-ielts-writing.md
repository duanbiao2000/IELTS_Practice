## Task 1: Line Graph (Band 7.5+)

**Topic:** Renewable Energy Consumption in Five Countries (2010-2025)

**Type:** Line Graph

### The Question

The line graph shows the consumption of renewable energy as a percentage of total energy use in five countries (Germany, Denmark, Sweden, Norway, and Finland) from 2010 to 2025.

Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

---

### Essay (Band 7.5+ Model)

The line graph illustrates the percentage of renewable energy consumption out of total energy use in five European nations over a 15-year period from 2010 to 2025. Overall, all countries experienced upward trends, with Denmark and Sweden showing the most dramatic increases, while Germany and Norway maintained more moderate growth. Finland exhibited the lowest but steady progression.

Denmark's renewable energy usage rose sharply from approximately 25% in 2010 to nearly 55% by 2025, representing more than a twofold increase. Sweden followed a similar trajectory, climbing from roughly 20% to about 50% during the same timeframe. Notably, these two nations consistently outperformed the other three countries throughout the period.

Germany demonstrated a more gradual but consistent upward movement, starting at 30% and reaching 45% by the end of the period. Norway, which began at the highest point among the five countries at 35%, experienced relatively modest growth to 40% in 2025. Finland, despite starting from the lowest position of 15% in 2010, maintained steady annual increments, ultimately achieving 35% renewable energy consumption by 2025.

## Task 2: Agree/Disagree (Band 7.5+)

**Topic:** Remote Work

**Question:**

Some people believe that remote work has negatively impacted teamwork and collaboration in organizations. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement?

---

### Essay (Band 7.5+ Model)

The proliferation of remote work arrangements has sparked intense debate regarding their impact on organizational dynamics. While some critics argue that telecommuting undermines teamwork and collaboration, I contend that this perspective is overly simplistic. In my view, remote work has actually transformed, rather than diminished, collaborative practices, though it does present certain challenges that require intentional management.

Admittedly, the absence of face-to-face interaction can create obstacles to spontaneous collaboration and informal knowledge exchange. Serendipitous encounters in office spaces often generate innovative ideas and strengthen interpersonal relationships that are difficult to replicate virtually. Furthermore, remote work may exacerbate feelings of isolation among team members, particularly for extroverted individuals who thrive on social interaction. The lack of non-verbal cues in digital communication can also lead to misunderstandings and reduced rapport.

However, remote work has catalyzed the development of more structured and inclusive collaborative methodologies. Organizations have been compelled to implement robust digital tools and frameworks that facilitate cross-functional communication across geographical boundaries. Video conferencing platforms, project management software, and asynchronous communication channels have democratized participation, allowing quieter team members to contribute more effectively. Moreover, the deliberate scheduling of virtual meetings has increased meeting efficiency and reduced unnecessary interruptions that plague traditional office environments.

Furthermore, remote work has fostered a more inclusive organizational culture by eliminating location-based biases. Talent acquisition is no longer constrained by geographical proximity, enabling companies to assemble diverse teams with varied perspectives and expertise. This diversity, when properly leveraged, enhances creativity and problem-solving capabilities. Additionally, the flexibility inherent in remote arrangements has been shown to improve employee well-being and job satisfaction, which are critical factors in maintaining high-performance collaborative cultures.

In conclusion, while remote work undeniably alters the landscape of teamwork, it does not inherently diminish collaboration. The key lies in organizations' willingness to adapt their management practices and invest in appropriate digital infrastructure. When implemented thoughtfully, remote work can enhance rather than hinder organizational collaboration by promoting more intentional, inclusive, and efficient ways of working together.

## ✍️ Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 (Academic Writing Task 1)
1. **Overview is crucial** - Always summarize the main trends in your introduction/overview paragraph
2. **Group information logically** - Don't describe every year or every country separately
3. **Use accurate data reporting** - "approximately," "roughly," "nearly" show precision
4. **Vary sentence structures** - Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences
5. **Avoid personal opinion** - This is descriptive writing, not argumentative

### Task 2 (Essay Writing)
1. **Clear position statement** - Your thesis should be unambiguous in the introduction
2. **Balanced argumentation** - Acknowledge counterarguments before refuting them
3. **Topic sentences** - Start each body paragraph with a clear main idea
4. **Cohesive devices** - Use linking words appropriately (however, moreover, furthermore)
5. **Academic register** - Formal vocabulary, objective tone, sophisticated grammar
6. **Concrete examples** - Support generalizations with specific instances
7. **Paragraph balance** - Similar length and development across body paragraphs

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
❌ Overusing personal pronouns (I, me, my) in Task 1
❌ Including opinions in Task 1 overview
❌ Using informal vocabulary (got, nice, really)
❌ Writing too many simple sentences
❌ Failing to address all parts of the Task 2 question
❌ Introducing new ideas in the conclusion

### Band 7.5+ Checklist
- [ ] Clear position with balanced argument
- [ ] Academic vocabulary throughout
- [ ] Varied sentence structures
- [ ] Accurate data reporting (Task 1)
- [ ] Logical paragraph organization
- [ ] Appropriate cohesive devices
- [ ] Consistent academic tone
- [ ] Within word limits (150-200 for Task 1, 250-320 for Task 2)
