## Task 1: Line Graph

**Topic:** Renewable Energy Consumption in Four Countries (2010-2020)
**Type:** Line Graph
**Word Count:** ~180 words
**Time:** 20 minutes

### The Graph Data

```
Renewable Energy Consumption (% of Total Energy)

Countries:
- Country A (Germany)
- Country B (Denmark)
- Country C (Spain)
- Country D (Poland)

Years: 2010, 2012, 2014, 2016, 2018, 2020

Data:
Country A: 15% → 22% → 28% → 35% → 42% → 48%
Country B: 18% → 25% → 32% → 38% → 45% → 52%
Country C: 12% → 16% → 20% → 25% → 28% → 32%
Country D: 5% → 7% → 9% → 11% → 13% → 15%
```

### Model Essay

The line graph illustrates the percentage of renewable energy consumed relative to total energy production in four European countries from 2010 to 2020. Overall, all four nations experienced upward trends in renewable energy adoption, with Denmark and Germany leading the transition, while Poland lagged significantly behind.

**[ANNOTATION]**  
**Overview**: "illustrates" (synonym for "shows"), "relative to" (prepositional phrase), "transition" (noun - Band 7.5+ vocabulary). The overview captures the main trends without specific data.

Denmark demonstrated the most remarkable progress, surging from 18% to 52% — a nearly threefold increase. Germany followed a similar trajectory, rising steadily from 15% to 48%. Both countries maintained consistent upward momentum, particularly after 2014 when growth accelerated.

**[ANNOTATION]**  
**Vocabulary**: "demonstrated" (synonym for "showed"), "remarkable" (strong adjective), "surging" (dynamic verb), "threefold" (precise quantifier), "trajectory" (advanced noun), "momentum" (academic term).  
**Sentence Structure**: "surging from 18% to 52% — a nearly threefold increase" (dash for emphasis), "particularly after 2014 when growth accelerated" (dependent clause).  
**Writing Technique**: Precise data reporting with comparison ("similar trajectory").

Spain exhibited moderate growth, climbing from 12% to 32% throughout the decade. Although its increase was less dramatic than the Scandinavian and German markets, Spain maintained a consistent upward trajectory. Conversely, Poland showed the slowest development, with renewable energy consumption merely tripling from 5% to 15%, remaining the lowest among the four nations.

**[ANNOTATION]**  
**Vocabulary**: "exhibited" (formal verb), "climbing" (active verb), "moderate" (descriptive adjective), "dramatic" (contrastive term), "merely" (adverb for emphasis), "conversely" (contrast marker).  
**Sentence Structure**: "Although its increase was less dramatic..." (concessive clause), "with renewable energy consumption merely tripling from 5% to 15%" (prepositional phrase with gerund).  
**Writing Technique**: Clear comparison structure ("Although... maintained").

In conclusion, the decade witnessed substantial progress in renewable energy adoption across all four countries, with Northern European nations outperforming their Southern and Eastern counterparts, reflecting regional disparities in renewable energy policies and infrastructure investment.

**[ANNOTATION]**  
**Vocabulary**: "witnessed" (formal verb), "substantial" (strong adjective), "outperforming" (gerund), "counterparts" (Band 7.5+ noun), "reflecting" (participle), "disparities" (advanced noun).  
**Sentence Structure**: "...with Northern European nations outperforming their Southern and Eastern counterparts, reflecting regional disparities..." (participial phrase structure).  
**Writing Technique**: Strong conclusion that synthesizes data with broader implications.

## Task 2: Agree/Disagree Essay

**Topic:** Some people believe that artificial intelligence will replace human teachers in schools. Do you agree or disagree with this view?
**Type:** Agree/Disagree
**Word Count:** ~300 words
**Time:** 40 minutes

### Model Essay

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence has sparked intense debate regarding its potential to supplant human educators in academic institutions. While AI possesses remarkable capabilities in personalized instruction and administrative efficiency, I firmly contend that it cannot fully replace human teachers due to the irreplaceable nature of emotional intelligence, mentorship, and ethical guidance in education.

**[ANNOTATION]**  
**Vocabulary**: "sparked intense debate" (idiomatic expression), "supplant" (Band 7.5+ synonym for "replace"), "academic institutions" (formal phrase), "possesses" (formal verb), "contend" (academic verb), "irreplaceable" (strong adjective), "mentorship" (advanced noun).  
**Sentence Structure**: "While AI possesses..., I firmly contend that..." (contrast structure), complex sentence with parallel items ("emotional intelligence, mentorship, and ethical guidance").  
**Writing Technique**: Clear thesis statement with three supporting points.

Undoubtedly, AI technology offers transformative potential in educational settings. Adaptive learning algorithms can customize curricula to individual student needs, providing instant feedback and identifying knowledge gaps with unprecedented precision. Furthermore, AI-powered systems excel at administrative tasks such as grading standardized assessments, freeing educators to focus on more impactful pedagogical activities. These technological advantages have revolutionized language learning platforms and virtual tutoring services, demonstrating AI's capacity to complement traditional teaching methods.

**[ANNOTATION]**  
**Vocabulary**: "Undoubtedly" (transition marker), "transformative potential" (collocation), "adaptive learning algorithms" (technical term), "customize" (verb), "unprecedented precision" (collocation), "excel at" (phrase), "pedagogical activities" (Band 7.5+ vocabulary), "complement" (precise verb), "revolutionized" (strong verb).  
**Sentence Structure**: Complex sentences with multiple clauses, use of passive voice ("can be customized"), parallel structure in listing.  
**Writing Technique**: Concession paragraph acknowledging AI strengths before presenting counterarguments.

However, the multifaceted role of human teachers transcends mere knowledge transmission. Education fundamentally involves nurturing emotional intelligence, fostering creativity, and instilling moral values — dimensions where AI unequivocally falls short. Experienced educators provide crucial mentorship, inspiring students through personal experiences and modeling desirable behaviors. They recognize subtle emotional cues, adapt to psychological needs, and create supportive environments that algorithms cannot replicate. Moreover, ethical dilemmas in education require human judgment and contextual understanding, qualities that current AI systems lack.

**[ANNOTATION]**  
**Vocabulary**: "multifaceted" (advanced adjective), "transcends" (Band 8 verb), "fundamentally involves" (emphasis), "nurturing" (verb), "instilling" (verb), "unequivocally" (adverb), "falls short" (idiom), "crucial mentorship" (collocation), "subtle emotional cues" (phrase), "psychological needs" (academic term), "contextual understanding" (advanced phrase).  
**Sentence Structure**: Long, complex sentences with embedded clauses ("where AI unequivocally falls short"), use of dashes for emphasis.  
**Writing Technique**: Strong argumentation with concrete examples of human qualities AI lacks.

In conclusion, while artificial intelligence undoubtedly enhances educational efficiency and personalization, it cannot replace human teachers' indispensable role in holistic development. The optimal educational paradigm lies not in substitution but in integration — leveraging AI's analytical strengths while preserving the human elements that make education truly transformative.

**[ANNOTATION]**  
**Vocabulary**: "undoubtedly enhances" (repetition for emphasis), "indispensable" (Band 8 adjective), "holistic development" (academic phrase), "optimal educational paradigm" (advanced collocation), "substitution" (noun), "integration" (noun), "leveraging" (gerund).  
**Sentence Structure**: "while..., it cannot..." (contrast structure), "not in substitution but in integration" (parallel contrast), dash for explanatory phrase.  
**Writing Technique**: Strong conclusion that acknowledges both sides while reinforcing thesis, forward-looking statement about future education.

## Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 (Academic Writing)
1. **Structure**: Overview first or last (not in the middle), followed by detailed body paragraphs
2. **Data Description**: Use precise language (nearly, approximately, roughly) instead of exact numbers every time
3. **Comparison**: Always compare data points, not just list them
4. **Variety**: Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences
5. **Vocabulary**: Use synonyms for common words (increase → rise, climb, surge, escalate)

### Task 2 (Essay Writing)
1. **Clear Position**: State your opinion clearly in the introduction
2. **Concession Paragraph**: Acknowledge the opposing view before presenting your arguments
3. **Specific Examples**: Provide concrete examples to support each point
4. **Cohesion**: Use linking devices effectively (However, Furthermore, Moreover, Conversely)
5. **Academic Tone**: Avoid contractions, use formal vocabulary, maintain objective voice

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Overusing simple sentences (score reduction in Coherence & Cohesion)
- ❌ Repeating the same vocabulary (score reduction in Lexical Resource)
- ❌ Not providing an overview in Task 1 (automatic Band 6 maximum)
- ❌ Writing less than 250 words for Task 2 (penalty applied)
- ❌ Not taking a clear position in Task 2 (score reduction in Task Response)
