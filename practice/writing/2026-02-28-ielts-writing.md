### Sample Essay (Band 7.5+)

The bar chart compares the energy consumption measured in gigawatt-hours across five nations—Australia, Brazil, China, Japan, and the United States—during three selected years: 1990, 2000, and 2010. Overall, the United States consistently consumed the most energy throughout the period, while Brazil remained the lowest consumer. All countries experienced an upward trend, with China showing the most dramatic increase.

In 1990, the United States consumed approximately 850 gigawatt-hours, nearly double that of Japan (420 GWh) and significantly higher than Australia (300 GWh), China (250 GWh), and Brazil (150 GWh). By 2000, the gap between the US and other nations widened further as its consumption surged to 1,200 GWh. Japan also experienced substantial growth to 600 GWh, whereas Australia and China saw moderate increases to 400 and 350 GWh respectively. Brazil’s consumption grew modestly to 200 GWh, maintaining its position as the smallest consumer.

The most notable change occurred between 2000 and 2010. China’s energy consumption skyrocketed from 350 to 1,100 GWh, surpassing Japan (700 GWh) and nearly matching the United States (1,300 GWh). Australia continued its steady rise to 550 GWh, while Brazil doubled its consumption to 400 GWh. By 2010, the disparity between the highest and lowest consumers had narrowed considerably, with China’s meteoric rise reshaping the global energy landscape.

## 📖 Task 2: Essay Writing

**Type:** Agree/Disagree  
**Topic:** Remote work will permanently replace traditional office-based employment in the next decade.

**Task:** Some experts predict that remote work will completely replace traditional office-based employment within the next decade. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this prediction?

---

### Sample Essay (Band 7.5+)

The advent of digital technology has fundamentally transformed the workplace, leading many analysts to forecast that remote work will entirely supplant traditional office environments within the next ten years. While I acknowledge the undeniable momentum of remote work, I disagree with the notion that it will completely eliminate physical offices. Instead, I believe a hybrid model will emerge as the dominant paradigm.

The rise of remote work is undeniably compelling. Technological advancements, including high-speed internet, cloud computing, and sophisticated collaboration tools, have effectively dismantled geographical barriers. Companies such as Twitter and Shopify have embraced fully remote structures, reporting increased productivity and employee satisfaction. Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic served as a catalyst, forcing organizations worldwide to adapt and proving that remote operations are not only feasible but often preferable. The economic benefits are equally persuasive, as businesses reduce overhead costs associated with office space, utilities, and commuting subsidies.

However, several critical factors argue against the complete abandonment of physical offices. Human interaction is intrinsic to organizational culture and innovation. Face-to-face collaboration fosters spontaneous idea exchange, strengthens professional relationships, and mitigates the isolation and burnout that plague remote workers. Furthermore, certain industries inherently require physical presence—healthcare, manufacturing, and education being prime examples. The mentorship of junior employees also benefits significantly from in-person guidance, as subtle learning opportunities through observation are difficult to replicate virtually.

Additionally, the hybrid model offers a pragmatic compromise that maximizes the advantages of both approaches. Employees can enjoy the flexibility and work-life balance of remote work while maintaining regular in-person connections. Organizations can optimize office space utilization, designing collaborative hubs rather than individual workstations. This balanced approach addresses the diverse needs of employees and accommodates varying job functions, creating a more resilient and adaptable workforce.

In conclusion, while remote work has revolutionized contemporary employment practices, it is unlikely to entirely replace traditional offices. The future workplace will likely be characterized by flexible arrangements that blend virtual and physical environments. This evolution represents not the end of the office, but its transformation into a more dynamic and purposeful space.

## ✍️ Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 (Data Description)
1. **Always include an overview** — This is crucial for Band 7+
2. **Use varied comparison structures** — "X was higher than Y," "X exceeded Y by..."
3. **Accurate reporting** — Never make up data; use "approximately" if needed
4. **Time phrases** — "In 1990," "By 2000," "Between 1990 and 2010"
5. **Synonyms for change** — "increased," "rose," "grew," "surged," "skyrocketed"
6. **Organization by logic** — Either by time (1990 → 2000 → 2010) or by categories

### Task 2 (Essay Writing)
1. **Clear position statement** — State your opinion clearly in the introduction
2. **Balanced structure** — If agree/disagree, address the counter-argument
3. **Topic sentences** — Each paragraph should start with a clear main idea
4. **Linking words** — "However," "Moreover," "Furthermore," "In conclusion"
5. **Specific examples** — Real companies, industries, or scenarios add credibility
6. **Varied sentence structures** — Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences
7. **No new arguments in conclusion** — Summarize only

### General Tips
1. **Avoid repetition** — Use synonyms instead of repeating the same word
2. **Academic tone** — Avoid slang, contractions, and overly casual language
3. **Proofread** — Leave 3-5 minutes at the end to check for errors
4. **Time management** — Task 1: 20 minutes, Task 2: 40 minutes
5. **Word count** — Task 1: 150+ words, Task 2: 250+ words
