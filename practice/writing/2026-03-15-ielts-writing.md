When examining the data collectively, a clear correlation emerges between economic development and internet penetration in workplace settings. ## Task 2: Essay Writing (Agree/Disagree)

**Topic:** Some people believe that universities should focus on providing skills for employment, while others think universities should focus on pure academic knowledge without practical applications. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.

---

### Sample Answer (Band 7.5+)

The debate over whether higher education institutions should prioritize employability skills or pure academic scholarship has intensified in recent decades. While some argue that universities should primarily equip students with marketable vocational competencies, others contend that the pursuit of theoretical knowledge should remain the core mission. In my view, a balanced approach that integrates both perspectives is most beneficial for individual and societal development.

Proponents of employment-focused education argue that universities have a responsibility to prepare graduates for the competitive job market. In an era of rapid technological advancement and economic uncertainty, students acquire specialized technical expertise, practical problem-solving abilities, and industry-relevant soft skills through vocational curricula. For instance, engineering programs that incorporate internships and industry partnerships produce graduates who are immediately workplace-ready, thereby reducing the skills gap that many employers report. This approach not only enhances individual career prospects but also contributes to economic productivity by supplying a workforce equipped with contemporary competencies.

Conversely, advocates for academic purism maintain that universities should serve as bastions of intellectual exploration and critical thinking, unconstrained by immediate market demands. They argue that the rigorous study of theoretical disciplines—philosophy, pure mathematics, theoretical physics—cultivates essential cognitive capacities: analytical reasoning, abstract conceptualization, and methodical skepticism. These intellectual foundations, though not directly applicable to specific occupations, foster innovation and long-term societal progress. History demonstrates that groundbreaking discoveries in quantum mechanics or theoretical economics often originated from academic inquiry unattached to commercial objectives, yet subsequently revolutionized industries and economies.

In my opinion, the dichotomy between practical skills and academic knowledge is ultimately false and counterproductive. The most effective higher education systems recognize that these domains are complementary rather than mutually exclusive. A philosophy student who develops exceptional logical reasoning abilities can apply these analytical skills to data science, business strategy, or software engineering. Similarly, a mathematics major specializing in abstract algebra may find unexpected applications in cryptography, artificial intelligence, or financial modeling. Universities should therefore design curricula that provide both foundational theoretical understanding and practical application opportunities through research projects, internships, and industry collaborations.

Ultimately, the purpose of higher education extends beyond mere job preparation or academic exercise—it encompasses personal intellectual growth, societal advancement, and the cultivation of adaptable lifelong learners. By embracing a synergistic approach that values both theoretical depth and practical relevance, universities can fulfill their multifaceted role in producing well-rounded graduates capable of navigating the complexities of the 21st century workforce while contributing meaningfully to human knowledge.

## Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 Strategies:
1. **Overview is essential** - Always include a summary of main trends/contrasts in your introduction or final paragraph
2. **Group data intelligently** - Don't list every number; group similar data and make comparisons
3. **Precise language** - Use exact verbs (rose/increased/soared, fell/dropped/plummeted) and adverbs (significantly/gradually/dramatically)
4. **Sentence variety** - Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences to demonstrate grammatical range

### Task 2 Strategies:
1. **Clear position** - State your opinion clearly and support it throughout the essay
2. **Balanced argument** - Present both views fairly before giving your own opinion
3. **Concluding thoughtfully** - Don't just repeat your thesis; broaden to implications or recommendations
4. **Concrete examples** - Use specific examples (internships, historical discoveries, real-world applications)

### General Band 7.5+ Techniques:
- **Avoid repetition** - Use synonyms and paraphrasing instead of repeating the same words
- **Academic tone** - Formal language, objective perspective, precise terminology
- **Cohesion devices** - Linking words and phrases to connect ideas smoothly
- **Proofreading** - Save 2-3 minutes to check for grammar, spelling, and punctuation errors
