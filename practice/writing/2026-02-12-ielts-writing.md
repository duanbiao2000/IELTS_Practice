## 📋 Task 1: Line Graph

**Topic:** Renewable Energy Consumption in Four Countries (2010-2020)

**Type:** Line Graph with Multiple Data Series

---

### 📝 Model Essay (Band 7.5+)

The line graph illustrates the trends in renewable energy consumption, measured as a percentage of total energy usage, across four countries (Germany, Japan, Brazil, and Sweden) over a ten-year period from 2010 to 2020.

Overall, there was a general upward trajectory in renewable energy adoption across all four nations, with Sweden consistently maintaining the highest consumption levels. While Germany and Brazil demonstrated similar growth patterns, Japan exhibited the most dramatic increase, albeit starting from the lowest baseline.

In 2010, Sweden led the way with approximately 45% of its energy derived from renewable sources, followed by Germany at 30%, Brazil at 25%, and Japan at a mere 15%. Over the subsequent decade, Sweden witnessed a steady but moderate rise, reaching roughly 55% by 2020. Similarly, Germany showed consistent growth, climbing from 30% to around 48%, indicating its commitment to green energy transition.

Brazil's trajectory paralleled Germany's, beginning at 25% and ascending to approximately 42% by the end of the period. However, the most remarkable transformation occurred in Japan. Despite starting from the lowest point, Japan's renewable energy consumption surged dramatically, nearly tripling from 15% to 43% between 2015 and 2020, likely driven by post-Fukushima policy shifts toward alternative energy sources.

By 2020, the gap between nations had narrowed significantly, with Sweden maintaining its lead (55%), followed by Japan and Germany (both around 43-48%), and Brazil slightly behind at 42%. This convergence suggests a global trend toward renewable energy adoption, regardless of initial disparities.

## 📋 Task 2: Agree/Disagree Essay

**Topic:** "Some people believe that universities should focus on providing academic knowledge, while others argue that they should also teach practical skills for employment. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

---

### 📝 Model Essay (Band 7.5+)

In an increasingly competitive global job market, the debate over the primary purpose of higher education has intensified. While some contend that universities should exclusively focus on academic knowledge and theoretical learning, I strongly advocate for a balanced approach that integrates both academic rigor and practical skill development. This essay will argue that universities must evolve to meet contemporary demands without compromising their scholarly foundations.

Admittedly, academic knowledge forms the cornerstone of higher education and should not be marginalized. Universities are uniquely positioned to cultivate critical thinking, analytical abilities, and intellectual curiosity—attributes that transcend specific occupations and benefit students throughout their lives. For instance, a philosophy graduate may not directly apply abstract reasoning concepts in their career, yet the cognitive discipline and logical analysis skills acquired are invaluable in problem-solving across diverse contexts. Moreover, academic knowledge often provides the theoretical framework necessary for innovation and groundbreaking discoveries in fields ranging from medicine to engineering.

However, ignoring practical skills would be a disservice to students and society alike. Employers increasingly demand graduates who can immediately contribute to their organizations, possessing not only theoretical understanding but also applicable competencies. Digital literacy, project management, communication skills, and industry-specific technical abilities are no longer optional but essential for professional success. Furthermore, the rapid pace of technological change necessitates continuous learning, and universities have a responsibility to equip students with the adaptability and practical tools required for lifelong learning in dynamic industries.

The most effective solution, therefore, lies in harmonizing academic and practical education rather than treating them as mutually exclusive. This could involve incorporating internships, industry partnerships, and problem-based learning into traditional curricula. For example, business students could analyze case studies from real companies while mastering theoretical economic principles, thereby bridging the gap between theory and practice. Similarly, engineering programs could combine laboratory work with design projects that address genuine industry challenges, ensuring graduates possess both conceptual understanding and hands-on experience.

In conclusion, while academic knowledge remains indispensable for intellectual development, universities must also prioritize practical skill acquisition to ensure graduates' employability and relevance in the modern workforce. By embracing a holistic educational model that seamlessly blends theory with practice, higher education institutions can fulfill their dual mission of cultivating enlightened minds and preparing competent professionals for the challenges of the twenty-first century.

## 📚 Vocabulary Summary

### Academic Verbs
- **Illustrate, demonstrate, exhibit** - formal ways to say "show"
- **Ascend, surge, soar** - advanced ways to describe "increase"
- **Cultivate, foster, nurture** - sophisticated terms for "develop/grow"
- **Harmonize, synthesize, integrate** - elevated ways to say "combine"

### Band 7.5+ Adjectives
- **Substantial, considerable, significant** - stronger than "big"
- **Indispensable, paramount, critical** - more emphatic than "important"
- **Remarkable, exceptional, extraordinary** - vivid descriptions
- **Contemporary, current, present-day** - time-related precision

### Sentence Structures
1. **Participial phrases:** "Starting from the lowest baseline, Japan exhibited..."
2. **Concessive clauses:** "Admittedly, academic knowledge forms the cornerstone..."
3. **Appositives:** "Universities, the bastions of intellectual pursuit, must evolve..."
4. **Parallel structures:** "not only theoretical understanding but also applicable competencies"
5. **Causal chains:** "driven by policy shifts → toward alternative energy sources"

## ✍️ Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 (Data Description)
1. **Start with a clear overview** - highlight main trends before diving into details
2. **Use varied data vocabulary** - avoid repeating "increased," use "surged," "ascended," "rose"
3. **Be specific but hedge** - use "approximately," "roughly" to acknowledge estimation
4. **Group logically** - compare similar trends, highlight contrasts
5. **End with synthesis** - connect to broader implications if possible

### Task 2 (Essay Writing)
1. **State your position clearly** - don't sit on the fence; pick a stance
2. **Balance both sides** - acknowledge counterarguments before refuting them
3. **Use topic sentences** - each paragraph should start with its main idea
4. **Provide specific examples** - hypothetical scenarios work if real examples aren't known
5. **Vary sentence length** - mix short impact sentences with long complex ones
6. **Avoid absolute statements** - use hedging language like "tends to," "often," "generally"

### Grammar Checklist
- [ ] Mixed simple/compound/complex sentences
- [ ] Correct use of articles (a/an/the)
- [ ] Subject-verb agreement
- [ ] Correct tense usage (past for Task 1 data, present for Task 2 opinions)
- [ ] Relative clauses (who, which, that)
- [ ] Passive voice where appropriate (is used, was achieved)

### Vocabulary Strategies
1. **Learn collocations, not isolated words** - "dramatic increase" not "big increase"
2. **Use synonyms strategically** - vary vocabulary but don't force it
3. **Topic-specific terminology** - "renewable energy," "employability," "intellectual curiosity"
4. **Academic register** - avoid contractions (don't → do not), slang, overly casual language
