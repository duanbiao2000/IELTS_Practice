## Task 1: Line Graph

**Topic:** The line graph shows the percentage of households in different types of accommodation in a European city between 1990 and 2020.

### Essay (~180 words)

The line graph illustrates the changes in the proportion of households living in three different types of accommodation—private rented, social housing, and owner-occupied—in a European city over a 30-year period from 1990 to 2020.

Overall, the owner-occupied sector experienced a significant upward trend, while private rented accommodation also witnessed growth. In contrast, the percentage of households in social housing declined considerably throughout the period.

In 1990, social housing was the most common form of accommodation, accounting for approximately 45% of households. Owner-occupied homes represented about 30%, while private rented properties made up the remaining 25%. Over the next decade, social housing saw a steady decrease, falling to around 35% by 2000, whereas owner-occupied accommodation rose gradually to 38%.

Between 2000 and 2020, the trends became more pronounced. Owner-occupied housing surged dramatically, reaching approximately 65% by the end of the period. Private rented accommodation also increased, growing from 25% in 2000 to roughly 30% in 2020. Conversely, social housing continued its downward trajectory, plummeting to a mere 5% in 2020. This dramatic shift indicates a fundamental transformation in the city's housing landscape over three decades.

### Annotations & Analysis

**Paragraph 1 (Introduction):**
- **Vocabulary:** *illustrates* (synonym for shows), *proportion* (synonym for percentage), *witnessed* (more academic than saw), *experienced* (variety in reporting verbs)
- **Structure:** Opening sentence using paraphrase, overall summary paragraph highlighting main trends
- **Writing Technique:** Clear paraphrase of the question statement, overview placed in separate paragraph for clarity
- **Band 7.5+ Factor:** Demonstrates vocabulary range and ability to synthesize key trends

**Paragraph 2 (Body 1 - Initial period):**
- **Vocabulary:** *accounting for* (academic phrase), *approximately* (precise language), *whereas* (contrast conjunction), *steady* (descriptive adjective)
- **Structure:** Detailed comparison of data points, chronological ordering
- **Writing Technique:** Comparing multiple data points in single sentences for cohesion
- **Band 7.5+ Factor:** Accurate data reporting with appropriate grouping, varied sentence structures

**Paragraph 3 (Body 2 - Later period + Conclusion):**
- **Vocabulary:** *pronounced* (advanced adjective), *surged* (dynamic verb), *plummeting* (strong verb), *fundamental transformation* (sophisticated phrase), *trajectory* (academic noun)
- **Structure:** Continues chronological narrative, adds concluding insight
- **Writing Technique:** Combining data description with analytical insight
- **Band 7.5+ Factor:** Complex sentences, analytical vocabulary, clear conclusion linking to bigger picture

## Task 2: Agree/Disagree Essay

**Topic:** Some people argue that universities should prioritize academic knowledge over practical skills. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

### Essay (~300 words)

The debate over whether universities should focus primarily on academic knowledge or practical skills has become increasingly contentious in recent years. While I acknowledge that academic scholarship forms the foundation of higher education, I largely disagree with the notion that it should take precedence over practical training. In my view, universities must strike a balanced approach that integrates both elements to prepare students adequately for the modern workforce.

Those who advocate for academic supremacy argue that theoretical understanding is essential for developing critical thinking and intellectual depth. Academic disciplines provide students with a framework for analyzing complex problems and generating new knowledge, which cannot be acquired through mere vocational training. Moreover, theoretical knowledge tends to be more enduring and adaptable across different career paths, whereas practical skills may become obsolete as technology advances. For instance, the fundamental principles of economics remain relevant regardless of technological changes, whereas specific technical skills may require constant updating.

However, the exclusive emphasis on academic knowledge can leave graduates ill-equipped for the realities of the job market. Employers increasingly demand graduates who possess not only theoretical understanding but also practical competencies such as communication, teamwork, and problem-solving abilities. Furthermore, many contemporary professions—ranging from engineering to healthcare—require a seamless integration of theory and practice. A medical student who understands anatomy in theory but lacks clinical experience would be dangerously unprepared for real-world practice.

In conclusion, while academic knowledge undoubtedly remains a crucial component of university education, it should not be prioritized at the expense of practical skills. The most effective approach is a holistic curriculum that blends theoretical understanding with hands-on application, thereby producing graduates who are both intellectually rigorous and professionally competent.

---

### Annotations & Analysis

**Paragraph 1 (Introduction):**
- **Vocabulary:** *contentious* (advanced adjective), *acknowledge* (balanced verb), *precedence* (formal noun), *strike a balanced approach* (idiomatic expression), *integrates* (academic verb)
- **Structure:** Thesis statement with clear position, outlining essay structure
- **Writing Technique:** Acknowledging counter-argument before stating own position (sophisticated approach)
- **Band 7.5+ Factor:** Clear thesis, sophisticated vocabulary, balanced viewpoint

**Paragraph 2 (Body 1 - Counter-argument):**
- **Vocabulary:** *supremacy* (formal noun), *intellectual depth* (academic phrase), *framework* (technical term), *enduring* (descriptive adjective), *obsolete* (academic adjective)
- **Structure:** Topic sentence, supporting points with examples, logical progression
- **Writing Technique:** Using hypothetical examples to illustrate points
- **Band 7.5+ Factor:** Cohesive devices (moreover, for instance), academic register, complex sentences

**Paragraph 3 (Body 2 - Own argument):**
- **Vocabulary:** *exclusive emphasis* (formal phrase), *ill-equipped* (idiomatic expression), *competencies* (academic noun), *seamless integration* (sophisticated phrase), *holistic* (academic adjective)
- **Structure:** Direct argument against exclusive academic focus, real-world examples
- **Writing Technique:** Contrast with previous paragraph (However), concrete examples
- **Band 7.5+ Factor:** Varied vocabulary range, critical thinking, workplace relevance

**Paragraph 4 (Conclusion):**
- **Vocabulary:** *undoubtedly* (emphatic adverb), *at the expense of* (idiomatic phrase), *holistic* (repetition with variation), *intellectually rigorous* (sophisticated phrase)
- **Structure:** Restate position, summarize main points, final recommendation
- **Writing Technique:** Synthesizing both viewpoints into balanced conclusion
- **Band 7.5+ Factor:** Clear conclusion, no new information, academic tone maintained

## Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### Task 1 Tips
1. **Always include an overview** - This is crucial for achieving higher bands
2. **Group data logically** - Don't just list numbers; compare and contrast
3. **Use precise language** - Avoid "went up" when you can use "surged" or "increased"
4. **Vary your reporting verbs** - Illustrate, depict, demonstrate, show, reveal
5. **Include numbers accurately** - Approximately, around, nearly, precisely

### Task 2 Tips
1. **Clear thesis statement** - Your position should be obvious in the introduction
2. **Balanced paragraphs** - Each paragraph should have one clear main idea
3. **Use examples** - Specific examples strengthen your arguments
4. **Avoid repetition** - Use synonyms and paraphrasing
5. **Academic register** - Maintain formal tone throughout
6. **Linking words** - Furthermore, however, in contrast, consequently

### General Tips
- **Plan before writing** - 2-3 minutes planning saves time later
- **Watch word count** - Task 1: 180-220, Task 2: 280-320
- **Proofread** - Leave 2-3 minutes to check for errors
- **Practice regularly** - Consistency is key to improvement
