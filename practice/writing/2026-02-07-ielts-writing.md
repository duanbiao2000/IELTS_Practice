## Writing Tips for Band 7.5+

### ✅ Task 1 Key Strategies

1. **Overview First**: Always include a clear overview statement in the introduction or first paragraph. Identify major trends, comparisons, or exceptions immediately.

2. **Vary Your Verbs**: Instead of repeating "shows" or "is," use alternatives:
   - illustrates, demonstrates, depicts, reveals, highlights, presents, indicates

3. **Precise Data Reporting**: Don't just list numbers. Use precise language:
   - "approximately 3.5 cups" (not "about 3.5")
   - "exhibited a marginal increase" (not "went up a little")

4. **Group and Compare**: Organize data logically:
   - Highest to lowest / lowest to highest
   - By similarities and differences
   - By categories or groups

5. **Synthesize, Don't List**: Look for patterns, not individual data points:
   - "The Nordic countries showed consistently higher consumption"
   - "Japan represented an outlier with significantly lower figures"

### ✅ Task 2 Key Strategies

1. **Clear Position in Introduction**: State your approach clearly:
   - "This essay will examine both advantages and disadvantages"
   - "This discussion will analyze the pros and cons"

2. **Topic Sentences**: Begin each paragraph with a clear topic sentence that states the main idea.

3. **Support with Evidence**: Use hypothetical research or general knowledge:
   - "Research demonstrates that..."
   - "Studies indicate..."
   - "A survey revealed..."

4. **Balance the Discussion**: Give roughly equal attention to both sides (unless instructed otherwise):
   - Two paragraphs for advantages, two for disadvantages
   - Or three paragraphs total: intro, body (both sides), conclusion

5. **Avoid Simple Sentences**: Mix sentence types:
   - **Simple:** "Remote work saves time."
   - **Compound:** "Remote work saves time, and it reduces costs."
   - **Complex:** "While remote work saves time, it may cause isolation."
   - **Complex-Compound:** "While remote work saves time, and it reduces costs, it may still cause isolation for some employees."

### ✅ Vocabulary Enhancement

1. **Upgrade Basic Words**:
   - "good" → beneficial, advantageous, favorable
   - "bad" → detrimental, harmful, adverse
   - "big" → substantial, considerable, significant
   - "show" → illustrate, demonstrate, reveal

2. **Use Collocations**:
   - "work-life balance" (not "balance between work and life")
   - "commuting time" (not "time to travel to work")
   - "job satisfaction" (not "happy about work")
   - "professional isolation" (not "being alone at work")

3. **Academic Prefixes and Suffixes**:
   - multi-faceted, inter-connected, co-workers
   - collaboration, productivity, flexibility
   - consideration, elimination, acceleration

### ✅ Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Task 1**:
   - ❌ Giving your opinion
   - ❌ Explaining why the data exists
   - ❌ Copying words from the question (paraphrase instead!)
   - ❌ Writing too much detail without overview

2. **Task 2**:
   - ❌ One-sided arguments (unless question asks for your opinion only)
   - ❌ Too many examples without explanation
   - ❌ Vague statements without support
   - ❌ Simple vocabulary throughout
