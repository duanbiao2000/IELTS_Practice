## 📚 Today's Key Learnings

### Sentence Structures
1. **Contrast:** While/Although + [clause], + [main clause]
   - *Example:* While the study initially focused on urban areas, it eventually expanded to rural regions.

2. **Comparison:** Both X and Y + [verb]
   - *Example:* Both qualitative and quantitative methods were employed in this research.

3. **Cause-Effect:** X results in/contributes to/leads to Y
   - *Example:* The new policy has led to a significant reduction in emissions.

### Collocations (Reading Focus)
- *widely accepted* (opinion)
- *significant impact* (result)
- *in contrast to* (comparison)
- *closely related* (relationship)
- *substantial evidence* (support)

### Insights from Common Reading Topics
**Academic Research Articles:**
- Look for *methodology* and *results* sections
- Pay attention to *limitation* paragraphs
- *Further research* often appears in conclusion

**Historical/Social Texts:**
- Track *chronology* with dates and time markers
- Note *contrasting viewpoints* between different groups
- *Cultural context* explains behavior differences
